Law and/or Accounting Firm Partner Income

Restrictions Verification % Used for Servicing
Foreign partner income received from major accounting or law firms.

Partner agreement stating fixed payment arrangements and 3 months credits to an account.

Note:

  • The letter from the employer or payslip must include current base salary/wage information.
  • In addition to the above, where the customer resides in a different country to the account where their foreign currency income is being paid into, also obtain the most recent three months bank statement/s from the country of residence to confirm the source of funds used to cover their living expenses. Review the statement/s to confirm the source of funds used to cover the customer’s living expenses is from their foreign currency income. Ensure the account conduct is satisfactory and there are no undisclosed commitments during the review period.
  • If the bank statements does not show transfers from the customer’s overseas account or the main source of funds use to cover their living expenses is not from their foreign currency income, leave comments with an explanation for Credit Decisioning to consider.


Bank statements must confirm all of the following:

  • Amount
  • Account name
  • Account number.
  • Description of the credit, e.g. salary, wage, pay or employer's name
  • Date range
  • Financial institution details.


Note:

  • Bank statements must be from the financial institution where the salary is credited and not a third party source.
  • An old bank statement can be provided to verify ownership of account.

80% used for both Gold and Silver currencies

70% used for all other currencies

 

Calculations

Step Action
1 Deduct the applicable percentage from the gross income as stated in Partner agreement.
2 Convert the remaining foreign currency income to Australian dollars using the XE Currency Converter.
Note: The XE Currency Converter must be opened in Internet Explorer.
3 Print a copy of the foreign currency calculation showing the foreign currency amount, exchange rate and date. This must be submitted with your supporting documents
4 Input the calculated amount into the ‘Gross Salary’ field of the application

 

Other/Special Notes

  • The currency stated on the partner agreement will determine the currency that is used for converting to Australian dollars. Where the currency on the partner agreement is different from the payslip and/or salary credits ensure the converted amount from the payslip is in line with the amount stated on the employment contract.
  • See Foreign Currency Income for translation requirements if original document is in a foreign language.
