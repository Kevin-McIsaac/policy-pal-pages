1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy

 
This policy outlines the parameters for assessing loan applications that involve migrant borrower(s) in a consumer mortgage application.
  • A migrant is defined as an applicant that has been granted with one of the Australian government temporary or permanent residence visa classifications for entry to Australia.
  • A permanent residence visa allows a person unrestricted working rights in Australia and they can remain in Australia indefinitely as opposed to a temporary residence visa which's a person to live and work in Australia for a limited time only.
  • If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, refer to Lenders Mortgage Insurance where required. 
  

2. Rules you must apply


2.1 Establishing citizenship and residency status

A customer’s citizenship, residency or non-residency status must be established at initial application stage for all consumer mortgage loans.
For applicants who are:
  • Australian citizens who reside in Australia, or out of Australia on either a temporary or permanent basis (i.e. Australian expat), refer to Eligibility policy > Australian Citizen residency status
  • New Zealand citizens, or they hold a permanent or temporary residency visa issued by the Australian government, refer to Migrant Lending
Where the above scenarios do not apply to the applicant, refer Non-Residents lending   , this will include applicants who:
  • are not an Australian or New Zealand citizen, or
  • do not hold either a permanent or temporary residency visa issued by the Australian government, or
  • hold a temporary visa issued b the Australian government, but reside out of Australia
Note: Lending to a non-resident applicant(s) is not permitted
 

2.2 Migrant lending

The purpose of the migrant lending package is to provide the opportunity for eligible migrants to obtain mortgage finance on their arrival in Australia.
Eligible applicants will be able to source mortgage loan products up to a maximum lending value ratio of 95% including LMI capitalisation if the visa type is permanent or 90% including LMI capitalisation if the visa type is temporary.
Refer to Assessing Loan to Value (LVR), Visa matrix and Applicant via Resident Type matrix for further information.
 

2.3 Eligibility criteria

Migrant lending policy is available to applicants meeting the criteria below:
  • The applicant(s) visa type must be listed as an approved migrant visa type within the Visa matrix and Applicant via Resident Type matrix
  • A copy of the migrant’s passport and visa must be obtained and held on file and the visa number added to comments – refer to Migrant lending package application process and Evidencing migrant visa for further details. Where the applicant:
    • Does not hold a passport, e.g. a refugee may only hold a Protection visa (subclass 866), the application may be referred to Credit for further consideration.
    • Has an expired passport but holds a permanent residence visa and resides in Australia - A copy of the Expired Passport, supported by a copy of the current visa is acceptable.
  • Certain migrant visa holders may not have access to all products and reference must be made to the Visa matrix and Applicant via Resident Type matrix prior to providing a product.
  • Applicants who are permanent residents living overseas purchasing an investment property must have a minimum of 12 months remaining on their Australian Visa (unless the visa does not have an expiry date).
  • Holders of a temporary visa must have a minimum of 12 months remaining on their Australian Visa. Additionally:
    • In a non-mortgage insured loan, a visa with no expiry date is considered to have met the minimum 12 month rule.
    • In a mortgage insured loan, all applications are to be referred to Credit for review prior to referral to the Mortgage Insurer. The Mortgage Insurer may consider any referral on a case-by-case basis, outside of the above, with supporting commentary (i.e. visa does not have an expiry date for visa 309 and 820 with confirmation of current status)
  • Normal credit underwriting standards including serviceability and verification rules apply
  • Where it is identified the Temporary residency visa holder earns foreign income, the applicant is not eligible and the application is unable to proceed.
   

2.4 Income and employment verification

  • If any foreign income is declared and required for servicing refer to Visa matrix, Applicant via Resident Type matrix and Applications with multiple applicants and varying visa types, citizenship or residency status .
  • Applicant’s employment details are to be verified using standard income verification policy in terms of rules outlined in Verifying Income .
 

2.5 Own contribution validation

Certain migrant visa holders as listed in the Visa matrix and Applicant via Resident Type matrix must provide bank statement evidence of their own contribution covering deposit, stamp duty, legal costs etc.
Bank statements must be in applicant name. Bank statements are to be held on file with other loan application documentation.
Refer to Document translation where the bank statements are in a language other than English or a currency other than Australian dollars.

Note: Where the account statement(s) does not evidence the full funds to complete / own contribution, it is acceptable to proceed, where a satisfactory explanation is entered in application supporting notes, e.g. the applicant has paid a deposit on the property being purchased and where combined with funds evidenced via account statements, confirms funds to complete / own contribution.

 

2.6 Document translation

In the event foreign income and other supporting documents are not in English  or display a currency other than AUD, refer to Document Standards policy > When supporting documents are not in English or display a currency other than AUD.
The original documents, translated documents and Foreign currency translation checklist  are to be retained in the loan file.

Link to  Translation Checklist P/C 2466  

  

2.7 Applications with multiple applicants and varying visa types, citizenship or residency status

Where there are joint/multiple applicants, all applicants must be eligible as per policy requirements, regardless of their contribution to the loan. I.e. if the applicant type is not acceptable to the bank then the individual is unable to be a borrower, guarantor or owner of the security property.
If the application involves a migrant, check the visa type. Brokers are to confirm if the:
  • Visa type s  permanent or temporary
  • Applicant’s employment status and income are compliant with the visa matrix
  • Visa is acceptable to the Mortgage Insurers, where the application is mortgage insured
  • The LVR may be restricted based on the visa type of the co-applicant.
  • The maximum LVR for a temporary visa holder is 90% inclusive of any capped mortgage insurance premium providing the visa is on both the Bank’s and the Mortgage insurers approved visa list.
  • Where one of the applicants holds a temporary or permanent visa and where the visa type is not on the Bank’s approved list, the application is to be declined.
  • Where one of the applicants holds a temporary or permanent visa and where the visa type is not acceptable to the mortgage insurer, then no MI is available.
  • Loan applications structured with multiple borrowers on the loan but only a single title holder are not permitted where one or more of the applicants is currently, or will continue to reside overseas post settlement. No financial benefit can be demonstrated where the sole or primary income earner will continue to reside overseas post settlement, but have no ownership of the security property.
  • Refer to Visa matrix and Applicant via Resident Type matrix for restrictions affecting each applicant type and to identify if mortgage insurance is available.
 

2.8 Approval from foreign investment review board (FIRB)

Temporary visa applicants require approval from the Federal Government Treasury – Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB). It is the responsibility of the purchaser to ensure approval is held. Employees are to advise temporary visa purchasers to make enquiries of the FIRB, refer www.firb.gov.au .

For all acceptable loan purposes and where the borrower is, or will be on the title, we must hold a copy of the FIRB approval prior to issuing a formal approval. The only allowable exception to the FIRB requirements is where the applicant is purchasing a property as Joint Tenants* (50/50), with an Australian or New Zealand citizen or a Permanent Resident who is their spouse.
*The exception does not apply where the property ownership is Tenants in Common (e.g. 80/20).

In all cases (purchase and refinance) the Broker Processing Unit (BPU) for all broker applications are to hold a copy of the FIRB approval and validate the loan application complies with any requirements listed on the FIRB approval. The check of the FIRB approval to loan application is to include but not limited to:
  • Applicants name
  • Property being purchased
  • Product selection (owner occupied vs investment products permitted)
  • Purpose (e.g. used as principle place of residence confirmed with post settlement postcode in the application being in line with the FIRB approval)
  • Rental income (e.g. if renting the property is not permitted, rental income on the property is not included in the origination system).
Refer to Visa matrix and Applicant via Resident Type matrix for further details.

Where the applicant lives and works overseas and FIRB indicates approval for principal place of residence # , then:
1. The property is to be their principal place of residence while in Australia.
2. The property cannot be used for investment (rental) or holiday purposes.
# Note: No rental income against this property acceptable

 

2.9 Visa matrix and Applicant via Resident Type Matrix Overview

Applications may be accepted for migrant lending with any one of the acceptable visas listed in the Visa matrix and Applicant via resident type Matrix .

The Acceptable Visa matrix details the:
  • Visa subclass number
  • Category of the visa type e.g. Permanent or temporary
  • If the visa is acceptable to Westpac
  • If the visa type is acceptable to mortgage insurers
  • Acceptable employment status
  • Acceptable income type.
Also refer Unacceptable Visas
The Applicant via resident type matrix   details:
  • How to load residency
  • Additional policy rules and conditions

Migrant lending customers will only be acceptable if all conditions set out in the Visa matrix and Applicant via resident type matrix (and any additional conditions listed in policy) is met.
Example:
  • Business visa types should reflect income status and documentation from within Australia.
  • Sponsored visa types should reflect income from that region, i.e. state or territory sponsored visa's should reflect income earned within Australia.
If a visa number is not currently on the acceptable list or the visa is no longer available, or you have been made aware of a new visa (or subclass) then – these can be referred to Credit for consideration and must be supported with detailed comments/notes and justification for acceptance.
Note:  for Applications that require Mortgage Insurance - The Mortgage Insurer may consider any referral on a case-by-case basis with supporting commentary


Broker Processing Unit (BPU) will enforce the Visa Matrix requirements.

3. Process to follow

3.1 Migrant lending package application process Follow the steps below to for migrant application process - all applications.

Step  
1 Process application as per normal and make a copy of customer's visa.
Note: All other lending criteria and process remain unchanged, such as the customer must hold a valid IDV number, income documents and POS conditions.
Note: All migrant temporary visa borrowers must provide a copy of their Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB) approval (where applicable) refer Approval from FIRB, which must be complied with.
2 In the Comments subject header input 'MBA'.
3 In the Comments field indicate Visa subclass and country of origin.
4 In Comments field, add any supporting information to support application.
5 Run credit decision.

3.2 Evidencing migrant visa

  • Migrant status must be evidenced via provision of a copy of a work visa approval issued by Department of Home Affairs - Immigration and citizenship or a printout supplied by the customer from the VEVO web page https://online.immi.gov.au/evo/firstParty?actionType=query
  • Where an applicant’s migrant category is Investor Visa type, evidence is to be provided of approved deposit funds held with State Treasury.
  • The Broker Processing Unit (BPU) for all broker applications are to hold a copy of the visa (or print out from the VEVO web page) and validate the applicant has been correctly entered in the applicable loan processing system.
