1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy?

The policy outlined below sets out the parameters for accepting Home Loan, Investment Property Loan and/or Home Equity Loan consumer mortgage proposals originated in the Westpac brand for certain qualifying professionals.
The Bank may consider waiver of Lenders’ Mortgage Insurance (LMI) requirement for certain eligible borrowers when the application satisfies the requirement as stipulated in Sports and Entertainment sector.

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Sports and Entertainment Sector

Applicable to Broker and accredited Sports and Entertainment Bankers where eligible Professional Sports and Entertainment persons can obtain loans to a maximum LVR of 90% without mortgage insurance subject to certain criteria. . 

The policy outlined below sets out the parameters for accepting Home Loan, Investment Property Loan and/or Home Equity Loan consumer mortgage proposals originated in the Westpac brand for certain qualifying Professional Sports and Entertainment persons. There is no authority to extend to other product types.
These requirements are to be read in conjunction with the policies and procedures contained in other sections of the Consumer Credit Policy Manual and Consumer Credit Procedures Manual.

All prevailing standard non-mortgage insured policies and product offerings* for fully documented loans apply with the only concession being to genuine savings requirements and LVR thresholds offered under this arrangement.
* Including Interest Only repayment terms up to:
  • 10 years for investment property loans
  • 5 years for home loans.
 

2.1.1 Eligible criteria

The target clients must meet the Westpac Premium or Private Bank criteria or have the potential to meet the criteria within the near future. Given the levels of criteria set from the outset, the Sector Policy will be most relevant to talent in their prime or high potential talent who have been identified as the stars of the future.
Note: To be classified as a Professional Sport and Entertainment Person, the talent must have an accredited Manager, Agent or Accountant.

For the purpose of this policy, eligible sports persons include the following:
  • Professional athlete (all sports);
  • Professional coach (all sports), retired athlete still working within the sports or entertainment industry

2.1.2 Special conditions

  • Loans with TAE (Total Approved Exposure) greater than $750,000 to will require appropriate PAII (or income protection insurance) to be evidenced for a MI waiver to be applied, when the applicant is not a professional athlete for:
    • AFL and AFLW (for player contracts signed before 31/12/2027)
    • NRL and NRLW (for player contracts signed before 31/10/2027)
  • Professional Sport and Entertainment persons will not be considered as contractors or independent operators for the purposes of financial records
  • Professional Sport and Entertainment person’s contracts are to be considered as the equivalent of fulltime employment contracts
  • Properties may either be principal places of residence or investment properties
  • Sports and Entertainment Sector Policy may only be provided by Brokers and accredited Sports and Entertainment Bankers and is not to be offered by Bankers that fall outside of the Westpac Sports & Entertainment segment
  • To be classified as a Professional Sport and Entertainment person, the talent must have an accredited Manager, Agent or Accountant. The responsibility for confirmation of this rests with the originating accredited Sports and Entertainment Banker or Broker.
* TAE – is used to define ‘total assessed exposure’ with the Westpac Group and is not the limit for a single loan unless the customer does not have any existing lending with the Group. Hence, the total sum of loans for any individual customer must not exceed $2.0m under this Sector Policy

Other requirements
All Owner Builder proposals excluded.

Applications assessed >90% LVR will be subject to Lender’s Mortgage Insurance (LMI) (and their acceptance to provide cover). Standard Mortgage Insurance policy(s) and guidelines apply.

 

2.1.3 Loan applications

Applications may be assessed for applicants who meet the definition of a Professional Sports and Entertainment persons, as outlined in "Eligibility: Sports and Entertainment Sector", whereby the following allowable variances to standard policy may be applied:
  • A maximum LVR of 90% may be approved without the need to obtain mortgage insurance, up to a maximum TAE of $2.0mil; and
  • Confirmation and validation of 5% genuine savings is not required for applications whereby mortgage insurance is not required (as per above).
This policy is to be used as a guide. Approval is not guaranteed in line with the maximum offerings available under this policy.

This policy is available for applications submitted for credit in the applicant’s own name, or related company or trust entities provided direct ownership and/or directorship by the applicant is evident.
Lending or property ownership may be in the name of the spouse or related entity, provided the applicant is involved in the transaction, at least as a guarantor.
