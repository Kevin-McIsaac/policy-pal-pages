1. Standards required

1.1 Market value

1.1.1 Real estate security

In situations where more than 1 market value is available for the same property, use the lower of the values (e.g. lower of contract price or valuation amount).

Definition

Market value for real estate is defined as:
‘The estimated amount which a seller would expect to receive from an informed purchaser for the property in the current resale market with normal marketing support through an Australian based real estate agency.
The market value is determined on a vacant possession basis and is to be exclusive of purchase incentives, non-fixed chattels, legal / stamp duty costs and rental guarantees.’
A market value assessed on this basis will not reflect the mortgagee in possession or other forced sale circumstances.

Exception - Defence Housing Authority properties

Where the security property is subject to a Defence Housing Authority (DHA) sale and leaseback arrangement, the panel valuer's valuation report will contain 2 property values. The lower figure represents the vacant possession value. The higher figure is the vacant possession value plus the worth of the government rental guarantee. Where the loan is subject to mortgage insurance or the LVR is > 80% and GOE returns a valuation required message, then the higher figure is acceptable if:
  • The rental guarantee assessment is not greater than 110% of the vacant possession assessment
  • The loan is amortising
  • The lease has 6 or more years to run excluding options to renew.
Where the loan is not subject to mortgage insurance or the LVR <= 80%, then the higher valuation figure is acceptable.

Assessment method

The market value can be determined by a number of methods depending on the stated purchase price, proposed total approved exposure and / or location.

1.3 Existing mortgage security

Existing residential mortgages held by Westpac can generally be used for increases or additional loans. A new mortgage will be required if:
  • The existing mortgage is not first party (i.e. the mortgagor/s and the debtor/s are not exactly the same, or the additional / new / increased loan has a different mortgagor/s or debtor/s)
  • There is a guarantor or third party involvement
  • The existing mortgage documents secure any business loan or liability
  • The customer is a company.
Note:
  • Further lending against existing third party mortgage security is not permitted.
  • Further lending against Asian loans established in overseas Westpac offices is not permitted. These loans would need to be fully repaid as part of any new origination.

Some older style mortgages can not be used for new borrowings. The Mortgage Centre / loan centre will prepare new mortgages if they are required.

 

1.4 Power of Attorney / Administration Order

A Power of Attorney (POA) or Administration Order is a formal instrument by which one person empowers another person to act for him / her for certain specific purposes.
 

1.4.1 Valid uses of a Power of Attorney / Administration Order

Where a Power of Attorney (POA) or Administration Order is in place, the Attorney/Administrator must act in the best interests of the donor (person giving the Power of Attorney) and not in the best interests of themselves.
Westpac's obligations under The Privacy Act, advises that a POA can not sign a credit application on behalf of a customer / donor where the applicant has capacity.
Brokers must also refer to > 03.09 Eligibility policy > Customers Experiencing Vulnerability

1.5 Undischarged mortgage

Westpac will not retain mortgages after loans have been repaid.
