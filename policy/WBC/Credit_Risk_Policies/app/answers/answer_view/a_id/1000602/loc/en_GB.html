1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy?

This policy will help you determine the maximum loan term when assessing a residential loan application.
Generally, the maximum loan term is determined on the applicant's ability to repay without their incurring substantial hardship.
Refer to individual Product Policies for any additional loan term restrictions or requirements that may apply to specific products.
If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, refer to Lenders Mortgage Insurance where required.
 

2. Rules you must apply
  

2.1 Assessing maximum loan term

The standard maximum term for an amortising loan (i.e. principal, interest and fee (PIF) repayment) is 30 years, however this will depend on the applicant’s age. For details regarding maximum Interest Only terms refer to the table Assessing maximum loan terms for interest only (IO) products.
When assessing loan applications and structuring the loan term, specific consideration should be given to the applicant’s remaining time in the workforce. The applicant’s ability to retire or service the new debt post retirement age must be considered, without their incurring substantial hardship.
The ability to meet repayment obligations and clear the debt may be from:
  • Income (including retirement income), or
  • Asset equity (including the sale of an asset)
 

2.2 Exit Strategy – Responsible lending

During the Requirement and Objective (R&O) conversation, where any borrower is 45 years or older at the time of application or will reach 75 years during the term of the credit contract, the mortgage broker  must:
  • Make further enquiries to find out:
    • When the applicant intends to retire (retirement age); and
    • Where retirement is likely to be prior to the completion of the requested loan term (ie, their intended retirement age falls during the loan term or they reach 75 of age during the loan term), what is their strategy for servicing and / or repayment of the debt following retirement.
If customer is already retired at the time they take the loan out and serviceability is calculated on their retirement income, we do not need to capture retirement strategy. Comments and the answer will be N/A.

Note: The borrower may use a combination of more than one strategy.
  • The maximum loan term cannot be extended past the borrower’s retirement age unless an appropriate exit strategy is established. 
  • Follow Credit Processes for Record Keeping Requirement under Responsible Lending .
  • Complete verification activities required under Verifying sources of exit strategy
Refer to Guidelines for Examples of Acceptable and Non-compliant Responses to Exit Strategy questions for further details. 

2.2.1 Exit strategy exclusions

  • Bridging loans with nil end debt do not require an exit strategy as serviceability is not required to be demonstrated.
  • Guarantors do not require an exit strategy as the Responsible Lending obligation is to capture the borrower’s exit strategy, not the guarantors.
 

2.3 Verifying sources of exit strategy

  • Verification of the exit strategy is to be completed when the proposed strategy is through:
    • Non-real estate asset, and/or
    • Real estate asset
  • When the borrower is relying only on the above strategies (individually or in combination), the associated asset values must be greater than or equal to the amortised loan amount for the loan being requested at the intended retirement age.
  • Where the borrower is proposing to replace a real estate asset with one of a lower security value (i.e. downsizing of property), the equity position will need to consider both the repayment of the debt and purchase of new real estate.

2.3.1 Document standards

The documents used for verification must:
  • comply with specific rules set out in this policy
  • comply with Document Standards
  • comply with the Minimum Document Standards Matrix

2.3.2 Document retention

All supporting documents are to be forwarded with the loan application documents to the processing team.

2.4 Referring to Credit

A small number of applications will require review by Credit to ensure that the loan term and source of clearance is reasonable.

Specifically, Credit referral is required where:
  • Lenders Mortgage INsurance is required, AND
  • Any applicant is aged 55 years or over at the time of the application, AND
  • The applicant will be aged 75 years or over at the completion of the loan term, AND
  • The application contains a single owner occupied security, AND
  • The Net Equity value is insufficient to clear the requested loan amount and any existing liabilities not clearing against the owner occupied security (see calculation below)
Submissions to Credit must include details of how the repayments are to be met or the debt is to be cleared once the applicant reaches retirement age, or the age of 75. Examples could include Superannuation, investment income, or sale of assets.
Credit may not approve the application if it cannot be clearly demonstrated that repayments will be met or debt cleared at retirement, or the age of 75.
  

2.5 Assessing maximum loan term for interest only (IO) products

 
Category Policy rule
Interest only (IO) repayment term
i.e. loan that offers IO repayment term in the beginning and thereafter principle and interest repayment for the rest of the loan term.
  • The maximum interest only repayment period is determined by the purpose of the loan as follows:
    1. Owner occupied – the interest only repayment period may be for a minimum of 1 year up to a maximum of 5 years.
    2. Investment – the interest only repayment period may be for a minimum of 1 year up to a maximum of 10 years.
  • The contract loan term will be the total of the interest only period plus the PIF period – the maximum total term is 30 years.
These loans are to have a minimum loan term of the requested interest only repayment period and at least a 1-year principle and interest term.
For example: if a customer has requested a 5 year interest only loan, then the minimum total loan term must be for at least 6 years (5 year interest only then 1 year principle and interest repayment). The overall term (total loan term) must be greater than the interest only term.
  • Interest only term can be a combination of loan terms i.e. 2 years then another 3 years.
  • After the maximum interest only term expires, i.e. 5 years for Owner Occupied and 10 years for Investment loan, the loan must be converted to PIF repayments.
  • For an existing owner occupied or investment loan, any requests for extension beyond the maximum interest only repayment period will require the loan to be re-originated. This ensures that our current policy and product requirements, and maximum risk appetite for interest only repayment periods, are complied with.
Bridging loan The maximum term for a bridging loan is 12 months.
 
 

3. Process to follow
 

3.1 Record Keeping Requirement under Responsible Lending

In all cases the outcomes of the Retirement Strategy – Responsible Lending questions must be documented. Where a combination of strategies will be used, the details of each strategy must be documented.
Brokers will use Applyonline.
Refer to Guidelines for Examples of Acceptable and Non-compliant Responses to Exit Strategy questions .
 

3.2 Exit strategy-Non-real estate and real estate assets

  In the origination system, record the value of the asset/s used in the exit strategy:
  1. for non-real estate assets enter the ‘account balance’ – refer to 2.3.3 Verifying non-real estate assets
  2. for real estate assets enter the ‘property value’ – refer to 2.3.4 Verifying real-estate assets
Note: If the customer declared value (from the loan application form) does not match the information in the supporting documents, adjust the value of the assets in the loan assessment
 

3.2.1 Shareholding calculation  

  1. Access the asx website: https://www.asx.com.au/ , from the share holding certificate identify the company ASX code.
  2. Enter the code into the look up field
  3. From the drop down list select the correct issuer, hit enter.
  4. Identify the “last” value
  5. Retain a copy of the ASX search result
  6. Multiply the number of shares held x last value
  7. Record the result of the calculation as the ‘account balance’

4. Guidelines

4.1 Examples of acceptable and non-compliant responses to exit strategy questions
 

Category Guidelines
Examples of appropriate comments (this is not an exhaustive list)
  1. ‘The customer plans to pay off the loan before their retirement age and currently has enough surplus income to do this.’
  2. ‘The customer has or will have income from other investments that can be used to service the debt following retirement. Details of the other investments are […].’
  3. ‘The customer has and will continue to have income from superannuation that can be used to service the debt following retirement. Their current superannuation balance is $[...].’
    As long as the total in superannuation supports the loan and leaves enough for the customer to live off, this is acceptable.
  4. ‘The customer currently has recurring rental income of $[…] per week that I have verified; this can be used to service the debt following retirement.’
  5. ‘The customer has other assets that they can sell to pay the remainder of the loan amount, without the risk of any financial hardship. Details of the other assets are […].’
  6. ‘The customer is an SMSF individual trustee who purchased the property as a superannuation investment. They advised that the property will be sold when contributions can no longer be made.’
  7. The customer is proposing to replace a real estate asset with one of a lower security value (downsizing of property). The owner occupied property is [….]’
Examples of responses which are not compliant (this is not an exhaustive list)
  1. ‘The customer plans to retire during the loan term but that in all likelihood they will probably get an inheritance before they retire, so they should be able to repay the loan.’
  2. ‘Customer plans to win the lottery!’
  3. ‘Customer says: “If something bad happens to me financially, my insurance should cover everything.’
  4. ‘Exit strategy is evident based on customer’s financial position and accounts held with us.’
