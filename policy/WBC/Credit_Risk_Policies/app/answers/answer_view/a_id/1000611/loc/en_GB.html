1. Description

The pipeline authority determines when an application may be considered for approval under policy no longer current when the application fails the new policy.
 

2. Standards required
  

2.1 Standard pipeline definition-for all channels

A pipeline application is defined as a Generic Origination Engine (GOE) or Loan Increase Application (LIA) application where an application fails new policy and:
  • A formal credit decision (including Approval In Principle) has been run prior to a policy change date.
  • An application has been received via ApplyOnline prior to a policy change date with a credit decision run on or after the policy change date.
  • The credit decision prior to the policy change date is not more than 90 days old.
 

 

2.2 Material changes

Pipeline applications (including Approval In Principle) must be reassessed using the new policy if any of the following changes are made:
Change Example
Applicants – deletion or addition Eg Additional borrower is added to application.
Eg Applicant becomes a guarantor or guarantor becomes an applicant.
Assets decrease Eg Title search indicates an asset is not owned by the borrower and is deleted.
Credit bureau. A new decline or refer decision is received related to bureau default or bankruptcy Eg Initial application shows no defaults At a decision run after the policy change applicant one now has a Telstra default for $120. In this case there is a new decline due to a bureau default and the loan application can not be honoured under pipeline.
Employment mode Eg. Applicant ones employment is changed from full time to casual (even if on the same income) would mean the loan application can not be honoured under pipeline.
Income reductions Eg Applicant two has allowable social security income removed as dependants are aged 15years old and social security income is not ongoing for at least 5years would mean the loan application can not be honoured under pipeline.
Increase in expenses, outgoings or existing loan repayments Eg Applicant one and two have increased rental outgo, this is an increase in expenses and the loan application can not be honoured under pipeline.
Eg, Applicant two has an existing Westpac loan ie not the new loan being processed.  The existing loan repayments are increased and the loan application can not be honoured under pipeline.
Increase in loan amount by >10% of the original loan amount for Non-Mortgage Insured applications Eg The initial application was an AIP for a loan of $400k and purchase of $500k. The applicants have now found a property costing more at $550k and require a loan for $440k. This is acceptable to honour as pipeline as the loan increase is <=10% of the original loan requested.
Note: The increased loan amount must have passed serviceability prior to the policy change. ie Provided income has not changed and the application fails serviceability under the new policy because of the increased loan amount only – then it is acceptable to approve and move on as pipeline.
Increase in loan amount for Mortgage Insured applications
Note: Acceptable under pipeline where there is a decrease in base loan amount for a mortgage insured application, provided there are no other changes which trigger re-assessment		 Any loan amount increase in a Mortgage Insured applicant cannot be progressed under pipeline.
Increase in liabilities (non-Westpac and or Westpac) Eg Applicant three has a new Visa card added to the initial loan application and the loan application can not be honoured under pipeline.
Increase in LVR Eg Application is lodged for a refinance of an existing debt. The customer estimates that the property is valued at $500k and based on the estimate our LVR is 80%.
When the valuation is completed the customer estimate was high and the actual value of the property came in at $470k. Our LVR is now 85%. As there has been an increase in LVR we are not able to honour under pipeline. Ie Mortgage Insurance cover under previous policy is not available.
Securities – deletion or addition
Note - the deletion or addition of security does not apply to AIP applications where the security was not previously identified. Where security was not previously identified the new security must meet all policy requirements to be acceptable as pipeline. Additionally the AIP to full approval must not contain any other unacceptable changes noted in section 1.		 Eg The initial application was an AIP for a loan of $400k and purchase of $500k but the actual security address was not identified. The applicants have now found a property costing slightly more at $550k and require a loan for $440k. The new security is not on the unacceptable Security Development Register list or in any other way not an acceptable residential security. This is acceptable to honour as pipeline given:
  • The loan increase is <=10% of the original loan requested. Refer the item above under " Increase in loan amount by >10% of the original loan amount".
  • The addition of an acceptable residential security property after initial AIP is acceptable.
Note: The increased loan amount must have passed serviceability prior to the policy change. ie Provided income has not changed and the application fails serviceability under the new policy because of the increased loan amount only – then it is acceptable to approve and move on as pipeline.
 
If one of the above changes are made the new policy and GOE/LIA outcomes must be adhered to.
 

2.3 Progressing a pipeline application

 Pipeline applications may be progressed based on past policy and approval where the credit decision has been re-run as a result of a change to the application and the change is not listed as a ‘Material Change’ above.
 

 3. Additional information

There can be special pipeline conditions relating to a specific policy change, where standard pipeline policy will not apply. This will be communicated accordingly.
