1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy

This policy will help you determine when it is appropriate to provide a personal guarantee for mortgage applications.
This policy applies to loan applications that are wholly or partially secured by a personal guarantee.
 

1.2 What is a guarantee?

A guarantee is a contractual promise given by a third party, i.e. the guarantor, who agrees to have a liability to pay for the loan if the customers are unable or unwilling to repay the loan.
The guarantor needs to seek independent legal advice to understand the implication of becoming a guarantor and must be interviewed separately in the absence of the borrower.
There are two types of guarantees which can be given by an individual guarantor:
  • Security (supported) guarantee: The guarantor’s security is used to support the loan application, i.e. the applicant should be able to service the proposed loan commitments, however requires assistance from a guarantor to provide a security. A guarantee is required from each mortgagor who is not a borrower for a particular facility.
  • Income (unsupported) guarantee: The guarantor’s income is used to support the loan application, i.e. the applicant’s capacity to service the loan commitments is dependent on the income of the guarantor. Reliance on income from a guarantor other than a married or de facto partner is unacceptable.
If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, refer to Lenders Mortgage Insurance where required
 

1.3 Background - Guarantees

The National Credit Code (NCC) and the Banking Code of Practice (BCoP) provide protections to individuals who enter into a guarantee as security for a loan.

The following is a summary of the broad principles regarding personal guarantees:
  • In all instances, regardless of product whenever a guarantee is to be taken, we require guarantors to obtain independent legal advice before entering into a guarantee for all Security Guarantees, and recommend they obtain independent legal advice for all Income Guarantees (if both Security and Income Guarantee is taken, legal advice is required). The completed Guarantor’s Solicitor’s Certificate is to be provided to the Bank prior to settlement (i.e. release of loan funds) to evidence that independent legal advice has been received. Waiving of independent legal advice where it is mandatory under policy may only be approved by Consumer Bank Credit / Group Credit on exception basis.
  • In the case of a Security (supported) Guarantee, a guarantor's income cannot be used to assist in the assessment of the borrower’s ability to service the new loan. The borrower must be able to service the loan on their own, or by virtue of a company or trust completely controlled by the borrower alone.
  • In all instances, prospective guarantors are to be informed that they need to make any appropriate enquiries about the credit worthiness, financial position and integrity of the borrowers. This should be pointed out at application and again at approval. (The prospective guarantors are reminded of this prior to signing the Guarantee and Indemnity.)
 

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Acceptable Guarantee Scenarios

For Consumer Lending policy, the Bank requires a guarantor to be an individual, an entity cannot be a guarantor.
It is contrary to Bank policy to add a borrower and or guarantor for the purpose of increasing serviceability.
 
Guarantees can be categorised as

  • Trustee’s Guarantee
  • Spousal Guarantee 
  • Family Security Guarantees
  • Other

The value ($) of the guarantee must be for the amount of credit (full loan amount) except for:

  • Family Security guarantees

  • Other exceptions approved by Credit which are limited to lending value required

The following rules apply to applications that are wholly or in partially secured by a personal guarantee:

Guarantee Scenario

Policy Rules

Trustee (personal trustees) Guarantees
 

The following Trust scenarios are acceptable:

  • Individual trustees guaranteeing a Family Trust.

  • Family Trust (personal trustees) guaranteeing an individual who must be a trustee of the Family Trust.

Spousal Guarantee (married or de facto)
 

A spouse (married or de facto) can provide a Security (Supported) Guarantee, an Income (Unsupported) Guarantee, or both a Security and Income Guarantee, where the following conditions have been met:

  • Application is for a single applicant and guarantor who are spousal (married or de facto)

  • The applicant / borrower generates an income in their own capacity

  • Where providing an Income (unsupported) Guarantee or both a Security and Income Guarantee:
    • The guarantor has acknowledged the arrangeent by signing the Financial Support Acknowlegdment and Undertaking
    • Guarantor income, assets and commitments is obtained and entered into the loan application

    • Income documentation is to be provided and income verified as per Verifying Income policy

    • Commitments documentation is to be provided to be verified as per Commitments policy

Family Security Guarantees
 

Certain family members / relatives of the borrower can be guarantors and use their property or Westpac Term Deposit as security to support the loan application. In these cases, borrowers can finance up to 100% of the purchase price, refinance amount or construction cost where:

  • Acceptable Purposes:
    • Purchase or refinance of:
      •  Owner Occupied - a property for own occupation where the borrower(s) have ownership of a maximum of one other property and the already owned property does not have sufficient equity to provide security for the new purchase without incurring mortgage insurance; or
      • Investment - a property for investment where the borrower(s) do not have ownership of any other property at time of application
    • Construction by Licensed Builder (not available for Owner Builder construction)
    • Direct costs associated with the security purchase / refinance / construction and provision of the loan - direct costs include Government costs, Solicitors costs, eligible Bank fees and Insurance costs.
    • Home improvements funded via progress drawdowns
    • Existing loan increase solely for the purpose of home improvement where:
      • an increase to an existing Family Security Guarantee is not required, and
      • funding is via progressive drawdowns (loan will need to be re-originated)
  • Un-acceptable Purposes (for new originations or increases to existing loans) including but not limited to:
    • Refinance of any debt other than that listed as 'acceptable' above (e,g, non-mortgage debt consolidation of any personal debts such as credit card or personal loans into the loan amount). Exceptions to this policy condition are not permitted. 
    • Security Guarantee added to an existing loan (loan to be fully re-originated)
    • Owner builder construction
    • Cash Out
    • Access to equity in the borrower’s property for the purpose of providing supporting security to another loan (via cross collateralisation or debt allocation /debt apportioning) while a Family Security Guarantee is in place.  If equity has been established in the borrower’s property, the borrower(s) are encouraged to release the Family Security Guarantee / guarantor’s security property.  Once the borrower is no longer receiving a benefit from a Family Security Guarantee, application processing can continue as per standard policy for home loan option and debt allocation may be considered.
  • Individual applicants are restricted to a maximum of one Family Security Guarantee
  • It is not acceptable to be taking a Family Security Guarantee where the borrower is seeking a Line of Credit (EAL) or Bridging loan product
  • Specific restrictions apply to the guarantor/s as follows:
    • Acceptable Family Security Guarantors are:
      • Parents or legal guardians,
      • Siblings; or
      • Sons and daughters.
Note: Step-parents, step-siblings and step-children are also acceptable as Family Security Guarantors.
  • No single Guarantee is to represent more than 50% of the value of the guarantor’s security
  • The Guarantee is to be supported by a 1st or 2nd mortgage other than:
    • Where the existing 1st mortgage is a reverse mortgage-styled facility; or
    • Where security is a 2nd mortgage and LMI is required and the first mortgage is not held by Westpac Brand (other group brands considered OFI)
  • Only one property can be offered by the guarantor/s as security for the Guarantee, however that property can be used for more than one Family Security Guarantee (e.g. property offered as guarantee to support other siblings’ loan)
  • The guarantor must have sufficient equity in their property such that LMI would not be required in order to support the limited guarantee being provided. 
  • For Mortgage Insured Loans:
    • The applicants need to contribute a minimum of 5% genuine savings of the security value of the property being purchased / refinanced / constructed where base LVR exceeds 85%
    • Short form valuations Mandatory for all security for proposals involving LMI 
    • Vacant land security will only be acceptable for the Borrowers secrity where we are also providing a construction loan (either as full approval or AIP held in the system) for LMI. (Vacant Land is acceptable as the Family Guarantor security subject to meeting acceptable security policy requirements)
    • Where LMI is required, the Borrower’s debt solely is to be insured – the Guarantee against the guarantor/s property cannot require LMI 
    • Maximum LVR is 90%, and no LMI premium capitalisation is permitted
    • Term Deposits as security are not acceptable for LMI
  • Serviceability is required to be demonstrated by the borrower/s independently of the guarantor/s
  • The value ($) of the Guarantee can be limited to less than the amount of Credit (Loan amount) to meet the LVR Policy. Refer to ‘Calculating available equity and limited guarantee amount for a Family-Security Guarantee’

Other Guarantee Situations
 

Other guarantee proposals are generally not acceptable unless a direct benefit can be established.
Requirements for establishing that the guarantor will receive a direct benefit will vary from one application to another. Westpac is required to demonstrate that the guarantor made a fully informed decision and will receive a measurable benefit that is comparable to the risk they are taking on. A financial benefit is the easiest to define and measure (e.g. the guarantor living in the property they are guaranteeing free of rent). Non-financial benefit is more difficult to measure and is, therefore, unlikely to be approved where the relationship is something other than a parent guaranteeing a loan for their children

  • Contact your dedicated Credit Hotline to discuss the nature of the application and get approval to proceed

  • If the application is to proceed, it will need to be referred to Credit for assessment before the provisional approval decision is advised to the customer.

  • Regardless of the guarantee amount, the borrowers are required to contribute a minimum of 5% of the security value of the property being purchased plus costs. The funds are to be visible in the applicants' assets with a comment in the application detailing which assets are being used to meet this requirement

 

2.2 Unacceptable guarantee scenarios

The following are unacceptable guarantors:

  • Non Residents
  • Temporary Australian Residency Visa Holders
  • Entities (these should be referred to Business Banking)
  • Any guarantee structure not identified as acceptable in this policy

Refer to  Migrant Lending>Rules you must apply>Visa Matrix and Applicant via Resident Type Matrix .

Note: A borrower that is unacceptable as per the Visa Matrix and Applicant via Resident Type Matrix is also unacceptable as a guarantor. 

 

2.3 Providing advice to borrowers

Where the proposed loan is either wholly or in part secured by a Security and/or Income Guarantee, the prospective borrowers must be made aware of the following requirements at the time of the interview:

  • Loans that involve individuals as Security guarantors are conditional on the guarantors obtaining independent legal advice and the Bank requires evidence that legal advice has been obtained. 
  • Independent legal advice for loans involving an Income Guarantor is recommended but not mandatory.
  • The solicitor's cost is the responsibility of the guarantor (or borrower).

Do not provide any advice in respect to the nature and effect of the guarantee.

Note: Do not entrust the customer / borrower to act as Westpac's 'agent' to procure the guarantor's signature. Hand a copy of the Guarantor's Consent to Variation, Replacement or Alteration (LD164) to each guarantor. Alternatively, the consent form can be posted to the individual guarantor.

 

2.4 Providing advice to guarantors

The guarantor/s must be interviewed separately by the Broker without the borrower present.
At the interview, all prospective guarantors must be made aware of that:

  • Independent legal advice and evidence of this advice is mandatory  for all Security Guarantees

  • It is recommended (however not mandatory) that legal advice is sought for Income Guarantees

  • There are financial risks involved in providing a guarantee, i.e. when the borrower is unable to the meet their financial obligations under the loan, the guarantor may need to pay for the debt commitments on behalf of the borrower.

  • The guarantor may request information about the proposed loan to be guaranteed.

If legal advice is required the guarantor needs to obtain this from a solicitor independent of the bank and the borrower to understand their obligations and liability under the guarantee.

Exceptions from legal advice

In the following scenarios legal advice may not be mandatory:

Scenario

Description

Mortgagor is also a co-borrower

Generally, the Bank does not consider a joint borrower as a guarantor.
The Bank does not insist on legal advice, except where the Bank has knowledge that the joint borrowing is for the sole benefit of the borrower who is not providing the security.
A co-borrower must not be included in a loan simply to ensure credit approval, particularly if the co-borrower is giving the security and will not benefit from the loan. In that case the co-borrower might later successfully claim that they were pressured into joining the primary applicant, with the result that they are released from any liability to the Bank.

Family Trust Guarantees processed by Business Banking Services (BBS)

Independent legal advice is mandatory for Family Trust Guarantees unless waived by Credit

 
Independence of the solicitor

The guarantor's solicitor must be independent of the Bank and the borrower, this includes:

  • The solicitor is not to be a solicitor for the Bank or for the borrower in the particular transaction

  • The solicitor is not being a solicitor for any other interested party who might have previously given security. An example of an interested party is a guarantor who, having previously provided security, stands to benefit by way of reduced liability, from any additional security given by other guarantors.

  • The solicitor is not to be a partner of any solicitor who acts for the borrower or other interested party.

Exemption of independence rule

The solicitor can amend the certificate to signify that they also act for the borrower. Westpac, as a matter of policy exception, will generally accept the solicitor's certificate in such cases.
Exemption will not apply, for example, if:

  • A guarantor is open to influence by the others, or

  • One guarantor is a spouse of the director who 'runs' the business, and is only involved in a formal manner or in some minor capacity.

 
 
Determining undue influence on the guarantor

If you know, or suspect, the guarantee relationship is one of dependency, reliance, or dominance, you must note in ApplyOnline comments so the Bank can insist on independent legal advice and a solicitor’s certificate. Otherwise, the courts might presume that there was undue influence and set aside the guarantee.
To help to determine whether there is undue influence, consider following questions:

  1. Might anyone, in addition to the borrower, benefit from the guarantee being given?

This could include:

  • Other individuals who have already given, or are about to give, guarantees (husband and wife)

  • If the borrower is a trust, a beneficiary of the trust, or

  • Anybody else who has a significant direct or indirect interest in the borrower (e.g. shareholder)

  1. What is the nature of the relationship between the guarantor and any other benefited person?

With some relationships there is a presumption of undue influence no matter (e.g. solicitor and client; parent and dependent child; trustee and beneficiary; priest and parishioner; doctor and patient). With others it depends on individual circumstances.
Where a spouse is providing a guarantee, it is safer to strongly recommend a solicitor's certificate except where it is clear that the spouse is:

  • Financially sophisticated

  • Aware of the borrower's position

  • Obtaining some benefit from the transaction.

2.6 Calculating available equity and limited guarantee amount for a Family Security Guarantee

The amount of available equity in security offered as the ‘Family –Security Guarantee’ must be determined, to ensure there is sufficient equity to provide the guarantee.

The table below details how to calculate available equity and limited guarantee amount for a loan involving a ‘Family Security Guarantee’.
Also refer to Process>Family Security Guarantee>Limited Guarantee
Feature Calculation
Available Equity in the Family Security Guarantee Determine:
A. the guarantee security value
B. the maximum non LMI LVR as per Loan to Value (LVR) policy
C1. any existing commitments (such as mortgages, pledges, debts, guarantees, pledges, business overdrafts or other liabilities) which have a right against the guarantee security
 
Available Equity = (A ‘Guarantor’s security value’ multiplied by B ‘Non LMI LVR%’) minus C1 ‘Existing commitments’
 
e.g. (A $500,000 multiplied by B 80% = $400,000) minus C1 $100,000 = $300,000 Available Equity
 
Limited Guarantee Amount Determine:
A. Loan amount
B. the non- guarantee security value
C. the non-MI LVR for the non-guarantee security as per Loan to Value (LVR) policy
D. the non-MI LVR for the guarantee security as per Loan to Value (LVR) policy
 
Guarantee Limit/Amount =( A ‘Loan amount’ minus  (B ‘Non-guarantee security collateral value’ multiplied by C ‘Non MI LVR% for the non-guarantee security collateral’ )) divided by D  ‘Non MI LVR% for the guarantee security’
 e.g.
(A $500,000 minus   (B $600,000 x C 80% of = $480,000 = $20,000)) divided by D 80% = $25,000 Limited Guarantee Amount

1 Add a 20% buffer to the value of a debt (higher of limit or outstanding balance) that is secured by a first mortgage to an ‘other’ financial institution.
Within Westpac: SGB, BSA, BoM and RAMS are considered to be ‘other’ financial institutions.
 
 

3. Process to follow

3.1 Family Security Guarantee - Limited Guarantee

We will take a guarantee limited by a dollar amount from a family member, supported by mortgage over an acceptable security in terms of policy (refer to Acceptable Securities policy).
The following is the process required to determine acceptability:

  • The supporting security property must have sufficient equity available, after taking into account any existing (prior) mortgages, guarantees, pledges or other liabilities, as well as the amount  of the Guarantee, to support a maximum total LVR that would not require LMI under the Bank’s normal LVR policy
  • Confirm with the guarantor the total amount of any existing debts that may be secured by the guarantee property. The guarantor may need to consider not only the current balance of the primary loan they have outstanding, but any available redraw / unused credit limits, any prior  guarantees issued to other family members, business overdrafts or investment loans which may have been supported (cross-collateralised) against the guarantors security property.
     
    • Verify details of each of the guarantor/s commitments that are secured by guarantors property as per Commitments policy > verifying customer commitments > commitment type.  
    • Where the commitment is not the guarantors own but relates to an additional existing guarantee, obtain a copy of the existing guarantee details. 
Use the Guarantee calculator (in Assess “Other Calculators”) to determine the amount of the limited supported guarantee to be taken. The amount of the Guarantee can then be loaded into ApplyOnline
