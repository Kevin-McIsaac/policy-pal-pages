1. About this policy

1.1 About Lenders Mortgage Insurance

Lenders Mortgage Insurance:

  • Protects Westpac against loss from a borrower defaulting under a loan arrangement
  • Does not provide mortgage repayment insurance for the borrower
  • Is generally available for housing, investment property and equity loans only
  • Is available for most types of residential property
  • Is required when the loan to security value ratio (LVR) exceeds Westpac’s maximum lending value ratio.  Refer Assessing Loan to Value Ratio (LVR)
  • Cover provided will be for 100% of the principal, interest, legal and selling costs.

1 Capitalised LVR is used when the premium has been capitalised
 

2.2 Approval Authority

  When issuing an LMI policy, approval or approval in principle should not be issued until applicants have met acceptable minimum credit standards 
 WLMI operates a Delegated Underwriting Authority (“DUA”) in conjunction with authorised WBC Credit employees, whereby WBC may issue LMI policies on behalf of WLMI , provided that certain criteria are met.
Approval Criteria is detailed within Consumer Credit Policy. 
WLMI also has its own set of acceptance criteria based on the level of risk it is prepared to undertake, and may vary from the Bank’s credit policy for acceptable risk.
NOTE:
  •  An application meeting LMI lending value ratio is not an indication of approval
 

2.3 Genuine savings, 10% equity, Customer contribution

New loans and loan increases requiring LMI must comply with the policy detailed under Refer to Genuine Savings, Equity and Customer Contribution
 

2.4 Premium Capitalisation

Customers have the option to capitalise the LMI premium on top of the base loan amount.
All Credit rules utilise the base loan amount and the base LVR, with the exception of serviceability assessments where repayments are based on the capitalised loan amount
 

2.5 Existing insured loans - Variations and Top Up premiums

 
Scenario Action LMI waiver - Legacy LMI providers ‘QBE and Helia (formerly Genworth)’
For:
  • A Loan Increase or other variation to an existing mortgaged insured loan, or
  • An internal refinance of an existing mortgaged insured loan 1 , or
  • A new application using security collateral already covered by an existing mortgage insurance policy
  • Whenever the mortgage insurer’s risk is increased or amended, the mortgage insurer will be notified by the responsible processing area
  • Prior to the Bank notifying approval to the customer, the mortgage insurer is to approve the variation and use of the existing policy
  • The change will generally be applied to the existing policy and insurer
  • Where additional LMI is required, the premium will be calculated by using the new total exposure amount and LVR Policy, and taking into consideration any previous premiums paid by the customer (premium credits).
Top up premium requests will be completed by the approval confirmation area who will request the premium amount from the mortgage insurer.
 
Exceptions apply for ‘Legacy LMI providers ‘QBE and Helia (formerly Genworth)' as detailed in the next column		 Where Lender’s Mortgage Insurance is required and the existing policy cover is held with QBE or Helia (formerly Genworth):
  • Credit will waive Lender’s Mortgage Insurance up to the MI-Max base LVR applicable under ‘ Loan to Value Ratio (LVR) ’ policy for any of the below scenarios:
    • Loan increase to the existing insured loan
    • An internal refinance of the existing mortgaged insured loan 1
    • A new application using security collateral already covered by the existing mortgage insurance policy
Applications with this waiver applied are assessed to non-LMI standards
Warning:
It is not permissible to have multiple insurers with an interest in the same property.
For any new mortgage insured application, re-structure will be required, where:
  • An existing security collateral offered is already covered by an existing ALMI or WLMI mortgage insurance policy, and
  • Another existing security collateral offered is already covered by an existing QBE or Helia (formerly Genworth) mortgage insurance policy
  1 For WBC: SGB, BoM, BSA and RAMS are considered to be external providers

 

2.6 Requests for cancellation

The Mortgage Centre will request cancellation and appropriate premium refund only where:
Scenario Condition
For proposals which proceed
  • The loan has been paid and discharged in full, and
  • Within the first two years of the policy commencement, and
  • The loan has not been reported to have arrears, and
  • Where the insurer is QBE or Helia (formerly Genworth), the amount payable is not less than $500, or
  • Where the insurer is WLMI or WLMI-A, the amount payable is not less than $400
For proposals which do not proceed If the proposal does not proceed for any reason and the premium has already been paid
 
NOTE: Any requests for refunds where the loan is not repaid in full and discharged are not acceptable

2.7 Base LVR >90-95%

The below applies where the base LVR is >90-95% in addition to (or above where more restrictive) standard LMI policy
 

2.7.1 Purpose

Acceptable purposes:
  • The purchase or construction of owner occupied residential property
  • The purchase or construction of a residential investment property
  • The purchase of vacant land
  • Home improvements / additions.
Refer to Construction Loans for construction loan policy including progress draw requirements.
 

2.7.2 Security

All security properties must be valued as per Assessing Residential Security Value
 

2.7.3 Repayment Type

Loan repayments can be principal, interest and fees only or interest only, but must also comply with
  • The specific product policy, and
  • Assessing Loan Value Ratio (LVR)
 

2.7.4 Borrowers

All borrowers must be:
  • Mortgagors, and
  • Australian citizen living and working in Australia or a permanent resident living and working in Australia

 

2.7.5 Exclusions

The following exclusions apply:
  • Refinance of existing loans (except WBC loan refinances where the customer is returning to construct or improve property)
  • Equity Access Loans (EAL)
  • Loans for working capital
  • Loans to repay private loans, solicitor loans or directors loans
  • Loans for payment of personal or business tax liabilities
  • Adverse Bureau
  • Not available for company Borrowers or Family Trusts
  • Investment Property Loans (either standalone or combined with a Home loan or Equity Access loan) with all security being Non-owner occupied
  • No cash out permitted
Note:  Refer Assessing Loan Value Ratio (LVR) for full details on acceptable LVR’s for all products.

3. Premium rates, stamp duty

A premium for the Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) cover is:
  • Calculated by the Generic Origination Engine (GOE) or Loan Increase Application (LIA)
  • Calculated on the base loan amount and the base lending value ratio (LVR) -including where the premium is capitalised
  • Payable by the customer
Refer to LMI Premium Calculators on BrokerHub

4. Process to follow

4.1 Approval process

The Generic Origination Engine (GOE) or Loan Increase Application (LIA) will:

  • Assess whether Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) is required
  • Select the appropriate mortgage insurance company
The system generated credit decision will either:
  • Pass the mortgage insurance rules, or
  • Fail the mortgage insurance rules and be declined, or
  •  Fail the mortgage insurance rules and be referred to Credit Operations for a decision. Depending on the authority level required to assess a policy exception, Credit or the Mortgage Centre may need to refer to the insurer
Final approval is subject to the satisfactory completion of all verification activities required of the Broker and the Mortgage Centre.
