1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy

Purpose This policy identifies the standards associated with an applicant’s citizenship and residency status 
Applicable to Consumer mortgage applications where a borrower is seeking a
  • New loan
  • Loan Increase
  • Variation to an existing loan
  •  
Associated policies You may also need to refer to other lending policy rules, including but not limited to:
  • Document Standards
  • Eligibility
  • Lending Value Ratio (LVR)
  • Verifying income
  • If the application:
    • requires mortgage insurance, where required refer to:
      • Lenders Mortgage Insurance
    • involves the Home Guarantee Scheme, also refer to Home Guarantee Scheme

2. Rules you must apply


2.1 How to apply this policy

Establish citizenship and residency status: A customer’s citizenship, residency or non-residency status must be established at initial application stage for all consumer mortgage loans
Applying policy:
 		 Criteria: Policy rules

Eligible applicants:
  • Australian citizen
 Apply the ‘Eligibility  > Australian Citizen residency status’ policy
  • New Zealand citizen 
 Apply the ‘Applicant via resident type matrix’ policy
  • Not an Australian or New Zealand citizen, but:
    • holds an approved permanent residency visa issued by the Australian government, or
    • holds an approved temporary residency visa issued by the Australian government, and:
      • ordinarily resides in Australia
 Apply the ‘Migrant Lending’ policy
 
Ineligible applicants: Are defined as ‘Non-residents’. Lending to a non-resident is not permitted either as a borrower or guarantor, even where their income is not relied on to meet serviceability, for:
  • A new consumer mortgage, or
  • An increase to an existing consumer mortgage, or
  • A loan variation activity which requires a serviceability assessment. 
No exceptions to the above are permitted
Multiple applicants:
  • The LVR may be restricted based on the co-applicant features (e.g citizenship, visa type). Refer Loan to Value Ratio (LVR) > LVR by borrower type and income source
 


2.2 Migrant lending

Definitions: Migrant: A migrant is defined as an individual that has been granted an Australian government residence visa for entry into Australia
Permanent residence visa: Allows a person unrestricted working rights in Australia and they can remain in Australia indefinitely
 
Temporary residence visa: Allows a person to live in Australia for a limited time only and they may have restricted working rights
When to apply this policy: Refer to ‘How to apply this policy’
Policy rules: Criteria: Policy Rules
Acceptable Visa Matrix and Applicant via resident type matrix:
  • The visa number must listed on and meet the applicable conditions of the Acceptable Visa matrix 
  • Applicable conditions in the Applicant via resident type matrix must be met
  • Any visa conditions must be met e.g.
    • Business visa types should reflect income status and documentation from within Australia.
    • Sponsored visa types should reflect income from that region, i.e. state or territory sponsored visa's should reflect income earned within Australia.
Unacceptable Visa If a visa is listed as an Unacceptable Visa, the visa holder cannot be included either as a borrower, guarantor and their income cannot be used toward serviceability.

No exceptions permitted
Visa not listed  A permanent or temporary visa that is not listed:
  • can be referred to Credit for consideration, if supported by comments / notes and justification for acceptance
Evidencing passport and visa: A copy of the migrant’s current passport and evidence of Australian visa details (excluding the Australian visa number) must be obtained and held on file
Additional requirements:
Expired passport: Acceptable if:
  • supported by a copy of a current permanent residence visa, and
  • applicant resides in Australia
Passport not held: The application may be referred to Credit for further consideration. (e.g. a refugee might only hold a Protection visa subclass 866)
Foreign investment review board (FIRB) approval: If the ‘Applicant via resident Type Matrix’ specifies ‘FIRB required’, the lender / assessor must:
    • Hold a copy of the FIRB approval prior to issuing a formal approval. For a refinance, the original FIRB approval is required
    • Validate that the loan application complies with the conditions under which the FIRB approval was issued
Also refer to  ‘FIRB approval exception’
Additional requirements:
Applicant living and working overseas: If FIRB indicates approval is for their principal place of residence, then the property:
  • Must be their principal place of residence while in Australia.
  • Cannot be used for investment (rental) or holiday purposes and rental income earned by this property is not acceptable
FIRB approval to a specific development: In some cases FIRB approval is granted to a specific development. The Developer may receive a certificate identifying that further foreign investment approval is not required when sales are made to foreign persons.

A copy of the FIRB approval issued to the developer is to be retained on file. The offered security property (for purchase or refinance) should match the property identified in the FIRB approval.
FIRB approval exception: FIRB approval is not required where the applicant is purchasing or owns a property as Joint Tenants* (50/50), with an Australian or New Zealand citizen, or a Permanent Resident, who is their spouse.
 
*Exception does not apply if the property ownership is ‘Tenants in Common’ (e.g. 80 / 20)’

Authorities to Return (ATR) and Return Endorsements (RE) are old style return visas issued to permanent residents between 1 March 1976 and 31 December 1986. Evidence was issued in the form of a stamp or label in the holder’s passport and contain the words ‘Authority to Return’ or ‘Return Endorsement’. Authorities to Return and Return Endorsements can no longer be granted but may still be valid. The issue with the ATR/RE is they may only be evidenced in the passport and the Department of Home Affairs will not know from their own systems whether or not the visa was ever granted.

2.4 Proof of own contribution

Definition: Funds to complete or own contribution is generally considered to be the shortfall between the purchase price (inclusive of legal and stamp duty) and the loan being provided by the Bank.
 
Policy rules: Criteria: Policy Rules
If the Applicant via Resident Type Matrix specifies ‘proof of own contribution required’:
 		 Applicant(s) must provide evidence of their funds to complete / own contribution via account statements, to confirm that the source of funds is from a genuine financial institution (e.g. bank)
Where the account statement(s) does not evidence the full funds to complete / own contribution, it is acceptable to proceed, where a satisfactory explanation is entered in application supporting notes, e.g. the applicant has paid a deposit on the property being purchased and where combined with funds evidenced via account statements, confirms funds to complete / own contribution.
If the above does not apply: Apply the ‘Genuine Savings, Equity and Customer Contribution > Customer Contribution’ policy
 

3. Process to follow

3.1 Migrant lending package application process

Process application as per normal and upload a copy of customer's visa type within ApplyOnline in accordance with section 3.2 below. In loan application notes, add any supporting information (excluding applicant’s Australian visa number).

Note:
All other lending criteria and process remain unchanged, such as the customer must hold a valid IDV and income documents.
All migrant temporary visa borrowers must provide a copy of their Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB) approval (where applicable), refer Approval from FIRB, which must be complied with and uploaded into ApplyOnline.
 

3.2 Evidencing migrant visa

  • Migrant status must be evidenced via provision of a:
    • copy of a work visa approval issued by Department of Home Affairs – Immigration and citizenship; or
    • printout supplied by the customer from the VEVO website https://online.immi.gov.au/evo/firstParty?actionType=query
  • Where an applicant’s migrant category is Investor Visa type, evidence is to be provided of approved deposit funds held with State Treasury
