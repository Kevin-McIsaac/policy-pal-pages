Credit policy and procedures

Income validation

Information on how we assess other income types. Excludes PAYG and self-employed.

Last update: 8 December 2023

Income source

  • Dividends
  • Extended leave
  • Foreign income
  • Government payments
  • Government pension
  • Paid parental leave
  • Superannuation
  • Workers compensation

Dividend income

Income validations for investment income generated from shares listed on the ASX 200.

Validate using 12-month dividend advice notice

  1. Identify dividend payments — identify the value at which the shares were paid, as per the dividend notice confirmation.
  2. Confirm current shareholding - confirm the number of shares currently held as per the statement or letter held. Examples of acceptable statements include CHESS statement and shareholding certificate.
  3. Calculating total dividend value.
    • Use the total income as per the dividend advice notice if the number of shares remains the same or has increased since the date on the dividend notice.
    • If the number of shares has reduced since the date on the dividend notice, multiply the number of shares by the dividend paid as per the dividend notice.

Validate income with Australian tax return

  1. Identify shares from the Australian Tax Return or Shareholding Balance. Confirm that the shares are listed on ASX 200.
  2. Identify dividend payments - identify the franking dividend listed on the Australian Tax Return. Franking credits should not be used.
  3. Confirm current shareholding – confirm that the applicant still holds the shares from the statement or letter held. Examples of acceptable statements include CHESS statement or Shareholding certificate.
  4. Calculating total dividend value. Use the total franking dividend received as per the Australian Tax Return.

Extended leave

  1. Validate the paid leave, if applicable. Do not annualise this amount.
  2. Validate the return-to-work base salary.
  3. Validate the return-to-work letter. The letter must:
    • Be on company letterhead.
    • Include applicants name.
    • Include current length of employment.
    • Detail the terms of extended leave.
    • Include the return-to-work date.
    • Include base PAYG salary upon return to work.
  4. Complete all three servicing calculators (NICL) including servicing position (paid leave), servicing position during extended leave (unpaid leave) and servicing position on return to work (servicing pass).
  5. Review the savings evidence held (i.e. Australian savings account bank statement) to ensure sufficient funds are held to cover the servicing deficit during extended leave.
  6. Ensure that the foreseeable change in circumstances question is marked as ‘Yes’.

Foreign income

For verification of foreign income received in a currency other than Australian Dollars (AUD), please refer to the Foreign PAYG Income Calculator tab on the Serviceability Calculator.

Government payments and pension

Government payments

  1. Identify the number of dependents and age of each dependent.
  2. Review the evidence for payment amounts.

Note: Deductions relating to an advancement/debt must be included in the applicant’s statement of position as a liability.

Example:
For two dependents listed aged 10 and 15
Total payment of $800
Payment amount used: $800 / 2 = $400
Supplementary payments are not acceptable as part of servicing.
For any dependents aged 15 and over, their payments must not be utilised.

Government pension

  1. Review the evidence for payment amounts.
  2. Record pensions as Personal income type.

Supplement payments are not acceptable as part of servicing.

Note: Deductions relating to an advancement/debt must be included in the applicant’s statement of position as a liability.

Paid parental leave

This is how the validations team verifies parental leave income.

  1. Calculate the remaining salary to be received by the applicant. Do not annualise this.
  2. Calculate the total payments to be received from Centrelink, if applicable. Do not annualise this.

Ensure foreseeable change in circumstances has been marked as ‘Yes’.

Superannuation

Retail superannuation

  1. Identify the annual pension amount by reviewing the superannuation statement provided.
    For payments from Commonwealth Superannuation Corporation, refer to the Government pensions and payments policy.
  2. Confirm that the payments are not made in lump sum - are received as an income stream, i.e. received monthly.

Note: Lump sum payments are unacceptable.

Self-managed superannuation fund

  1. Review all evidence provided as per policy.
  2. Use the taxable (gross) and tax-free (net) components.

Workers compensation

  1. Validate income using the payment and frequency as per the insurer letter provided.
