About the Code

The Banking Code of Practice (the Code) is an industry code that’s published by the Australian Banking Association (ABA). The Code sets out the banking industry’s key commitments to individual and small business customers and their respective guarantors, and serves as the benchmark for governance across the banking industry.

The Code consists of 10 parts and covers the following topics:

  • How the Code works
  • Your banking relationship
  • Opening an account and using our banking services
  • Inclusive and accessible banking
  • When you apply for a loan
  • Lending to small business
  • Guaranteeing a loan
  • Managing your account
  • When things go wrong
  • Resolving your complaint

A guarantee will be covered by BCOP where the guarantee is given by an individual, and the guarantee secures a loan that is given to another individual or a small business that is covered by BCOP (clause 93). The Code gives more clarity on the definitions of who may be covered:

  • An individual is a person who is not treated as a business under the Code (a customer is treated as a business if the receive or apply for a banking service for a predominantly trading or commercial purpose and where the NCCC does not apply) (cl 1).
  • A small business is one where at the time of receiving the banking service, one of the following apply (cl 1):
    a) it had an annual turnover of less than $10 million in the previous financial year; and
    b) it has fewer than 100 full-time equivalent employees; and
    c) it has less than $3 million total debt to all credit providers – including:
        i. any undrawn amounts under existing loans;
        ii. any loan being applied for; and
        iii. the debt of all its related entities that are businesses.

NAB has adopted the approach that a small business is one where its aggregate business lending is less than $5m.
