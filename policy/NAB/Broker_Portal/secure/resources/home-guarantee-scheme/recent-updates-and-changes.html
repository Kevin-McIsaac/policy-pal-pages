1 July 2024 - NOW LIVE Home Guarantee Scheme – FY25

NAB is pleased to continue helping customers into their first home with the new release of Home Guarantee Scheme (HGS) FY25 Scheme places.

FY25 Scheme Places (From 01 July 2024):

NAB has uplifted evidence requirements for FY25 to ensure conformance to HGS Scheme Rules. These changes include:

Australian Passports: Must be current and valid if being utilised to evidence Australian Citizenship.

Permanent Residency (NZ Citizens): New Zealand Citizens who hold a Special Category Visa (SCV) 444 are considered to be an ‘Australian Permanent Resident’ and therefore eligible for the scheme.

All Citizenship/Age documentation: Must be certified true copies and can be attested via:

  • Sighting the documents in person and provide an attestation in the applications notes; OR
  • Include these documents as part of the IDYou process (VOI); OR
  • Provide photocopies which are certified by an authorised person.

Genuine Savings: Ensure post-settlement customer savings are comprehensively explained, especially if post-settlement savings are greater than $30,000. If customers have savings of 20% or more evidenced, they are not eligible for the schemes.

Post-Settlement Address:

HGS rules state that customers must move into the property within the timeframe set out by Housing Australia.

Customers must update their address once they have purchased their property, by either:

  • Confirming it as the post-settlement address in the lending application; or
  • Letting NAB know once they’ve moved in

Note:  For existing purchases, the customer(s) must move in within 6 months.

For construction purchases, the customer(s) must move in once an occupancy certificate is issued. 

Ongoing Scheme Eligibility: Customers must also be reminded that the HGS secured property must not be rented out as per the conditions of the scheme otherwise the Government Guarantee will be void, meaning they will be liable for any shortfall should they find themselves in a default position.

20 November 2023 – Uplifting of Maximum Deposit Utilised and Retained Savings Policy

As part of NAB’s commitment to upholding the intent and integrity of the Home Guarantee schemes, we will be requiring brokers to confirm that customers are utilising their maximum deposit for the HGS schemes in the form of a note and to provide an explanation as to the purpose of any retained savings after utilising their maximum deposit when applying under the Home Guarantee Schemes.

Under Section 29K of Housing Australia’s Investment Mandate (IM), one of the key principles for the operation of the Home Guarantee Schemes is to:

“ensure that eligible home buyers have used the maximum amount of savings as a deposit, subject to the credit policies of eligible lenders”

This means that customers applying for a Home Guarantee Scheme must use their maximum deposit available and not look to minimise their deposit amount when purchasing a new property for the purpose of maximising LVR and the Government Guarantee.

Important: If customers have a 20% deposit or more evidenced in their accounts/statements or lending application then they are not eligible for the schemes.

As such, where customers have total savings which amount to less than 20% of the Property Purchase Price and are planning to retain some savings for purchase-related costs such as moving costs, furniture and/or other personal reasons, please ensure this is documented in your application and provide specific reasoning as to why they are retaining certain savings -

  • Customer(s) have utilised their maximum deposit for the HGS schemes and any retained savings is to be used for (state reasons)

01 July 2023 - Home Guarantee Scheme – FY24 Expanded Eligibility Criteria


Assisting more home buyers with the expanded Eligibility Criteria

The Home Guarantee Scheme (HGS) will be expanding their Eligibility Criteria from 1 July 2023 for the FY24 implementation of the scheme.

The first change will enable Australian Permanent Residents, in addition to Citizens, to apply for all the schemes currently on offer; i.e the First Home Buyer Guarantee, Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee and the Family Home Guarantee.

Where joint applications previously required the applicants to be either married or defacto with each other, moving forward the FY24 First Home Buyer and Regional First Home Buyer Guarantees will allow joint applications irrespective of their marital status; i.e. friends, siblings and other family members will be able to participate.

The First Home and Regional First Home Buyer Guarantees will also allow non-first home buyers to join the schemes, ensuring they have not owned a property in Australia in the last 10 years.

Lastly, single legal guardians such as aunts, uncles and grandparents will be eligible alongside single natural or adoptive parents for the FY24 Family Home Guarantee.

What you need to know

From Saturday 1 July 2023, the FY24 scheme places will be made available. Please note that the above expanded eligibility criteria will only apply to applicants who have scheme reservations made on or after 1 July 2023. FY23 scheme places or prior will continue to abide by the previous eligibility criteria.

Important: NAB can support your customers with HGS applications submitted under the current FY23 eligibility. There will however be a small interim period where NAB cannot accept applications under the FY24 new expanded eligibility criteria (see below for new expanded eligibility) while we upgrade our lodgement systems to support these new requirements.

We anticipate that NAB will be in a position to accept new FY24 eligibility applications from late July 2023. Keep an eye out for further communication in the coming weeks to provide you with updated information and verification requirements needed.

Expression of Interest (New eligibility)

In the meantime, to support further, you can email HGSBrokerSupport@nab.com.au with the attached template (DOCX, 50KB), opens in a new window to join the waitlist for Permanent Residents and/or joint applicants who are not married or de facto. This will allow us to contact you in due course to submit the application once our lodgement systems are updated.

FY24 Marital evidence for Married/De-Facto:

As part of the FY24 change, we will no longer be required to evidence Married/de facto status for joint applicants under the First Home Buyer or Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee schemes.

Please note that this only applies for scheme places reserved on or after 1 July 2023. If reserved an FY23 scheme place, we will still require marital status evidence as previously highlighted. Whilst it’s still mandatory to try and obtain evidence to confirm de facto status for joint applicants (if not married), you will now be able to use the Home Buyer Declaration if efforts to obtain other forms of evidence from your customers are not possible.

  • Input a note or provide an email detailing effort in obtaining the other forms of evidence, and if unable to obtain that you are relying on Home Buyer Declaration to evidence the De Facto status. (FY23 or prior scheme places only)

What’s next?
Start conversations with your customers about how you can help them buy their new home sooner with only a 5% deposit for the First Home Guarantee, Regional First Home Buyers Guarantee or 2% deposit for the Family Home Guarantee with no Lenders Mortgage Insurance. Then, get them ready to complete their conditional approval application so they have the best chance of securing a new Scheme place when it’s released on 1 July 2023.
 

09 May 2023 - Home Guarantee Scheme – Evidence Requirements Update


Making evidence requirements easier for our customers

What you need to know

From Thursday 4 May 2023, the evidence requirements for certain Home Guarantee Scheme applications criteria will be improved to reduce roadblocks and pain points. This will also support easier processing allowing for a shorter time to unconditional approval for applications under the Home Guarantee Schemes.

Marital Status for De-Facto:

Whilst it’s still mandatory to try and obtain evidence to confirm de facto status for joint applicants (if not married), you will now be able to use the Home Buyer Declaration if efforts to obtain other forms of evidence from your customers are not possible.

  • Input a note or provide an email detailing effort in obtaining the other forms of evidence, and if unable to obtain that you are relying on Home Buyer Declaration to evidence the De Facto status.

Home Buyer Declaration:

If a customer hasn’t provided the Home Buyer Declaration in its entirety, we now just need to ensure the customer has completed the required sections for the scheme type they have entered into to proceed.

  • Example: if a First Home Buyer Guarantee application, then you would need to ensure at a minimum that Parts A, D and F are completed and returned (pages 1-5, 9, 11 & 12) instead of requiring all pages (1-12) to be provided.

Regional First Home Buyer – Evidence of Living in an eligible Regional Area:

If your customer is unable to provide the current forms of evidence, they can now provide documentation that evidence the following:

  • their name/s
  • address (in an eligible regional area)
  • length of time – whether frequency in terms of month, quarterly, half yearly, yearly or in terms of a period, i.e. January to March 2023 can be accepted.

Important: This evidence must be an authentic document provided by a company/organisation and can’t be a document created for the purposes of meeting this evidence requirement, i.e. a word document.

Retained Savings:

NHFIC require customers to utilise as much of their deposit with regards to the Home Guarantee Schemes and not to minimise their deposit provided to maximise LVR. Improved work instructions will be provided to ensure brokers are aware of this requirement.

Further support

If you have any questions, email the HGS Support Team at HGSBrokerSupport@nab.com.au or speak to your BDM.

30 September 2022 – Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee Scheme FY23 Release from 1 October 2022

Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee Scheme – open for applications at NAB

NAB has helped more than 13,000 customers purchase their first home through the Home Guarantee Scheme.

Soon, the Regional First Home Buyers Guarantee (RFHBG) will be available to assist aspiring first home buyers living in regional Australia achieve their dreams of home ownership sooner.

This new scheme will join the current schemes, the First Home Guarantee and Family Home Guarantee, allowing first home buyers to purchase a home with just a 5% deposit.

What you need to know

Starting 1 October 2022, there will be 10,000 RFHBG scheme places made available across all participating Home Guarantee Scheme Lenders per financial year until 2025.

Property price caps, Notice of Assessment cap and the minimum 5% deposit requirements remain the same as the First Home Guarantee. 

Eligibility for the RFHBG includes:

  • First home buyer(s) must have lived in a regional area for at least the 12 months prior to applying. If joint, at least one of the borrowers must have lived in the regional area for at least 12 months.
    • Evidence for this may include
      • Copy of utility bill for relevant period
      • Copy of rental agreement for 12-month period
      • Copy of previous FY NOA’s which cover eligibility period at the same regional address
    • Australian Defence Force members may be eligible for an exemption ensuring they provide an official statement confirming any posting required in the course of the person performing their duties over the last 12 months.
  • Eligible applicant(s) will then be able to purchase a home in their current or adjoining regional area.
  • The NHFIC Property Price Cap page will include a new tool to check if and where the eligible purchase may be located.

To apply, follow the instructions on the NAB Broker Portal to confirm your customer is eligible for the Scheme, then submit an application via ApplyOnline.

Make sure you add ‘Government Guarantee – First Home Guarantee’ as a secondary security in the Security tab, include 7777-77-77777NAB7 as a dummy ID for the Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee scheme and attach the required supporting documents to confirm eligibility.

Important: NHFIC require applicant(s) to apply for the relevant scheme they are eligible for utilising a ‘waterfall’ approach; i.e.,

  • where a borrower(s) is/are eligible for the Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee (RFHBG) and/or Family Home Guarantee (FHG), they are not eligible under the First Home Buyer Guarantee (FHBG)
  • where a borrower is eligible for both the RFHBG and FHG, the participating lender may determine the Guarantee to be applied

In the event it is found that a borrower(s) were eligible for the RFHBG or FHG, and a FHBG Guarantee was issued, this will impact the validity of the FHBG Guarantee. 

Further support

For more information, check the Resources tab of the NAB Broker Portal, or refer to the New Housing Finance and Investment Corporation’s website, opens in a new window .

30 June 2021 - Family Home Guarantees available at NAB from 1 July

From Thursday 1 July 2021, 10,000 Family Home Guarantees (FHG or Scheme) will be available to eligible single parents with dependants and NAB is once again proud to be part of this Government scheme.

Your customers can use the Family Home Guarantee to build a new home or buy an existing home with a deposit of as little as 2%. Both first home buyers and previous homeowners can apply to purchase an owner occupied property.

Important information

  • Applicants must be single with at least one dependent.
  • 2% deposit must be a ‘genuine contribution’ as per NAB credit policy.
  • There are no restrictions on prior home ownership history, but applicants can’t currently own a home.
  • Property must be the buyer’s primary place of residence.
  • Limited to applicants with income of $125,000 or less in the previous financial year.

Note: while the National Housing and Finance Investment Corporation (NHFIC) can support customers with a minimum 2% deposit, any upfront costs or deposit requirements set out by the vendor must be complied with and provided by the customer. Neither NAB nor NHFIC will cover these upfront costs (including stamp duty, conveyancing, etc.). 

For information on how to apply for the Family Home Guarantee, please see “Applying for Conditional Approval and Reserving a Scheme Place”
