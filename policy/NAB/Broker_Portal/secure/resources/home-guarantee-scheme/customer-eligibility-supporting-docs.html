Eligibility criteria

Australian citizen or permanent resident* aged 18 years or above

*Permanent residents only eligible for FY25/24 scheme places

Detailed criteria and evidence required

To verify and evidence Australian citizenship and age, use at least one of the following identity documents:

  • Australian passport (Must be current); OR
  • Australian birth certificate issued by Births, Deaths, & Marriages (BDM); OR
  • Citizenship certificate (and another identity document with date of birth e.g., drivers licence).

Note:

  • IMPORTANT: Australian Passport/Citizenship copies must be done either in person and noted in the application OR be certified true copies or third parties such as VOI
  • No other document type is acceptable to evidence Australian citizenship. If using IDyou, VOI document can be utilised to evidence citizenship ensuring at least one of the above acceptable document types are provided and verified. If you have used any of the above documents for VOI, you will not need to obtain a separate certified true copy document to evidence eligibility.

To verify and evidence permanent residency and age, use at least one of the following identity documents (FY25/24 scheme places only):

  • Foreign passport + VEVO check, OR
  • Australian Government ImmiCard + VEVO check.

NEW for FY25: New Zealand Citizens who hold a Special Category Visa (SCV) 444 are considered to be ‘Australian Permanent Residents’.

Note: in the event that the customer is unable to provide a foreign passport or Australian Government ImmiCard to prove permanent residency, a current 'Titre de Voyage' passport (plus VEVO check) is acceptable. No other visa type is acceptable.

Attach to the application for each applicant under ‘Evidence of Citizenship/Customer Identification’.

Note: VOI may be completed with alternate identity documents, but at least one of the identity document scenarios listed above must be attached to your customer’s application.

Home Buyer Declaration

Detailed criteria and evidence required

Ensure your customer(s) have a copy of the Completing the Home Buyer Declaration, opens in new window guide to ensure information declared on the Home Buyer Declaration is aligned with Scheme requirements.

  • First Home Guarantee Home Buyer Declaration, opens in new window

Mandatory sections: Part A (Pages 6, 7), Part C (Page 10), Part E (Pages 12, 13)

Helpful tips:

  • For a single (one) applicant, any questions that relate to providing a joint applicants name can be left blank ensuring the question advises 'if applicable'.
  • For joint applications, the Home Buyer Declaration must be completed separately for each applicant.
  • Utilise the Lender Checklist – Housing Australia Home Buyer Declaration to compare customer(s) declaration and ensure information declared by the customer(s) is aligned with Scheme requirements.
  • Furthermore, the survey questions at the end of the Home Buyer Declaration are also not required to be attached.

Important:

  • The declaration must be completed and signed, by all customers and a witness in accordance with Commonwealth Statutory Declaration Guidelines, where signatures must be a wet signature (not electronically signed) and declarations witnessed-in person (not via zoom/video conference).
  • The declaration must be completed within 100 days of when unconditional approval is provided to ensure currency of the declaration.
  • Witnesses must include their full qualification with no abbreviations. This must include years of continuous service if noted in the ‘Acceptable Witness’ list on page 14-15 of the Home Buyer Declaration Form. Refer to additional notes below for witness discretion.
  • Where a qualification/occupation includes years of service, the witness must include either a) the number of years continuous service (e.g., 7 years continuous service); or b) the statement in the witness list (e.g., with 5 or more years of continuous service). Refer to additional notes below for witness discretion.
  • Where required, the full address must be included (with suburb, post code and state are required).

Notes for the FY25 Home Buyer Declaration:

  • The declaration should typically be provided in its entirety however it is not mandatory. Ensure, at minimum, that the pages noted above are provided for the appropriate scheme the customer(s) are enrolled under.
  • This declaration must be used for all Guarantee Certificates generated from the 1st September 2024 onwards.
  • This declaration can be used for previous FY Scheme places i.e., the FY25 Home Buyer Declaration can be used for an FY24 Scheme place however we cannot accept an FY23 Home Byer Declaration for an FY25 Scheme place.
  • For Part A Question 2: 'I have not previously used any name other than the name(s) declared above except for', should be completed (if there are any previous names) or alternatively, filled in with 'N/A' or 'No other previous names' if they do not have any other previous name. However, if the applicant has not answered the question, a discretion by Credit Assessment may be provided on a case-by-case basis depending on how urgent finance/settlement is.
  • For Part A Question 4: 'I am: An Australian Citizen / A Permanent Resident', typically, the applicant needs to declare ‘True’ for one option and ‘False’ for the other option as applicants cannot be both an Australian citizen and permanent resident at the same time. However as long as the applicant has declared ‘True’ for at least one of the options, either Australian citizen or permanent resident, you can accept the declaration even if they have selected ‘True’ for the other option, i.e., if they have selected ‘True’ for Australian citizen and ‘True’ for permanent resident - this is acceptable.
  • Customers can type their responses or use a pen. They should not use a pencil because information could be erased. The declaration must be signed in front of the approved witness with a pen.
  • All fields must be completed correctly including dates, e.g., dates must not be ‘TBC’ or in MM/YYYY format - they must be DD/MM/YYYY and must align with the Housing Australia date requirements for each guarantee type (e.g. move in within 6 months if an existing property or if building a property through the New Home Guarantee, construction must start within 12 months for FY22 places).

To avoid rework/delay, credit assessors may use their discretion to accept the following:

  • If the witness is required to provide years of continuous service, but no years of continuous service have been provided, discretion may be used to and proceed without the years.
  • If the witness has abbreviated their occupation/qualification, the credit assessor may accept it provided the abbreviation is well-known (e.g., 'GP' is acceptable for 'General Practitioner' under the witness category 'Medical Professional', while 'Com Dec' or 'C. Dec' are acceptable for 'Commissioner of Declarations').

Detailed criteria and evidence required

If these criteria will not be met, the purchase is ineligible under the Home Guarantee Scheme.

Construction loan contracts must satisfy the following dates:​​​​​​​

  • Contracts for ‘House and Land packages’, ‘Land and separate contract to build home’ and ‘off-the-plan’ purchases may be dated prior to or after 1 January 2020.
  • Construction must start within 12 months of the contract agreement date; AND
  • Construction must be completed within 24 months of the construction commencement date.​​​​​​​​​​​​​
  • Building contracts must be provided by a licensed builder/developer.
  • Land and/or build contracts must not have previously settled. Customers cannot purchase and settle land with another institution, then refinance the land to NAB and take out an HGS home loan.
  • For land and build scenarios, both land and build contracts must be held by the customer and included in the application prior to issuing unconditional approval.
  • Variations that are not essential for the construction of a modest home are not allowed to be funded via the Housing Australia Guarantee.
  • ​​​​​​​Housing Australia deem any variations to only include essential necessities; examples of essential necessities can include air-conditioning, or a driveway should the original builder not be able to install/deliver these items whereas non-essential variations include pools or sheds, which go against Housing Australia's intent of the scheme which is to enable customers to get into a modest home quicker using the Government Guarantee.

Note: all out of build contract/quote variations (separate to the building contract) may be accepted as long as the variation contract/quote is included within the valuation for the overall build. Ensuring that this is met, we can evidence intent to Housing Australia that the variation works will take place which is considered acceptable under the Scheme rules for construction deals.

Eligibility criteria

Maximum Deposit Utilised and Explanation of Retained Savings

Detailed criteria and evidence required

Ensure applicant(s) have used the maximum amount of their savings as a deposit.

  • It is suggested that settlement related costs and a small but reasonable amount could be retained, while ensuring the integrity and principles of the scheme are upheld. For construction loans only, HGS participating lenders could allow for this ‘small but reasonable amount’ to be higher than a non-construction loan.
  • It is expected that clients use the majority of their savings with the deposit and are not to retain large surplus cash assets for the purpose of maximising the LVR and Guarantee.
  • For construction loans only, HGS participating lenders could allow for this ‘small but reasonable amount’ to be higher than a non-construction loan.

If customers have less than 20% cash savings of property purchase evidenced and are retaining some savings for their new purchase for moving costs, furniture and/or personal reasons, please ensure this is documented.

NOTE:

  • Housing Australia consider $30k as a reasonable amount of savings to keep aside for furniture, moving costs etc,. A modest amount of cash reserves can be held back to assist with additional costs such as: moving costs, reasonable furnishings, special needs modifications to the property, etc
  • For example, for someone with $120K cash assets, it may be reasonable for a client to put $100K as a deposit and withhold $20K for 'reasonable costs'.

It would not typically be acceptable to put $20K as a deposit and withhold $100K for 'reasonable costs', unless solid justification was provided.

Eligible Dependent Child

Detailed criteria and evidence required

Eligible dependent child (or children) criteria:

  • Has not turned 16 or
  • Aged between 16 and 22 who is wholly or substantially dependant on the applicant or
  • For FY24/FY25, aged 22 and over who is wholly or substantially dependent on the applicant due to being permanently disabled.

  • Dependent’s birth certificate
  • Dependent’s adoption certificate
  • Consent or parenting order issued by the Family Court of Australia

  • Centrelink documentation addressed to the applicant showing they are receiving a benefit for a dependent or that details the care arrangement for the dependent (this should be paired with evidence of the dependents age should the documentation not have that detail).​​​​​​​

Important: documentation issued by foreign jurisdictions (not in English) must be accompanied by a translation conducted by a National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) locally accredited translator.   • The declaration should typically be provided in its entirety however it is not mandatory. Ensure, at minimum, that the pages noted above are provided for the appropriate scheme the customer(s) are enrolled under.
  • This declaration must be used for all Guarantee Certificates generated from the 1st of September 2023 onwards.
  • This declaration can be used for previous FY Scheme places
  • For Part A Question 2: 'I have not previously used any name other than the name(s) declared above except for', should be completed (if there are any previous names) or alternatively, filled in with 'N/A' or 'No other previous names' if they do not have any other previous name.   • For Part A Question 4: 'I am: An Australian Citizen / A Permanent Resident', typically, the applicant needs to declare ‘True’ for one option and ‘False’ for the other option as applicants cannot be both an Australian citizen and permanent resident at the same time. However as long as the applicant has declared ‘True’ for at least one of the options, either Australian citizen or permanent resident, you can accept the declaration even if they have selected ‘True’ for the other option, i.e., if they have selected ‘True’ for Australian citizen and ‘True’ for permanent resident - this is acceptable.
