Before you can reserve a scheme place for your customer, you’ll need to put through a home loan application and obtain a conditional approval status.

You can watch this video tutorial to better understand how to process an ApplyOnline application where the borrowers are applying under the Home Guarantee Scheme with participating lenders, Video Tutorial for HGS applications, opens in a new window .

Mandatory documents for Approval in Principal (AIP):

  1. Personal Consent Form.
  2. Evidence of Australian citizenship or permanent residency and aged 18 or over. (IMPORTANT: Copies of Citizenship/Age must be done either in person and noted in the application OR be certified true copies or documents attached as part of VOI)
  3. Medicare card (digital copies acceptable).
  4. FY24 Notice of Assessment.

Once your customer(s) have purchased their new home and signed a Contract of Sale and/or building contract, please upload a copy of the documents into the application.

Pre-approval process

  1. Citizenship & age evidence: obtain copy of your customer(s)’ relevant ID document(s) to evidence Australian citizenship & age/permanent residency & age, and upload to ‘Supporting Docs’ tab in ApplyOnline under ‘Evidence of Citizenship/Customer Identification’. (IMPORTANT: Copies of Citizenship/Age must be done either in person and noted in the application OR be certified true copies via notary or third parties such as VOI)
    • Important for permanent residency: obtain reasonable confirmation of Australian permanent residency from your customer at AIP stage as the final VEVO check is only done at unconditional approval. If the Credit Assessment team find no eligible permanent residency OR they are not a New Zealand Special Category Visa holder (SCV 444) visa on the applicant(s) foreign passport or ImmiCard, the customer will not be allowed onto the schemes and thus at risk of losing their deposit/purchase.
    • An easy way to confirm your applicant(s) Australian permanent residency status, is to request your customer(s) provide their Visa Grant Notification Letter. In the Visa Grant Notification Letter, it will state the Visa Subclass as well as Visa Type.
      • Please ensure that a Visa Type that is classed as 'Permanent' OR is a ‘New Zealand special category visa holder’ for New Zealand Citizens is noted on the Visa Grant Notification Letter.
      • You can also see the Department of Immigration's website to search up their respective Visa Sub-Class numbers or request your customer(s) to visit Proof of permanent residency (homeaffairs.gov.au) to prove their Australian permanent residency status.
  2. ‘Maximum Deposit Utilised’ and ‘Explanation of Retained Savings’: ensure that you discuss with your customer(s) that they must utilise their maximum deposit amount when purchasing their new property and be able to provide an explanation for any modest amount of retained savings left over after using their maximum deposit amount (for detailed eligibility, refer to >
    •  If your customer(s) have more than 20% deposit evidenced and are retaining savings for their new purchase and/or personal reasons, obtain further information to confirm their eligibility for the schemes.
    • Your customer(s) may not be eligible for the HGS schemes if a rational justification has not been noted for utilising only part of the 20% deposit that’s evidenced within the customer’s savings and/or, decide to input more of a deposit to where their total savings will be less than 20% of the property purchase price.
      NOTE:
      - Housing Australia consider $30k as a reasonable amount of savings to keep aside for furniture, moving costs etc,. A modest amount of cash reserves can be held back to assist with additional costs such as: moving costs, reasonable furnishings, special needs modifications to the property, etc.
      - For example, for someone with $120K cash assets, it may be reasonable for a client to put $100K as a deposit and withhold $20K for 'reasonable costs'.
      - It would not typically be acceptable to put $20K as a deposit and withhold $100K for 'reasonable costs', unless solid justification was provided.

      Important:       it is expected that clients use the majority of their savings with the deposit and are not to retain large surplus cash assets for the purpose of maximising the LVR and guarantee. If customers intend to put aside some savings for costs that may be considered more than 'reasonable', ensure a note is input for justification as to why applicant(s) are not utilising their maximum deposit/savings amount.
  3. FY24 ATO NOA: obtain a copy of your customer’s FY24 (ending 30 June 2024) ATO Notice of Assessment (NOA) OR evidence of non-lodgment (if the customer is not required to complete an income tax return, having earned less than $18,200) and attach in the ‘Supporting Docs’ tab in ApplyOnline with all TFNs masked (if applying as a couple, provide both customers’ ATO NOAs).
    •  Important: Estimates of FY24 NOA are no longer acceptable after 31 August 2024.
    • Please contact the Broker HGS Support team on hgsbrokersupport@nab.com.au if customer does not have a valid FY24 NOA due to the following reasons:
      1. Applicant(s) do not have a TFN; OR
      2. Applicant(s) were previously working overseas and obtained a TFN on/after 1 July 2024 and thus are not able to obtain an FY24 NOA.
  4. Medicare ID: obtain a copy of your customer(s)’ Medicare ID (digital copies are acceptable) and upload to ‘Supporting Docs’ tab in ApplyOnline under ‘HGS (First Home Loan Deposit Scheme) - Medicare Card’.
  5. Add a comment for the relevant scheme to the application detailing the following:

Family Home Guarantee (FHG)

OR

- If a non-first home buyer who has not owned property in the last 10 years: 'Enquiry made to confirm that applicant has not owned property in the last 10 years.'

OR

- If a non-first home buyer who has owned property in the last 10 year: 'Enquiry made to confirm that applicant has owned property in the last 10 years.'

Important for the FY25 Family Home Guarantee: if they currently own property, they must intend to not own that property(ies) upon settlement of the new HGS purchase.

Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee (RFHBG)

- If a first home buyer: 'Enquiry made to confirm that applicant is a first home buyer and has never owned property before.'

OR

- If a non-first home buyer who has not owned property in the last 10 years: 'Enquiry made to confirm that applicant is a non-first home buyer who has not owned property in the last 10 years.'

Note: if non-first home buyer who has owned property in the last 10 years, they are not eligible for the RFHBG schemes.

Enquiry/enquires made for scheme eligibility note

First Home Guarantee (FHBG) 

Ensure that the applicant(s) are not eligible for the Family Home or Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee schemes; (add/delete where appropriate):

- Enquiry made to determine that the applicant(s) are not eligible for the Family Home Guarantee as they are not a single parent/legal guardian and/or have an eligible dependent child and/or are married/de facto or applying as a joint application.

AND

- Enquiry made to determine that the applicant(s) are not eligible for the Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee scheme as they do not live in a regional area and/or have not lived in the regional area/adjoining regional area for at least 12 months and/or are purchasing a property that is not within a regional area.

Family Home Guarantee (FHG)

Ensure that enquiry is conducted to validate that the applicant:

- Has at least one eligible dependant and

 - Is an eligible single parent or legal guardian

  • If a single parent, please provide a comment that they are the single parent to an eligible dependent.
  • If a legal guardian, please provide a comment that they are the legal guardian to an eligible dependent.
Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee (RFHBG)

If single applicant:

Enquiry conducted to validate that the applicant:

- Has lived in an eligible regional/adjoining regional area for the preceding 12 months

OR

- Will have lived in the regional/adjoining regional area for the preceding 12 months prior to unconditional approval

OR

- Has a valid exemption due to relocation as part of their duties (ADF or employees).

If joint applicants: 

Enquiry conducted to validate that [applicant 1 OR applicant 2 OR both applicants *delete where applicable*]

- Has/have lived in an eligible regional/adjoining regional area for the preceding 12 months

OR

- Will have lived in the regional/adjoining regional area for the preceding 12 months prior to unconditional approval

OR

- Has a valid exemption due to relocation as part of their duties (ADF or employees).
