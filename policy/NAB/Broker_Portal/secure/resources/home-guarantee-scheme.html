The Home Guarantee Scheme (HGS) is a government-backed initiative to help Australians fast track their home ownership dreams.

FY25 Scheme Places available now through NAB:

NAB has uplifted evidence requirements for FY25 to ensure conformance to HGS Scheme Rules. These changes include:

Australian Passports: Must be current and valid if being utilised to evidence Australian Citizenship

Permanent Residency (NZ Citizens): New Zealand Citizens who hold a Special Category Visa (SCV) 444 are considered to be an ‘Australian Permanent Resident’ and therefore eligible for the scheme.

All Citizenship/Age documentation: Must be certified true copies and can be attested via:

  • Sighting the documents in person and provide an attestation in the applications notes; OR
  • Include these documents as part of the IDYou process (VOI); OR

Provide photocopies which are certified by an authorised person; e.g pharmacist etc,

Genuine Savings: Ensure post-settlement customer savings are comprehensively explained, especially if post-settlement savings are greater than $30,000. If customers have savings of 20% or more evidenced, they are not eligible for the schemes.

Post-Settlement Address:

HGS rules state that customers must move into the property within the timeframe set out by Housing Australia.

Customers must update their address once they have purchased their property, by either:

  • Confirming it as the post-settlement address in the lending application; or
  • Letting NAB know once they’ve moved in

Note:

  • For existing purchases, the customer(s) must move in within 6 months.

For construction purchases, the customer(s) must move in once an occupancy certificate is issued.

Ongoing Scheme Eligibility: Customers must also be reminded that the HGS secured property must not be rented out as per the conditions of the scheme otherwise the Government Guarantee will be void, meaning they will be liable for any shortfall should they find themselves in a default position.

 

Evidencing income eligibility for FY25 Scheme Places:

The below provides a high-level view of key eligibility criteria per scheme, however for the complete list, refer to the Customer Eligibility page.

First Home Guarantee (FHBG), opens in a new window  for single or joint applicants buying their home. Applicants:

  • Must be first home buyers or be non-first home buyers who have not owned a property in the past 10 years.
  • Must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident (all applicants) and aged 18 or over.
  • Can apply as an individual or joint applicants (friends, siblings, and other family members).
  • May buy or build a property of any types (the property can be new or established).

Family Home Guarantee (FHG), opens in a new window  for single parents or legal guardians, buying their new home or re-entering the property market. Applicants:

  • May have owned a home, but must not currently own residential land or property.
  • Must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident (all applicants) and aged 18 or over.
  • Must apply as an individual applicant and be single, divorced or widowed (separated, de facto or married are not eligible).
  • May buy or build a property of any types (the property can be new or established).

Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee, opens in a new window (RFHBG) for single or joint applicants buying their new home. Applicants:

  • Must be first home buyers or be non-first home buyers who have not owned a property in the past 10 years.
  • Must be an Australian citizen or permanent resident (all applicants) and aged 18 or over.
  • Can apply as an individual or joint applicants (friends, siblings, and other family members).
  • Must be living and have lived in an acceptable regional area for at least 12 months (if joint then at least one applicant must fulfil this condition).
  • May buy or build a property of any types (the property can be new or established) within their current or adjoining regional areas.
