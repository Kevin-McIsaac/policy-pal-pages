Loan to Valuation Ratios

Why

Home Loans are affordable because the value of the security held exceeds the amount of the loan.

What

The amount of the loan relative to the security value, expressed as a percentage, is referred to as the Loan to Valuation (LVR) ratio.

Maximum LVRs are set for security types, depending on location, loan types or loan amount.

Maximum LVR by Loan Purpose and Loan Type Maximum LVR and Loan Amount by Location

The maximum LVR for loans that are ineligible for Lenders Mortgage Insurance is 80%.

Loans are not provided to non-residents apart from Australian citizens that are temporarily living overseas.

The maximum LVR for non-resident Australian citizens temporarily living overseas OR residents that do not have a permanent right to reside in Australia is 70% (see Lending to Non-Residents for more detail).
