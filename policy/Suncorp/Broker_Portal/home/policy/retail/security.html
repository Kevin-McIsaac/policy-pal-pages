Security

Why

Home Loans are affordable because they are backed by security, typically residential property.

What

It is preferred that all lending be supported by a first registered security interest (or documented priority agreements to achieve a first ranking position) over Acceptable Security.

Other Security may still be considered but will be subject to a maximum LVR of 80% as Lenders Mortgage Insurance is unavailable for loans supported by these types of security. Refer to your Business Development Manager (BDM) for guidance.

Securities that are not acceptable include:

  • Company or Stratum Title Units,
  • Commercial/ industrial/ rural enterprise property, and
  • Term Deposits with banks other than Suncorp Bank and all other forms of accounts, with Suncorp Bank or other institutions.

Note: Dual purpose (residential and business) are to be considered through Business Banking.

  • When >4 properties are offered as collateral and the predominant source of income is derived from rental income, applications are to be submitted through Business Banking as unregulated lending.

These purchases pose a greater than normal risk due to the construction time frames of up to 18 months or more, possible negative movements in property values, the potential for design changes during construction and changes to the customer’s circumstances. The structure of loans for off the plan purchases and other things you need to consider can be found here.

Off the Plan purchases are normally funded in two parts:

Part 1 - Bank Guarantee or loan to fund deposit

  • Cannot be secured against property being purchased.
  • Customers are to be advised that, by providing the guarantee/ funding, Suncorp Bank is not committing to provide end finance.

Note: Any pre-approval for the loan to complete the purchase (Part 2) cannot be given unless the property is to be completed and title issued within 90 days.

Refer to Borrowers & Guarantors for further details.

You should consider the customer’s capacity to repay any future debt and advise the customer that any future funding would be subject to a separate application and the requirements and conditions applicable at the time of any new loan application.

Part 2 - Loan to complete purchase

  • Credit Policy applicable at the time is to apply, including the provision of up to date documentation evidencing capacity to repay.
  • Pre-approval for the loan to complete the purchase cannot be given unless the property is to be completed and title is to be issued within 90 days.
  • As Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) cannot be obtained until the property and surrounding development is fully completed, you will need to consider the customer’s ability to provide additional equity.

The term ‘split contract’ refers to the sale of a property ‘off the plan’ whereby the land is sold and settled under a contract of sale and is subject to the purchaser entering into a building contract for construction of a dwelling with a designated builder. These arrangements present a greater risk and are not acceptable. Refer to further guidance on how to identify split contracts.

The following characteristics identify split contracts:

  • Typically found in a community title/ strata title/ plan of subdivision where an owners’ corporation exists (or is likely to exist in the future) with common property;
  • Have restrictive covenants in the land or building contract (or both) which may include restrictions on sale of the land, and/ or the construction/ occupation of the proposed improvements;
  • The land price is simply determined arithmetically by subtracting the build cost from the proposed sale price of the building. The residual amount forms the land contract sum which may not reflect market value at the time;
  • Common walls or common slab (but this is not always the case);
  • The land contract requires the purchaser to enter into a building contract with the vendor (or a builder nominated/ preferred by the vendor);
  • The land can only be sold or mortgaged with the developer’s consent.

The term ‘Turn-Key’ applies when a property is sold as a completed dwelling, however, is yet to be built. The purchaser will settle the purchase with one only payment of the full purchase price, and only when the property is completed and ready to be occupied. The following attributes usually apply to Turn-Key contracts:

  • The property being purchased has been contracted on an “on completion” basis, and
  • Will be fully completed prior to settlement, and
  • The purchase contract does not include the requirement for progress funding, and
  • The property will be a stand-alone dwelling and a separate title for the property will be issued prior to settlement.

The property will be fully completed with no requirement of the owner to complete items such as, landscaping, driveway, fencing, window treatments and floor coverings.

Turn-Key properties are considered as established dwellings.

Flood affected properties are considered on a case-by-case basis, having regard to the customer’s overall risk profile.

Flood affected properties will generally be accepted where the LVR does not exceed 70% and the valuer or local government search confirms the frequency of flooding is 1 in 50 years or longer (e.g. 1 in 100 years).

If a property is identified in a bush fire zone, the risk is to be investigated and mitigated. This could be achieved by conditioning the facility for evidence of appropriate insurance covering the full replacement value of the property.

These properties present as higher risk as they are subject to rules and regulations that surround their renovation and maintenance. Limits to work that may be done to a property, and the increased costs in maintaining the property to the required standard, has the potential to limit buyer interest.

Heritage listed residential properties are considered on a case-by-case basis, having regard to the customer’s overall risk profile, and additional collateral that may support the loan.

Your submission notes should identify and mitigate the nature of the contamination.

The Bank has a very low appetite for these properties. Only exceptional applications that involve an NRAS property will be considered. Refer to your Business Development Manager (BDM) for guidance.
