Income

Why

Verifying income is an important step that helps ensure the customer can afford the loan.

What

Income may only be included in a Capacity to Repay (CTR) calculation if it is:

  • earned by a borrower or financially interdependent guarantor,
  • meets the qualifying criteria, and
  • confirmed as reliable and ongoing for the foreseeable future.

The documents you will need to validate income is set out in the Submission Checklist and Definition Guide (PDF).

Common Questions

You must establish how commitments will be met during the period of parental leave without causing financial hardship and commentary is to be included in your submission notes (e.g. use of accumulated savings and/ or parental/ accrued leave arrangements).

Financial interdependence is defined as persons in a spousal (or equivalent) relationship AND co-habiting AND providing each other with financial or domestic support or personal care.

Where financial interdependence is evident the co-borrower or guarantor's income may be used to offset their portion of joint expenses and liabilities (see Borrowers & Guarantors)

Calculation of YTD and Secondary Income

We understand, when calculating income, the customer’s individual circumstances need to be considered. We recommend you use the YTD Income Calculator to assist you to determine the amount of annualised YTD and secondary income that can be used in the Capacity to Repay (CTR) calculation.

Permanent rental is defined as a house, unit/townhouse/apartment where part of the property serves as a full time rental, has a formal lease agreement, and an owner is eligible to deduct expenses related to the rental income for income tax purposes.

Short term/ holiday let is defined as properties which is rented out on a temporary basis e.g. short stay or holiday accommodation.

The treatment of permanent rentals and short term/holiday let properties is as follows: 

Income included in the CTR calculation

75% of gross income

Expenses

Reflected within the income shading and includes 5% for vacancies and 20% for expenses

Income validation requirements

Any of the following documents:

  • Recent Property Manager’s statement, or
  • Bank statement confirming consistent payments from the tenant or managing agent, covering at least 3 months to the date of application, or
  • Most recent years tax return and ATO Notices of Assessment, or
  • Suncorp Bank records, or
  • Signed rental tenancy agreement, or
  • Fair Market Rental from a full valuation report by a valuer by a Bank panel valuer, or
  • For new purchases, a fully executed Contract for Sale where the term and income is confirmed.

Where the property is not currently tenanted a rental appraisal or similar issued by a local real estate agent may be used where the intent is to rent the property.

Note: Negative/positive gearing is automatically calculated within the CTR. The gearing amount, which is apportioned to the borrower’s ownership percentage, is made up of:

  • 100% of the interest expense; and
  • Allowance for claimable rental expenses (based on 20% of the declared gross income).

General

Overtime, Allowances, Commission and Bonuses are classified as secondary income. Where source documents or other information indicates differing income levels, you should:

  • adopt the lowest income figure, or
  • justify why the lowest figure should not be taken and record the reasons in your submission including reasons for the discrepancies or differences.

You should always consider whether income which has been earned historically will be earned consistently going forward, when considering inclusion in capacity to repay calculations. Income used should be considered as reliable for the foreseeable future.

Where income is annualised you must pay special attention to potential variances caused by seasonal influences or non-recurring events.

Where source documents reveal a reduction in normally consistent payments due to a non- recurring event (e.g. unpaid leave), you may use the consistent payment subject to investigation and mitigants being recorded in your submission notes.

When including irregular or annual bonus income, you should consider whether the funds can be relied upon to meet monthly financial commitments and include commentary in your submission notes.

When assessing whether contract and/or temporary agency income is reliable and ongoing for the foreseeable future, you should consider such things as:

  • The duration and expiry date of the current employment contract and any history of contract renewal with the same employer.
  • The demand for the customer’s skills and qualifications, i.e. employability. For example, an engineer may be more likely to quickly obtain employment at a similar level of income with a different employer than an unskilled office worker.
  • The customer’s location and access to industry opportunities either locally, on a fly-in fly-out basis, or requiring permanent relocation. Where opportunities require travel or relocation, the customer’s willingness and ability to attend work site must be established.
  • The customer’s history of concurrent employment contracts with different employers at a similar level of income.

It is preferred that loan repayments are met by income derived in Australia.

Where income from overseas is relied upon, potential fluctuations in foreign exchange rates create risk. Consequently, not more than 70% of the AUD equivalent of income received on a consistent basis from overseas may be included in Capacity to Repay (CTR) calculations, subject to:

  • it being verified as personal income (e.g. salary/wage, pensions/superannuation) - foreign business income is not acceptable,
  • the income having been consistently received for at least six months,
  • currency being listed on the Bank’s current “SML Buys Inward TTs Rate” list (see Acceptable Currencies), and
  • the conversion to AUD being conducted by referring to the rate table applicable to the date of the CTR calculation.

Documentation is to evidence:

  • employment terms,
  • income amount and currency type,
  • payment frequency.

Documentation in foreign languages will not be accepted unless it is supported by a certified English language translation and the accuracy of the translated text can be confirmed by a Suncorp Bank employee.

Note: Loans are not provided to non-residents apart from Australian citizens that are temporarily living overseas (see Lending to Non-Residents for more detail).

Please refer to our website for a list of acceptable currencies.

Verification of a self-employed customers income can now be completed as either ‘Basic’, requiring less documentation but only eligible for certain business, loan structures and collateral, or ‘Comprehensive’ requiring full documentation but eligibility is not restricted by company or loan structures.

To submit a self-employed application using ‘Basic’ income verification, ALL the criteria must be met. For further information please discuss with your Suncorp Bank Business Development Manager.
 

‘Basic’ Validation Requirements

Criteria:

  • Customers are borrowing as individuals;
  • Borrowing purpose may be Owner Occupied or Investment;
  • Loan structure may be either P&I or IO;
  • LVR is ≤80%;
  • ≤3 collateral properties where cross collateralised to the new lending;
  • Business trading for ≥2 years (to be confirmed by ACN/ABN search);
  • The maximum decrease in income is ≤ 20% compared to the prior year.

Note: Existing rental income, which is part of Taxable Income on the Notice of Assessment, should not be recorded as a separate income source (applicable to that borrower/s share of ownership).

Income documents:

  • 2 years’ most recent ATO Notice of Assessments (NOA)

Calculation of Self-employed Income – ‘Basic’ Option

Income included in the capacity to repay calculations is to be based on the:

  • Average of the income taken from the last 2 consecutive years’ NOA where the most recent year is higher; or
  • Lower amount where the most recent years’ income taken from the NOA is lower than the prior year.

Note: Any income captured in NOA cannot be separately input into CTR (e.g. rent or share income) and any capital gains income must be excluded from servicing calculations.

‘Comprehensive’ Validation Requirements:

For customers who do not meet the ‘Basic’ criteria, all of the following is required.

Income documents:

  • Accountant prepared tax returns and financial statements for the most recent financial years
  • If the most recent* financial statements are older than 18 months, data is to be validated by obtaining interim management accounts.

Note: Where tax returns are not prepared by an Accountant, then the ATO Notice of Assessment is to be provided, where applicable for individuals.

Supporting comments and additional information:

  • Business/financial information - A brief background of the customer’s business and details of financial analysis you have completed.
  • Capacity to Repay - Include all business debts in capacity calculations. 

This can be identified by:

  • Review the liabilities in the balance sheet and include associated repayments for loans that will continue. 
  • Include repayments on any new loans drawn after the balance sheet date. 
  • Consider the following when recording repayment details in capacity to repay calculations, updated loan balances, interest rate, remaining loan term, and current repayments.  

Business account statements - minimum 1 month business transaction statement statement to be provided when any of the following applies to the loan application LVR >80%, DTI >5, when a customer has a new credit report, where the primary purpose is a debt consolidation or when third party/ Guaranteed collateral is being offered as security. 
 

Calculation of Self-employed Income – ‘Comprehensive’ Option

A “Cash from Operations” calculation is used for self-employed customers and is set out below. This calculation is a common accounting practice that converts accrual based financial statements to cash equivalents, showing the cash flow in a clear and concise manner.

Cash from Operations may be included in Capacity to Repay (CTR) calculations where the customer has held ownership in the business for at least 2 years. Cash from Operations for CTR purposes is to be averaged over at least the last two consecutive years’ financial records.

The documents you will need to validate self-employed income is set out in the Submission Checklist and Definition Guide.

Gross Profit
+ other business income
- business expenses
+/- adjustments for abnormal income/expense
= Net Profit Before Tax
+ interest expense
+ financed leasing expense
+ depreciation/amortisation of intangibles
+ salaries/wages paid to owner*
= Cash from Operations

*Salaries and wages paid to owner may only be added when the owner has provided their personal guarantee or is a borrower.
