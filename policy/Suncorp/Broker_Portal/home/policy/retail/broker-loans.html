Broker Authority - Existing Loans

We have improved our processes to allow you to access information on behalf of your customer. There is a Broker Authority section in the Apply Online application form for new lending, which must be signed by the customer to authorise access.

To access information on behalf of your customer for existing loans, the Broker Authority Existing Loan form will need to be signed, completed and returned. The following guidelines have been provided to assist you in making an enquiry about a customer’s loan.

The types of enquires that can now be made by you as an agent, fall into two categories;

During application, which includes a settlement period. 

Until the expiry of the loan agreement/s that are established as an outcome of the application, or until you cease to operate as an accredited representative of the Broker Group, whichever event occurs first. This type of authority is covered by the Option to Extend authority mentioned further below.

Download the authority form

Common Questions

The top section of the Broker Authority (included in the Apply Online application form) will provide the required authority for the during application type enquiries, and in all instances the Broker Authority must be signed by all customers to allow you to access this relevant information.

There is then also the option for the customer to elect the “additional authorities” by ticking the below options within the Broker Authority.

1a. Option to extend:

This extends the Broker Authority until the expiry of the loan agreement/s that are established as an outcome of this application or until you cease to operate as an accredited representative of the Broker Group, whichever event occurs first. This provides an extended authority which enables you to;

  • facilitate a request to vary a loan agreement;
  • facilitate a request for a further application for finance;
  • facilitate a Direct Debit Request for a loan;
  • enquire on the progress of loan disbursements for construction loans; and
  • request information about my/our loan/s and any associated accounts that may be required to complete the variation request. This request for information does not include borrower or guarantor contact details.

Please note:

  • any requests that require signed authority will still be required to be executed by all borrowers and guarantors (where applicable) prior to being processed;
  • the Broker Authority does not extend to the Broker updating contact details;
  • Suncorp Bank can cancel or refuse to accept instructions from a Broker appointed by this Broker Authority at any time where it is necessary to comply with the law or any applicable Code or to protect our Legitimate Interests or if we reasonably suspect the Broker is not acting or may not act in your best interests;
  • The borrower/s can cancel this authorisation by notice in writing to the Bank at any time by completing the Broker Authority Existing Loan form.

1b. Document dispatch – Loan Documents:

This provides an extended authority which allows you to automatically receive a copy of the borrower’s loan documents. The applicant/s copy (borrower/guarantor) will still be required to be sent to the customer, so a separate instruction in the brokers commentary should be made around how these are to be dispatched.

Note: We are unable to provide a copy of guarantor documents. 

  • Email: At this stage the application form does not support emailing the loan document to the customer, so the separate Authority to Email Loan Documents forms would still be required to be executed for the applicants
  • Standard Mail: No further authority from the customer is required.

1c. Document dispatch – Settlement Letter:

Also allows you to receive a copy of the settlement letter (on request only). This will not be automatically distributed. Please note the customer can request to cancel the Broker Authority at any time, by completing the Broker Authority Existing loan/s form.

It’s important that you proactively seek the relevant information directly from your customers and by reviewing the documentation available (loan contract, statements etc), prior to contacting Suncorp Bank. Understanding the customers’ requirements and objectives is part of your responsible lending obligations and must be completed prior to requesting a loan variation or further application for finance.

Enquiries which would reasonably relate to the home loan application and settlement period (three months after the loan settlement date or the first construction progress draw down)

  • progression and status of the loan application, including changes to the structure of the facility
  • valuation delays and/or outcomes
  • discussion around enquiries from Suncorp Bank for further information
  • discussion regarding an approval outcome
  • checking on loan direct debit instructions
  • checking on Suncorp Bank internal transfers (to a loan)
  • verifying where surplus funds have been credited too, if this cannot be located
  • initiating a request to correct any errors made as part of settlement

You will be required to provide your accreditation details, as disclosed on the application form

  • Broker Name, Broker Accreditation Code, Broker Group and Broker Group Code

Please complete the Broker Authority Existing loan form

The form should be emailed to the Suncorp Bank internal processing team,  CustomerandBrokerSupport@suncorpbank.com.au . Please allow up to three working days for the request to be processed and the customer/s profiles to be updated prior to contacting your dedicated Broker Support Team. The form can be lodged at the same time as a request for a variation. 

Once your customer has provided the extended authority via the Broker Authority Existing loan form or within a recently settled loan application, you can now request the Loan Variation Form to be emailed to you directly. To facilitate this, we will check the correct authority is held and validate your accreditation details. Please supply the below details within the email and ensure they can be quickly located.

  • Status Withdrawn: The application cannot be discussed

Example 1:

When an application is made, the broker (Broker A) can be nominated as an authority on accounts that settle as part of that application. If the broker wants authority on accounts that the applicant already holds, they would also need to complete the Broker Authority Existing loan, form, noting the existing account numbers

If the customer originates a subsequent application with a new broker (Broker B), this broker can be nominated on accounts that settle as part of this application, however Broker A would still have authority on the prior account/s supplied.

If the customer then completes a request to “cancel” a Broker Authority, this will cancel Broker A and Broker B authorities.

Example 2:

If an aggregator wanted to have multiple brokers listed as an authority on a customer’s profile, then the aggregator can request the customer provide authority by completing the Broker Authority – existing loan/s form and listing multiple brokers. The authority will only be valid for accounts that are currently held, so this may need to be updated as the customer extends their Suncorp Bank lending.
