Foreign PAYG Base - full time and permanent part time

 

 

 

Restrictions
 Verification
 % used for servicing
None

Employment contract or letter from employer and both of the following:

  • Three months' salary credits to an account and
  • One acceptable payslip


Note:

  • The letter from the employer or payslip must include current base salary/wage information.
  • In addition to the above, where the customer resides in a different country to the account where their foreign currency income is being paid into, also obtain the most recent three months bank statement/s from the country of residence. Review the statement/s to confirm the source of funds used to cover the customer’s living expenses is from their foreign currency income. Ensure the account conduct is satisfactory and there are no undisclosed commitments during the review period.
  • If the bank statements does not show transfers from the customer’s overseas account or the main source of funds use to cover their living expenses is not from their foreign currency income, leave comments with an explanation for Credit Decisioning to consider.


Bank statements must confirm all of the following:

  • Amount
  • Account name
  • Account number
  • Description of the credit, e/g/ salary, wage, pay or employer's name
  • Date range
  • Financial insitution details.

Note:
  • Bank statements must be from the financial institution where the salary is credited and not a third party source.
  • An old bank statement can be provided to verify ownership of account.

80% for Gold and Silver currencies

70% for all other currencies
Calculations
Step
 Action
1
 Review the bank account statements to confirm regular salary credits from their employer.
2
 Use the base salary/wage and multiply by actual frequency of receipt (e.g. weekly, fortnightly).
The YTD amount is not to be used for calculation purposes.
3
 Convert the foreign currency base salary/wage income to Australian dollars using the EX Currency Converter.
4
 Print a copy of the foreign currency calculation showing the foreign currency amount, exchange rate and date. This must be included with your supporting documents.
  Enter as the converted income as calculated in step 3 as “Salary/Wages"

Other/Special Notes

  • Any part time arrangement must be taken into account, (e.g. working only 4 days a week).
  • The currency stated on the employment contract will determine the currency that is used for converting to Australian dollars. Where the currency on the employment contract is different from the payslip and/or salary credits ensure the converted amount from the payslip is in line with the amount stated on the employment contract.
  • See Foreign Currency Income for translation requirements if original document is in a foreign language.
