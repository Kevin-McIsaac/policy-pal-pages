Off The Plan

Back to Home Loan Products

 

Description

Off the plan purchases are best described as a 'buy now and pay later' method of acquiring a residential property. A potential home buyer can purchase a property that has not yet been built.

These customers exchange contracts for a property from the description in the developer's plan. Generally, buyers are required to pay a 10% deposit upon exchange of contracts with the balance to be paid upon completion of the property which can take up to 18 months.

 

Features

The application should be treated as a home seeker until a satisfactory on completion valuation has been received and the customers financial details have been re-verified within 90 days prior to settlement.

 

Loan options

  • Standard Variable Rate
  • Extra Variable Rate 

Note: Off the plan purchase is not available for guarantor's support. This restriction does not apply in the case of marital / de facto relationships

 

Conditions
 

  • Loan approval is conditional based on the following being obtained, as a minimum:
    • A satisfactory 'on completion' valuation is conducted 90 days prior to settlement. Note: A 'final inspection' is not required prior to settlement.
    • Re-verification of the customer's financial details.
  • If there are any changes to the security position and/or the repayment capacity, the approval may be withdrawn.
  • When valuing 'off the plan' a valuer must:
    • note the off the plan purchase price and sale date.
    • indicate whether they have sighted the contract of sale and special conditions.
    • indicate the anticipated completion date of the development.
  • If not complete at the time of inspection the valuer must:
    • value the property 'as if complete' and ensure the report clearly indicates this detail.
    • provide a detailed description of outstanding works
  • For applications involving LMI, approval is extended to 6 months only. LMI approval lapses after the 6 months. After this time a new application must be completed.
  • The loan cannot be switched to another loan type until it has been funded.
