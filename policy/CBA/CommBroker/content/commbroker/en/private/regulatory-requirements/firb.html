Under Australiaâ€™s foreign investment framework, foreign persons generally need to apply for foreign investment approval before purchasing residential real estate in Australia

 FIRB approval are required to be obtained before they can acquire an interest in residential real estate. (Refer Eligible Borrowers  for exemption & http://firb.gov.au/ for current requirements)

An interest in residential real estate can include:

  • signing an unconditional contract agreeing to purchase a property;
  • signing an unconditional contract agreeing to purchase a share of a dwelling (such as joint tenants or tenants in common with any number of other people)

What you need know

  • If any one of the Home Loan applicants are not a Permanent Resident, then Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB) approval may be required prior to loan approval
  • For Specified Temporary Resident & Overseas citizens refer to Eligible Borrowers for eligibility

The FIRB approval may vary for specified temporary residents and overseas citizen. FIRB Condition may prohibit customer from buying a new dwelling/vacant residential land or a second hand or established dwelling

Specified Temporary residents may apply to purchase one established dwelling to use as a residence while they live in Australia however they may be restricted by FIRB to acquire established dwellings to rent out or for use as a holiday home.

When a FIRB approval is provided, ensure the following information in the FIRB approval is aligned to the application:

  • Applicant's Name
  • Security address / Property being purchased
  • Loan purpose (e.g. investment versus owner occupied)
  • Property classification / sub classification (e.g. new dwelling or established dwelling or vacant land)
  • Any other restrictions / conditions applied by the FIRB

If FIRB Exemption Certificate is provided, ensure that the name on the Exemption certificate is the same at the applicants.
