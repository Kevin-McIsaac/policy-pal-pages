Regulatory Requirements

Financial Abuse

Regulatory Requirements

Take care when dealing with customers vulnerable to financial abuse. Financial abuse can take a variety of different forms within different relationships, including intimate partners, family members or carers.
 

What is financial abuse?

Financial abuse is when a person (it could be a partner, a family member or a friend) improperly controls your decisions, access to your money or property without your consent. Anyone could be subject to financial abuse, vulnerable circumstances, or be taken advantage of. This can affect a customer both financially and psychologically. In order to protect the customer, you should always be proactive in looking for any warning signs.

Potential signs of financial abuse

The following scenarios are common examples where a customer may show signs that they are vulnerable to financial abuse:

  • Appears to be coerced into applying for a loan by a family member, friend or other person
  • Remains silent while another party does all the talking
  • Appears withdrawn and fearful (particularly of the person accompanying them)
  • Gives implausible explanations or is confused about what they are doing with their money
  • Expresses concern about missing funds, or personal or financial documents
  • A customer contacts you to discuss risks and their responsibilities of a loan after loan documents have been signed and advises they were coerced into applying for the loan
  • During discussions one borrower advises they are going through a separation with the joint applicant, and doesn’t understand their risk or responsibilities
  • A customer who is unaware of the other applicant’s debt level and seems nervous and shocked when this is identified during a loan application
  • A customer who speaks no/limited English, is being asked to be a Co-Borrower on a loan and doesn’t understand their risk or responsibilities
  • A customer who becomes agitated when you advise that you must speak to all borrowers/account holders
  • A customer receives no benefit by being on the loan and is unaware of the risks involved
  • A third party is speaking on behalf of another customer who is quiet during the interview
  • A customer is confused during the application or does not understand what they are being asked to do
  • A customers language/body language changes when not in the company of the Co-Borrower
  • A customer has no access to their banking, or their money is controlled by a third party.

 

Guidelines to identify and assist customers vulnerable to financial abuse.

Follow the procedure below which outlines probing questions and actions to take when you feel that your customer may be vulnerable to financial abuse or being taken advantage of by a third party for financial gain or benefit.

We have a simple e-learning module that outlines how to better identify and care for customers who are potentially vulnerable or experiencing financial abuse. If you would like to complete this short e-learning module, please email: thirdpartylearning@cba.com.au

 

Process to hide previously issued joint statements

For any joint Home loan account, statements issued prior to 19th June 2021 will be visible to both borrowers on Netbank and/or the Commbank App.

A customer may request to have their own statements for joint accounts hidden from other account holders.

If the customer wishes to hide a past statement, please email your Relationship Manager with the subject line “Request for statement to be hidden” and include the below information:

  • Loan account number
  • Customer name
  • Loan product type
  • Statement dates requesting to be hidden
