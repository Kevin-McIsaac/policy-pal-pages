Spam Act

Back to Regulatory Requirements

Under the Spam Act, it is illegal to send, or cause to be sent, 'unsolicited commercial electronic messages' that have an Australian link. A message has an Australian link if it originates or was commissioned in Australia, or originates overseas but has been sent to an address accessed in Australia. The legislation sets out penalties of up to $1.1 million a day for repeat corporate offenders. if you are intending to send any material to customers by email or SMS that could be construed as an offer to supply or advertise goods or services or a supplier of such or advertise or promote a business opportunity then you need to comply with the Spam Act.

 

To comply, you will need the consent of the individual to whom you are communicating to receive the electronic message; you will need to include your identity in the message including name and contact phone number; you will need to provide a functioning unsubscribe facility.
