1. Overview

The Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006 (AML/CTF Act) imposes certain obligations on reporting entities. A "reporting entity" is a person or entity that provides any "designated service" stated in the AML/CTF Act. “Designated services” include most products and services offered by the Commonwealth Bank.

The AML/CTF Act requires each reporting entity to adopt and maintain an Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Program that meets the requirements of the Act, Regulations and Rules. A breach of the AML/CTF Act and Rules may attract civil and criminal penalties.

As Brokers for the Commonwealth Bank, third party brokers (including mortgage brokers) are obliged to undertake the following:

  • identify all customers receiving a "designated service" (refer "Customer Identification")
  • Forward (or retain where instructed) copies of any and all customer documentation to the Commonwealth Bank
  • maintaining an Employee Due Diligence Programme (EDD) and perform due diligence on applicable employees of the third party
  • undergo AML/CTF awareness training via a AML/CTF programme approved by us
  • report any suspect transactions/matters connected with a product of the Commonwealth Bank with a customer who has previously taken a product with the Commonwealth Bank

The Commonwealth Bank will consider terminating the relationship with any third party agent that does not comply with these procedures.

 

2. Customer Identification

Under the AML/CTF Act 2006, the requirements for customer identification and verification vary by entity type.  The information that needs to be collected per entity type should already be stated in the application form that the customer needs to complete or in a separate client information form.

Customer identification process should be performed as part of a face to face personal meeting with the customer. Where verification is required all original ID documents must be presented by the customer and sighted by the broker.  Whilst "face to face" interviews will normally occur, it is acceptable for an interview to be conducted by video conference, if video conference is not available telephone may be used due to the remoteness or special circumstances of the applicant. In the event a face to face interview is not reasonably possible (e.g. due to remote locations), you can also accept copies of their identification documents that have been certified by a prescribed person. Alternatively, a Commonwealth Bank Officer is to identify the customer. Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander customers who do not have conventional identity documents should be referred to a Commonwealth Bank branch to be identified and verified.

For all new to bank customers who don’t have an existing CommBank product or service CBA will complete electronic verification (ECV).

CBA will process the customer’s details utilising ECV

  • If the customers passes ECV the application will progress for pre-assessment
  • If the customer fails ECV we will notify you to request the customer attend a CBA branch to complete identification
If the customer fails on the background check conducted against illion (a credit reporting body) they will receive an automatic email providing them with information on the result and how to check their records. Issuing the notification is a legal requirement which doesn’t leave a record on the customers file

 

2.1 Beneficial Owners

Brokers are required to determine the beneficial owner of all customers. A beneficial owner is defined as any individual who directly or indirectly owns or controls the customer (Refer to 3.2 Australian Domestic Companies, item 7). The tables below set out what is required to determine this for each customer type, the exceptions, and what steps must be taken to verify this information.

Please follow the identification procedures stated at 3.1 through to 3.3 on this page for the different entity types that we deal with through this channel.
