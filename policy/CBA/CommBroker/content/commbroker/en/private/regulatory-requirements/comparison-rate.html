Comparison Rate Legislation

Back to Regulatory Requirements

A 'Comparison Rate' is an attempt to express some of the costs of a loan into a single interest rate. These 'costs' include the nominal interest rate, some up-front fees and charges and ongoing loan service fees. It does not include fees that are unascertainable or event based fees and charges, for example Early Repayment Adjustments and Repayment Redraw fees.

The aim of the comparison rate is to help consumers make a more informed judgement of the costs of a loan, and in so doing, help them to compare various loan products and services offered by the various lending institutions.

For example, a lender may currently advertise their rate as 4.99%pa, yet the loan attracts a $20 a month Loan Service Fee (LSF) and a $1000 Establishment Fee. The Comparison Rate legislation applies a formula that aims to show the real or 'true' rate that the customer will be paying for the life of the loan. Effectively, the rate may be closer to 5.5%pa, after taking the monthly LSF and the Establishment Fee into consideration.

 

Products Affected

 

The Comparison Rate applies all Loan products subject to the Consumer Credit Code. The Comparison Rate applies to Home Loan & Personal Loan Commonwealth Bank products.

The Comparison Rate legislation does not apply to:

  • Credit Cards;
  • Smart Access/ Complete Access and Streamline Overdrafts;
  • Viridian Lines of Credit;
  • Off-The-Plan loans

 

Comparison Rate Warning

All financial institutions and mortgage providers will be required to quote a warning whenever they quote a Comparison Rate.

"Comparison rate calculated on a $150,000 secured loan over a 25 year term. WARNING: Comparison rate is true only for the examples given and may not include all fees and charges. Different terms, fees or other loan amounts might result in a different comparison rate. Comparison rates for variable Interest Only loans are based on an initial 5 year Interest Only period. Comparison rates for fixed or guaranteed Interest Only loans are based on an initial Interest Only period equal in length to the fixed or guaranteed period. During an interest only period, your interest only payments will not reduce your loan balance. This may mean you pay more interest over the life of the loan.”
