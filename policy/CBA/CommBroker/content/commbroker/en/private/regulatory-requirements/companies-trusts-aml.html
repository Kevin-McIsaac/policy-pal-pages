Companies

Determine the Customer Type

Confirm the customers’ business structure is a Domestic Proprietary Company.

A Domestic Proprietary company is:

  • A Legal entity separate from its director and owners
  • Companies carrying on business in Australia must be registered under the Corporation Act with the Australian Securities and Investment Commission (ASIC)
  • Will have a unique nine digit ACN number which is issued by ASIC
  • Has the word Proprietary in its name (Pty Ltd or P/L or Pty Limited)
  • Has at least one director and is not listed on a stock exchange
 
 
Your role in customer on-boarding non-personal customer
 
Our AML / CTF program requires that we know the persons and organisations / entities to whom we are providing products and services. Before proceeding with application you need to be confident that:
  • The customer is who they say they are;
  • For non-individual customer types, the customer exists;
  • For non-individual customer types, we know the identity of certain persons connected to the customer; and
  • We can identify the beneficial owners of the customer.

Beneficial Owners
 
 Beneficial Owners can be associated with both Trust and Company applications.
For Companies, the Beneficial Owner of the company must be identified by virtue of ownership or control.
Identify all individuals that own 25% or more of the shares in the Company. If no person owns 25% or more of the Company, identify the individual that controls 25% or more of the shares, e.g. through voting rights. If no person owns or controls 25% or more of the shares, identify the natural person who makes the financial and operating decisions, e.g. CEO.
You must complete full AML verification for all Beneficial Owners, including beneficial owners who are related parties (i.e. an individual who is not a party to the loan).
Note:
  1. A person can be a Director of the company as well as a Beneficial Owner. In this scenario, when submitting the application enter their details under director and also under Beneficial Owner.
  2. If a company is Beneficial owner (holds 25% or more share in the company) then the individuals who are beneficial owners of that company must to be identified and copy of the ID documents provided.
 
 
Beneficiaries
 
Beneficiaries are only associated with Trust Applications. The trustee can be corporate trustee or individual trustee.
All the full name of the beneficiaries listed in the Trust deed must be confirmed with the customer and details updated in the application submission.
This information is available via the extract of the Trust Deed or the relevant page of the certified copy of the Trust Deed (i.e. the page/s where the names and structure of the Trust is detailed).
Beneficiary ID Verification is not required unless they are party to the loan or they are also beneficial owner (of corporate trustee).
 
 
Collect information
 
What you need to collect from the customer
  • Full name of the company
  • Unique identification number, e.g. ACN issued to the company by ASIC
  • Whether the company is registered by ASIC as a proprietary or public company
  • Names of all Directors if the company is registered as a proprietary company name, date of birth and address of each Beneficial Owner, being the individuals owning equal to or greater than 25% of the company
  • Full address of the company’s registered office & postal address
  • Full address of the company’s principal place of business,
  • Organisation Type & Sub Type
  • Contact details (phone & email) & preference if applicable
  • If the company is operating as a charity, the purpose of the charity
  • Industry
  • Not for Profit status
Note:
Use the verification documents (ASIC search/Trust Deed) to validate the information provided by the customer. Where there is discrepancy or Information missing/incomplete clarify with the customer/contact person prior to submitting the application.

Contact and Address Details
 
Apply the Customer's Addresses per the correct classification
 
Registered Address: The full street address of the business's registered office listed with ASIC. The Registered Address cannot be a post office box address
 
Principal Place of Business: The full street address of a business's principal place of business. The address cannot be a post office box address and can be different to a registered address
 
Postal Address: The address where mail will be sent. The address can be a physical address or post office box address and can be different or the same as the residential / registered / principal place of business's address
 
 
Minimum information required to meet Common Reporting Standard (CRS) tax residency requirements for a Domestic Company:
  • Name of the country(ies) where they have tax residency (including Australia)
  • Tax Identification Number (TIN) for each country of tax residency, or reason for not being able to provide the TIN
  • Self-certification, confirming the tax residency details are correct and the
  • Tax residency and TIN/reason for not being able to provide TIN of Beneficial
Notes:
  1. If Customers country of tax residency is 'Australia', Select Country of tax residency does not require the customer to disclose the TIN
  2. Reasons for no TIN; A customer may a advise that they do not have a Tax identification number (TIN), acceptable reasons for not providing a TIN are:
  • Customer hasn't been issued with a TIN by their country of tax residence
  • Customer's country of tax residence does not issue a TIN
  • Country of tax residence does not require the customer to disclose TIN
 
 

TRUST

Description of a Trust

A trust is a legal structure that allows one party to hold property for the benefit of another. A trustee distributes income in accordance with the trust deed.

  • A Trust has:
  • A trust name.
  • Beneficiaries
  • May have a business name and an Australian Business Name (ABN)
  • Be established in Australia
  • Trustee may be an individuals and/or companies
 
Types of trusts (examples)
  • Discretionary(formal) trust
  • Unit trust
  • Family Trust
 
For the purpose of meeting identification requirements of the Trust, the copies of the Trust Deed must be provided to the Bank.
  • Where the customer has provided the original copy of trust deed and the Original has been sighted. Prior to attaching copy of the trust deed to the application note the front page of the trust deed with the following –
  • Original trust deed sighted and include date and the Broker details
 
  • Where customer has provided the certified copy of the trust deed with wet-ink, only a prescribed persons can certify documents as true copies of the original.
  • The Prescribed person must examine the original trust deed and certify the copy of document by writing with wet-ink
    “I certify this to be true and correct copy of the original document”
Note: Retain the copy of the trust deed used to verify for record and future reference.
