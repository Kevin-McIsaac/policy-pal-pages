Credit Policy | Eligible Income Types for Coronavirus

What

Due to the Coronavirus, in order to continue to serve our customers and communities we need to temporarily change our lending standards to ensure borrowing is appropriate in the current environment.

This page will assist guide you through the credit changes that you need to apply for all new and in progress applications.

Why

These changes will ensure that we continue to meet our responsible lending obligations.

How

Responsible Lending

  • You must ensure you have a robust conversation with your customer to understand if their financial situation has been impacted by any Covid related events (including but not limited to lockdowns, restrictions, business closures or isolation requirements. 
  •  You must record this conversation in your application comments. Things that should be covered include:
  • Is the customer still able to work at their usual capacity/
  • Has their income reduced or stopped?
  • Will the customerâ€™s income remain stable if they are required to self isolate?
  • Have they recently been advised of any changes/future changes to their income or employment?

 Full Verification

Income type used for servicing
  • You must always complete a servicing assessment as per the Eligible Income - Self Employed requirements.
  • Where the most recent year is used for servicing and servicing is not evident, the following exception may be requested from a Credit Assessor
If... Then...
Servicing is evident on the most recent years financials No exception required
Servicing is not evident on most recent financials required

You will need to obtain one of the following:

  • Business Activity Statements (BAS) covering two consecutive quarters, must include the most recent BAS period; or
  • Six months' business trading account statements, no older than 14 days preceding the firm date of the application, showing credits evidencing consistent trading income.
  • Then complete the following calculation to determine if an exception can be requested.

Calculation:

Add together the sales figures shown on the BAS or credit to the business trading account statements provided.

Annualise this figure using this method:

 

For BAS

Combined Sales figure ÷ Number of BAS Statements received x Total number of BAS Statements in the year

 

For Business Transaction Account Statements

Combined Sales figure ÷ Number of month of Statements received x 12

Compare the annualised sales figure to the sales figure for the from the previous financial year:

If... Then...

If the annualised sales figure is similar or higher than the sales figure in the previous financial year

Note: If the sale figure is lower, based on the annualised sales, an exception can only be requested if this is 20% or lower when compared to previous financial year.

An exception can be requested.

Note: Your application comments must detail how you have annualised the sales and mitigants for the exception request.
If the annualised sales figure is more than 20% lower than the previous financials sales figure An exception cannot be requested and the figure used in the servicing calculator must be used.
