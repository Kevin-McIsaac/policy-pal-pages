Emailing documents to the Bank

Back to Sending Documents to the Bank

 

Step Process
1
  • Only quote the Bank Reference Number (BRN) in the email subject line
  • Remove all email signatures (signatures with links to Facebook, LinkedIn, or include images can cause issues) and other non-essential content, as the entire email body content will be imaged too.
2 Check the file type of all attached documents. PDF is the best format to send files.

The Bank is only able to accept documents via email with the following file types: .doc, .docx, .xls, .xlsx, .pdf, .html, .htm, .bmp, .tif, .tiff, .jpeg, .gif, .zip, .ppt, .eml, .txt, .rtf

Note: Please send all other files types not listed here via fax (refer to Faxing documents to the Bank).
3

Check the size of the email
 

The Bank is unable to accept emails larger than the 20MB in size and individual files > 10MB. If emails of 20MB or larger are sent you will receive an automated email response to advise non-receipt.
 

Steps to follow if your email is larger than 20MB or individual document is larger than 10MB in size:

  • If the email contains individual documents less than 20MB - send the individual documents in separate emails. Remember to quote the Bank Reference Number (BRN) in the subject line of each email sent.
  • If the email contains individual documents 20MB or over - print and fax the document (refer to Faxing documents to the Bank).
  • Single documents are to have a maximum of 100-200 pages to enable quicker processing. If you have longer documents, split them and attach them in parts.
  • Do not attach zoomed-in images captured on phones directly or include them in the PDF. Print and rescan the image to an A4-sized document.

 

Note: Individual documents larger than 3MB may show with more than one Mail Item Received activity in Loan Tracking.
4 Send the email to tpbpaperlessloan@cba.com.au
Refer to our Contact Us pages for a complete listing of email addresses.

Important: Failure to send your documents to the correct email address will delay your application.

Note for multiple applications: If you need to send the same document/s for customers with multiple applications (i.e.more than one BRN), it is preferred that you send a separate email for each application quoting the specific BRN per email.
