Direct Debit Dishonour Notice

Back to Regulatory Requirements

 

Due to changes resulting from the National Consumer Credit Protection Legislation financial institutions now need to inform a customer if they do not make a direct debit payment. The notification will take the form of a Direct Debit Dishonour notice. For further information on the legislation refer to Broker Update 02/07/10.

What you need to know
The first time a Direct Debit Repayment (DDR) established with another financial institution for the payment of a Home Loan, Line of Credit, Personal Loan or Credit Card, is dishonoured by the customer, the Borrower(s) and any Guarantor(s) will now receive a notice in the mail.

What you need to do
Please be aware of this procedure.
