Transfer Stamp Duty

Transfer Stamp Duty is a government duty and applies whenever property is being purchased, sold or ownership transferred.

Customers can include their Transfer Stamp Duty in their Home Loan and access these funds prior to settlement.

Note: For ACT property purchase contracts entered into on and from Monday 18 September 2017

  • Stamp duty is paid post settlement
  • Ensure your customers have sufficient funds available post settlement. 
  • Payments are to be made via BPAY or Electronic Funds Transfer and Bank Cheques will no longer be accepted by the ACT Revenue Office (RO).

    •  

For more information, customers should consult their solicitor or conveyancer, or visit the ACT Revenue Office Conveyancing Duty website.

 

Transfer Stamp Duty Surcharge for non residents

A non-resident purchasing residential property in New South Wales, Victoria, Queensland, South Australia Tasmania or WA is required to pay a surcharge on Transfer Stamp Duty.

The surcharge in New South Wales is 8% of the dutiable value of the property.

The surcharge in Victoria is 7% of the dutiable value of the property.

The surcharge in Queensland is 7% of the dutiable value of the property.

The surcharge in South Australia is 7% of the dutiable value of the property.

The surcharge in Tasmania is 8% of the dutiable value of the property.

The surcharge in Western Australia is 7% of the dutiable value of the property.
