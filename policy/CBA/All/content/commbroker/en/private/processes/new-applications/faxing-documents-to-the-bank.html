Faxing Documents to the Bank

Back to Sending Documents to the Bank

 

 Step Process 
 1

The first document you fax MUST be one of the below documents:

  • Home Loan On-Boarding Application (002-829); or
  • Top Up Application Checklist (003-614); or
  • More Information Required Form (004-269); or
  • 'Application Exception - Missing Information' fax header (this will be sent to you when we need more information for your Home Loan); or
  • 'Top Up - More Information Required' fax header (this will be sent to you when we need more information for your Top Up)


 

Note:

  • If you include your own coversheet or comments, please ensure one of the above documents is still the first document you send.
  • If you use the same first document for customers with multiple applications, record one BRN in the 'Bank Reference Number' box and note all other BRN's elsewhere on the first page.

 
 2

Always quote the 'Bank Reference Number' and the 'RM State' on this document (type and fill where possible)
 

 

Note:

  • The 'RM State' is the state of your Relationship Manager.

 

Exception for Manual Top Ups Only: You may fax documents without quoting the BRN if the BRN has yet to be sent to you from the Bank. If you do not have the BRN include your Agent ID, Account Number, Customer or Guarantor Name and Broker Application ID/Opportunity Number in the comments.
 

 
 3

Send the fax to the correct fax number. 

Do not fax more than 100 pages at a time. Split your mail item into segments of less than 100 pages if you need to forward large documents (i.e. Construction/Building information). Ensure you place one of the pages noted in Step 1 at the front of each section.

Refer to our Contact Us pages for a complete listing of fax numbers.

Important: Failure to send your documents to the correct fax number will delay your application.
