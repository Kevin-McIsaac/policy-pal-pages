1. About this policy
 

1.1 Why this policy

This policy will help you with validating information provided by the applicant during the application process.
Validation of the information provided by the customer is part of the Responsible Lending scrutiny check and helps us ensure an accurate serviceability assessment is undertaken.
This policy is applicable where the loan activity is a:

  • New loan application
  • Loan increase
  • Any servicing activity requiring a serviceability assessment
You may also need to refer to other lending policy rules, including but not limited to:
  • Serviceability assessment
  • Verifying income
  • Living expenses
  • Commitments
If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, refer to Lenders mortgage insurance (LMI) where required.
 

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Validation of information
  As part of validating the information provided by the customer we must review:
  • The last three months of transactions (debits / withdrawals / credits ) to all StG / BSA / BoM accounts
  • Any other supporting documentation obtained to comply with normal policy requirements (e.g. bank statements for evidencing genuine savings, pay documentation i.e. pay slips or tax returns)
  • Regardless of any document received being mandatory or not, it must be checked for undisclosed liabilities and appropriate action taken. Refer to:
    • Document review – what to look for
  • The Comprehensive Credit Bureau report (CCR) - refer to  Credit Bureau commitment mismatch
The above are separate to our requirements to check conduct, look for red flags or Customers Experiencing Vulnerability

4. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

 
Question Answer
What is ‘DEFT’ and what action do I need to take if I identify a ‘DEFT’ debit transaction? DEFT is a payment system, similar to B-pay, which enables a payer (our customer) to make a payment to a biller.
The biller could be any biller who requires or permits payments through the DEFT payment system.
For example, the biller might be:
  • a financial institution our customer has a debt to (a Commitment)
  • a real estate agency our customer pays rent to (a Commitment)
  • a strata management company our customer pays strata fees to (a Living expense)
 
If the associated transaction narrative for the DEFT debit:
  • Does not clearly evidence the nature of the payment:
Clarify the nature of the payment with the customer, following the ‘Action to take’ instructions documented in this policy.
  • Does evidence the nature of the payment:
If the payment is a potential undisclosed commitment or living expense, follow the ‘Action to take’ instructions documented in this policy.
Remember that it is important to comment on all your enquiries, findings and actions to provide an audit trail and prevent re-work.
My customer declared a commitment but I can’t see the account as being ‘open’ on the credit bureau report, why is that?  Not all credit providers are currently participating in positive reporting of credit facilities.
If your customer declares a commitment which does not appear on the report, refer to Commitments policy for more information about how to verify the commitment.
Where can I find out more information about comprehensive credit reporting? For customers refer to www.creditsmart.org.au
 
I have some historical statements for the applicant and can see that prior to this application being lodged there were payments being made to a credit card. 
The credit card has not been declared in the application process.
When I spoke with the applicant they advised me that the card was closed prior to lodging this application.
The credit card does not appear on the credit bureau report and there were no further payments made to the credit card after the application lodgement date.
Do I need to collect further supporting documents from the applicant?		 Record details of your discussion with the applicant in the assessment notes.
Where you have no other conflicting information to suggest that the credit card is still open, you may accept the applicants explanation that the credit card was closed prior to this application and do not need to obtain further evidence of the facility closure.
 
My customer has an external facility where the bureau report is showing poor repayment history.
The customer has given me supporting documents which show the repayment history is good.
What should I do?		 To query the facility and have the bureau content amended:
  • The applicant may choose to make direct contact with the credit provider
