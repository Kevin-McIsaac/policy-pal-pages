1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy?

 The Bank may consider waiver of the Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) requirement for certain eligible borrowers when the application satisfies the requirements as stipulated in Medico Sector Policy.
 

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Medico Sector Policy (Medico)

2.1.1 Overview - Medico

This policy sets out the parameters for accepting consumer mortgage proposals originated via First Party Retail Lenders and selected Finance Brokers for certain qualifying medical professionals.
Applications may be assessed for applicants who meet the definition of a Medical Professional , whereby the following allowable variances to standard policy may be applied:
  • LVR: a maximum LVR of 90% may be approved without the need to obtain mortgage insurance, up to a maximum total OOT of $7.5m. Borrower OOT is measured at St.George Bank Group level for loans where LVR is >80%. Refer to LVR policy allowable variance - Medico for further details; and
  • Genuine Savings: confirmation and validation of 5% genuine savings is not required for applications whereby mortgage insurance is not required (as per above).
All other prevailing standard non-mortgage insured policies for fully documented loans apply.
This policy is to be used as a guide. Approval is not guaranteed in line with the maximum offerings available under this policy.
This policy is available for application submitted for credit in the Medico’s own name, or related company or trust entities, provided direct ownership and/or directorship by the medico is evident.
There is no authority to extend this sector policy to other occupation types or products - no exceptions permitted.
 

2.1.2 Eligibility criteria - Medico

Medical professional
For the purpose of this policy the definition of a medical professional (‘Medico’) includes the following:

Medical professional with no minimum income threshold:
  • Dentists
  • General Practitioners
  • Hospital-employed Doctors (e.g. Intern, Resident, Registrar, Staff Specialist)
  • Medical Specialists - refer to List of Australian Recognised Medical Specialties for the list of acceptable that fall under Medical Specialties
  • Optometrists
  • Pharmacists
  • Veterinary Practitioners.
Medical professional with a minimum income threshold of $90,000 per annum:
    • Audiologist
    • Chiropractors 
    • Midwife
    • Occupational therapist
    • Osteopath
    • Physiotherapists
    • Podiatrist
    • Psychologist
    • Radiographer
    • Registered nurse
    • Sonographer
    • Speech Pathologist
There is no authority to extend to other occupation types. (Nurses and Paramedics do not qualify for Medico policy.)
Confirmation must be obtained of the applicant’s qualifications and employment as a medical professional via the following:
  • Most recent Australian tax return or
  • Copy of Australian university degree or qualification, or
  • Copy of registration with the Medical Practitioners Board of Australia or equivalent body (copy of print out from the following web page is acceptable provided the registration is current http://www.ahpra.gov.au/Registration/Registers-of-Practitioners.aspx ).

Note: A Sonographer, Speech pathologist or an Audiologist do not currently require registration with AHPRA.
    • A Sonographer can also be verified through this website: https://www.asar.com.au/sonographer-info/find-a-sonographer/
    • A Speech pathologist must be registered as a "certified practising speech pathologist” (CPSP) with Speech Pathology Australia.  CPSP can also be verified from a membership certificate
    • An Audiologist can also be verified through this website:  https://audiology.asn.au/Consumer_Hub/Register_of_Audiology_Australia_Accredited_Audiologist_

If doubt exists refer to Credit for guidance.
A qualified medical professional who is working in medical research (in the same research field as their medical specialty) can be considered for Medico policy.

In each case applicants must be:
  • Australian citizens; or
  • approved permanent visa holders (refer to Migrant Lending Policy); or
  • approved temporary visa holders that are acceptable to the Bank and the Mortgage Insurer (refer to Migrant Lending Policy)
Residency, citizenship and VISA status must be verified at the time of loan application
  

2.1.3 Income verification - Medico

In the case of established self-employed qualifying Medical professionals without a minimum income threshold, the income can be assessed by using:
  • the Fast Track self-employed assessment method; or
  • the last completed personal tax return; and
    • Australian Taxation Office Notice of Assessment; or
    • If ATO NOA has not issued: latest Australian Tax Return lodgement extract from MyGov Portal
Note when assessed under the Fast Track self-employed assessment method the full Fast Track criteria applies, also if the latest tax return has been provided to confirm qualifications then Fast Track assessment method is not available.  

In the case of established self-employed qualifying Medical professionals with a minimum income threshold, the income can be assessed by using:
  • the last completed personal tax return; and
    • Australian Taxation Office Notice of Assessment; or
    • If ATO NOA has not issued: latest Australian Tax Return lodgement extract from MyGov Portal
Tax returns may be used until 15th May each year. Single year tax returns must meet standard documentation policy and be provided for all related entities (company, trust and/or partnership).

For PAYG medicos, standard income and employment verification is required to be undertaken as per normal policy requirements.

In the case of relatively newly qualified medicos, income may be projected on the future income earning potential of the individual, but this will need to be documented to Credit in each case. Appropriate comments supporting the submission must be provided.

For Medical professionals with a minimum income threshold the following additional criteria apply:
  • The minimum income threshold is a gross taxable income of $90,000 per annum.
  • The income used to meet the income threshold requirement must be income from the applicants’ qualification.
  • Fast Track Self-employed assessment method is not available
  • If the application contains multiple qualifying professionals, the combined income of the qualifying professionals must meet the minimum gross taxable income stated above.
Applicants earning foreign income are ineligible for this policy, as it exceeds the maximum LVR available for this income type.
Refer to Verifying Income policy.
 
Where an application is being assessed under this policy with multiple applicants
 
If one of the applicants is self-employed but not an eligible medical professional, they are required to provide 2 years of financials and tax returns.  Refer to Self-employed applicants policy > rules.

2.1.5 Transfer of company debt to personal names in the consumer portfolio - Medico

"Company" debts are defined as those used for purchase or construction of residential and/or business premises.
Where it can be established that a personal debt was obtained in a company name with the outstanding debt appearing on the company's latest balance sheet, and the property securing that debt is held in personal name/s, requests that the facility/debt be transferred across to personal name/s will be accommodated.
Refinance of working capital, equipment finance and all other business related lending purposes are specifically excluded.
Broker is to verify the authenticity of the original transaction and provide appropriate commentary via ApplyOnline.
 

2.1.6 Products and discounts available - Medico

Applicants can choose from the standard product range, e.g. Home Loans - Fixed or variable.
Discounted or negotiated interest rates are only available by the addition of the Advantage Package option to the requested residential lending product. Advantage Package option is not available for Basic Home Loan product. Current advertised discounts apply.
Customers may qualify for Medico Sector Policy without the addition of Advantage Package. System constraints may make either the loan or borrower types ineligible for packaging.
The following are not eligible for mortgage insurance waiver under this policy:
  • Loans with Interest Only repayment types where the product does not convert to Principal and Interest repayments after the IO repayment period expires (including Portfolio Loans)
Brokers are not permitted to establish the loan with Principal and Interest repayment types and subsequently switch to Interest Only repayment types.

 

2.1.7 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ’s) - Medico

The following are common questions regarding Medico Sector Policy. If lenders are unsure how to proceed after reading the detailed policy and the below FAQ’s please contact your Credit Manager.
Q. If we have a qualified medical professional who is working in medical research (in the same research field as their specialty) can we use Medico policy?
A. Yes
 
Q. If we have a qualified medical professional who is working in research but as a geologist, can we allow under Medico policy?
A. No
 
Q. Where a doctor has obtained their qualifications in another country are they eligible for Medico policy?
A. Applicant must currently be registered to practice with either the Australian Medical Council (AMC), or Australian Dental Council (ADC); and must provide a copy of current registration with the Medical / Dental / Veterinary Practitioners Board of Australia or equivalent body in the State or Territory in which they practice.

3.1 LMI waiver process - Medico Sector Policy
All applications are to be submitted to the Credit Team for approval with the following notations:
  • Under the subject heading, load the letters ‘MEDICO’ and
  • Enter a comment against the deal noting submission under the “Medico sector” policy and requesting waiver of mortgage insurance.
