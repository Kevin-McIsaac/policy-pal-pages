1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy

 The following information is retained for reference purposes only. No lending is permitted non-residents
This policy outlined the parameters for assessing loan applications that involves non-resident borrower(s) in the consumer mortgage application.
 

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Definitions of non-residents lending
 

For residential lending purposes a non-resident individual is defined as:

  • A person who does not hold Australian Citizenship or approved Australian Permanent residency visa, or
  • A person who does hold an approved Australian Temporary residency visa however does not ordinarily reside in Australia – refer to Migrant Lending policy for details.
  • New Zealand citizens living and working in Australia are not considered non-residents for the purposes of this policy.
The following restrictions apply to all home loan applications where any applicant is considered to be a non-resident (as per above definition):
  • No lending is permitted, even if the applicant’s income is not relied on to meet serviceability
  • Joint loans where one applicant is a non-resident are also not acceptable. All applicants must be eligible as per policy requirements regardless of their contribution to the loan
  • Loan increases / top ups / refinances / cash out is not permitted on existing loans
  • Extension of interest only repayment period past the maximum permitted for the product is not acceptable (as this requires the loan to be re-originated and refinances are not permitted)
  • Guarantees from non-residents are not acceptable. I.e. non-residents are not acceptable as borrowers or owners.
No exceptions to the above are permitted.
 

2.2 Establishing citizenship / residency status

A customers citizenship, visa type or non-residency status must be established at initial application stage.
Non-resident status may be identified where a foreign passport is presented for identification purposes or the applicant(s) is identified as residing overseas.
 

2.3 Migrant applicants

This policy does not apply to migrant applicants. Refer to Migrant Lending policy for further information

2.4 Withholding tax and tax advice for non-residents

The following information is retained for reference purposes only. No lending is permitted non-residents
Lenders are to inform customers that they may have a potential liability to pay Withholding Tax in their country of residence and that they should undertake their own investigations in this regard and should always seek their own tax advice.
Note:
Tax advice of any nature must NOT be offered to applicants/borrowers under any circumstances.
 

2.5 Approval from foreign investment review board (FIRB)

The following information is retained for reference purposes only. No lending is permitted non-residents
Non-resident applications require approval from the Federal Government Treasury – Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB). It is the responsibility of the purchaser to ensure that approval is held. Employees are to advise non-resident purchases to make enquiries of the FIRB, refer www.firb.gov.au.
Non-residents are prohibited from acquiring established dwellings for investment purposes in Australia (excluding New Zealand residents who are exempt from this requirement).
In all cases (purchase, increase, top-up or refinance) lenders are to hold a copy of the FIRB approval prior to issuing a formal approval. No exceptions permitted by any DRA/CAL holder. This includes authorities held in Credit teams. The loan application is to be validated against the FIRB approval to ensure the application complies with the conditions under which the FIRB approval was issued.
The validation is to include, but is not limited to:
  • Applicants name
  • Property being purchased or refinanced
  • Loan type (owner occupied v’s investment)
  • Use of property (eg used as principle place of residence, as confirmed with post settlement address matching property address identified in FIRB approval)
  • Rental income (eg if renting of the property is not permitted, then this income can’t be used for serviceability calculations)
Note:
In some cases FIRB approval is granted to a specific development. The Developer may receive a certificate identifying that further foreign investment approval is not required when sales are made to foreign persons.
A copy of the FIRB approval issued to the developer is to be retained on file. The offered security property (for purchase or refinance) should match the property identified in the FIRB approval.
If the offered security is a ‘unit’ refer to Assessing Acceptable Securities and Security Development Register (SDR) as there may be additional restrictions applicable for larger developments (including exposure limits).



FIRB Frequently Asked Questions

Q1 – If an application indicates we are providing funds for the purchase of a property that is not being used as security for this transaction, do we need to obtain a copy of the FIRB for that property?
A1 – No, the bank only seeks FIRB documents in relation to a property or properties it is taking as security.
Q2 – If FIRB approval was not obtained at the time of the original purchase by our applicants, but they have subsequently obtained approval, can we proceed?
A2 – We can proceed if the FIRB document provided to us shows the applicant is compliant with the FIRB approval conditions. 
 

2.6 Special conditions letter for non-resident

The following information is retained for reference purposes only. No lending is permitted non-residents
On all non-resident loans, a special condition has to be included with the Loan Offer.
If formal approval is granted, the Lender instructs Customer Mortgage Services to prepare and issue the Loan Offer Document and Mortgage Documents.
