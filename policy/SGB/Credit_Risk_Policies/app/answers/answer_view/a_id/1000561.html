2.1 Environment risk
 

2.1 Environment risk

Purpose The Bank will comply with all legal requirements, including environmental laws, applicable to its operations.

The following instructions contain a brief overview in relation to the initial steps to be taken when real estate is offered as security.


 Definition and Scope
In legal terms, the term 'environment' is a very broad concept and includes:
  • land, air and water
  • living organisms and ecosystems
  • buildings and artificial structures
  • amenity (or enjoyment) of an area
'Pollution', 'contamination' or 'environmental harm' can be committed in many ways, including by:
  • discharge of any solid, liquid or gas into land, water or air
  • the emission of noise, heat, smoke, dust, fumes or odours
  • unjustified interference with the amenity of an area
  • unsightly structures or insanitary conditions of premises
  • actual or potential harm to the health or safety of humans
The main concern for a broker will often be pollution of property over which a security is held (i.e. a 'contaminated site'), as the presence of contaminants will have a direct impact upon the ability to fully realise the security.
However it is not the only concern as, for example, pollution generating from business operations may result in a civil liability in damages, the imposition of criminal fines, the suspension or cessation of profit-making activities or adverse publicity. These results clearly have a direct impact upon the realisation of a debenture or other security over the business operations, and may otherwise impair the borrower's capacity to repay its debt.
All sites may be potentially contaminated but specific industries have a higher probability of site contamination, including activities such as oil production and storage as well as chemical manufacture and formulation.
The level of risk of site contamination associated with any particular industrial, commercial or agricultural practice will depend on the regulatory framework and the safety procedures employed at the individual sites.
 

Procedure

The Home Loan Application form asks: 'Are you aware of any environmental contamination affecting any security property or any property adjoining any security property?'
If the property is affected, brokers are to enter details in the comments section of ApplyOnline and the loan file sent/escalated to Credit.
The file will be perused and will be returned to the broker either with a further questionnaire for the customer to complete or with approval to accept the security offered.
In either event no loan agreement is to be issued until all environmental issues are resolved.
