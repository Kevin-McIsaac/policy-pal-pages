1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy

 This policy tells you what valuation method to use and how to assess risk when assessing real estate as security for residential mortgage lending.
Refer to this policy in conjunction with the Assessing Acceptable Securities and Assessing Loan to Value Ratio .
For definition relating to this policy, refer to Glossary for further details.
If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, refer to  Lenders mortgage insurance (LMI) where required.
 

1.2 What are acceptable real estate valuation methods?

The bank accepts the following real estate valuation methods:
  • Residential and Construction Long Form valuation
  • Residential and Construction Short Form valuation
  • Desktop valuation
  • Automated Valuation Model (also known as  Modelled Estimate, Electronic Assessment, E-Val)
All residential valuations must be performed using the bank’s Valuation system - ValEx or Property Hub.
Valuations received are for the Bank's use only and are not to be provided to customers.

Important

The bank performs several validation checks in our origination system. These additional checks form part of the overall valuation strategy and policy.
 
 

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Assessing security value using valuation report

 Valuation reports are only to be accepted from valuers on the Westpac Group approved 'Residential Valuer' panel * . All valuation reports are to be addressed to the Bank.

The customer estimated market value is not to be amended within an application once a valuation (any type) has been completed.
 

2.1.1 Requesting a Valuation Report

Valuation report:
  • can only be requested by authorised staff member or third party of Westpac Banking Group via Valex or Property Hub
  • cannot be requested for the purchase of a property where the offer has not been accepted by the owner (to assist a prospective purchaser e.g. pre-purchase valuations are not permitted )
 

2.2 Contract of Sales (Purchase Price)

Risk Criteria
A contract of sale (COS) is not to be used as a security assessment.

A COS received during the application process must be reviewed to:
  • Ensure the security meets below criteria:
    • The property must be zoned to allow residential usage
    • The cost / value of any chattels (e.g. furnishings) and/or the value of any rebates or incentives offered by the vendor are to be deducted from the total purchase price to determine the security value
    • The security is being purchased
    • The Contract of Sale is not older than 3 months
  • Request a full valuation report, where it is identified that:
    • the transaction is not being completed at arm's length through an independent Australian based real estate agent (i.e. it is a private sale), or
    • the Contract of Sale indicates a ‘Put and Call’ contract , or
    • the Contract of Sale is older than 3 months

2.3 Assessing security value using Automated Valuation Model (AVM)

Risk Criteria
If an Automated Valuation Model (AVM) has been produced, it can be used to validate the purchase price or customer estimate market value.
 
Where an AVM search on a security property is successful, it is used to validate the purchase price or the customer estimated market value. The validation is restricted to:
  • Only being used for the specific customer the AVM was ordered for
  • Only being used where the LVR for the loan (i.e. the LVR inclusive of all security values) is equal to or less than the maximum end LVR (i.e. capitalised LVR) value returned with the search.
  • Where an AVM for a security in the loan returns a maximum end LVR result that is less than the LVR for the loan, then the security will require an alternate valuation method (either a Desktop if policy permits, or a full valuation).
Where an AVM has been completed, the customer estimated market value is not to be amended. The AVM result can only be relied upon based on the original customer estimated market value or contract price
  

2.4 Re-using of Existing Valuations

Valuation methods previously used to determine the value of a security are stored in the Bank’s systems and maybe re-use subject to meeting policy requirement. 
Existing valuation results (excluding AVM) for a specific property may be used for a different customer as a valid existing valuation in terms of assessing residential security.

2.6 Valuation Review

If the following criteria applies, the valuation can NOT be reviewed / challenged:
  • Security assessment is an electronic (E-val) assessment or desktop assessment
  • The resultant LVR is within acceptable non mortgaged insured lending guidelines
  • Date of the escalation is more than one month from the date of the valuation
  • If a loan is mortgage insured by Helia (formerly Genworth)
 
For all other situations, where a valuation report is completed and returned to STG/BSA/BoM by our Panel Valuer, and there are concerns expressed by our customers and/or Broker regarding the accuracy of information contained in the report, and there is other relevant factual sales information that supports consideration for a review, brokers are to:
  • Contact your Business Development Manager directly or request Mortgage Operations to raise a Value Query directly with the Valuer in ValEx system; AND
  • Provide additional comparable sales* for consideration
*Relevant information includes:
  • Commentary confirming an oversight or error made by the valuer; or
  • Valid Comparable sales (which do not already appear in the valuation report) within the last 6 months, with details of:
    • Within the same location and not from within the same complex if a new development similar price range
    • Similar with regards to condition, price range, type of property, land size, and layout (bedrooms, bathrooms etc)
Note: Sales evidence cannot include properties sold directly from the Developer; they must be re-sales.

3.2 Contract of Sale (Purchase Price)

A contract of sale (COS) is not to be used as a security assessment.  A COS received during the application process must be reviewed to ensure the security meets below criteria:
 

Contract of Sale Requirements

Obtaining a full copy of the Contract of Sale signed by the customer is preferred. However, it is not required in all cases to comply with this policy.  If the Contract of Sale is undated and is not a First Home Owners Grant (FHOG) application, assume that the contract date is the date the contract was received by the Bank.
 
A fully executed contract by vendor and purchases is required prior to settlement where:
  • Application is to be mortgage insured
  • FHOG applications
  • A Modelled Estimate is being used to validate the purchase price as the security valuation method.
 

Minimum Criteria per Contract

As a minimum, the pages containing the following information are to be obtained:
  • Name(s) of the vendor(s)
  • Full name(s) of the purchaser(s)
  • Name of the real estate agent (or vendor's agent)
  • Property address & title details
  • Purchase price
  • Contract date (dated within 3 months of the date of the loan application)
  • Any annexure, special conditions or schedule (where applicable)
  • Details of chattels and other items included in the purchase price (if applicable)
  • Lease / leaseback arrangements (if applicable)

Chattels

The below list of chattels are to be assessed as 'fixed' when they are specified in a contract of sale:
  • Air conditioning equipment (including heaters and fans)
  • BBQs
  • Built in furniture, outdoor furniture
  • Carpets / floor coverings
  • Clothes dryer, washing machine
  • Dishwashers
  • Fish tanks
  • Fridges / Freezers
  • Garden decor (statues, ponds, lights, plants, pots, fountains etc.)
  • Garden irrigation equipment (i.e. domestic use)
  • Garden sheds, clothes lines, cubby houses, bird aviary
  • Kitchen island benches detached / movable
  • Inbuilt Audio / Video systems (including home theatre systems)
  • Lawn mowers (including ride on mowers)
  • Light fittings
  • Ovens (including Microwave ovens) / Cooktops / Rangehoods
  • Pool tables
  • Pools, spas and equipment
  • Remote control devices
  • Security systems
  • Septic tanks
  • Television (including TV antennas, satellite dish or pay TV equipment)
  • Wardrobes
  • Water tanks / pumps
  • Window treatments.
 

3.3 Automated Valuation Modelled or Modelled Estimate

  A Modelled Estimate will determine whether or not the purchase price (PP) or customer estimate (CEMV) is validated.

New wording
A contract of sale (COS) is not to be used as a security assessment.
A COS received during the application process must be reviewed to:
  • Ensure the security meets below criteria:
    • The property must be zoned to allow residential usage
    • The cost / value of any chattels (e.g. furnishings) and/or the value of any rebates or incentives offered by the vendor are to be deducted from the total purchase price to determine the security value
    • The security is being purchased
    • The Contract of Sale is not older than 3 months
  • Request a full valuation report, where it is identified that:
    • the transaction is not being completed at arm's length through an independent Australian based real estate agent (i.e. it is a private sale), or
    • the Contract of Sale indicates a ‘Put and Call’ contract , or
    • the Contract of Sale is older than 3 months
If the Contract of Sale is undated and is not a First Home Owners Grant (FHOG) application, assume that the contract date is the date the contract was received by the Bank.
