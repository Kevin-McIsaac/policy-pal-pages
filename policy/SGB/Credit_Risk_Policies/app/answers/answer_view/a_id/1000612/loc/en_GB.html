04.05 Pipeline Authority

1. Overview

A pipeline application is defined as an application which previously met credit policy requirements but following the policy change effective date subsequently does not meet new credit policy. The policy change effective date will be communicated via a lending update.
 

2. Eligibility
  

  1. The following are considered eligible for standard pipeline treatment:
  • Application may be mortgage insured or non-mortgage insured. 
  • An application where a formal credit decision has been run prior to a policy change effective date.  This includes Approval in Principal assessments.
  • An application formally approved as at the credit policy change effective date and a minor change is required to correct a spelling error, or change product type (eg from fixed to variable or vice versa) the original approval is permitted to be re-instated.
  • An application received via a 3rd party lodgement system prior to a policy change effective date with a credit decision run on or after the policy change effective date.  Must be accompanied by standard supporting documents sufficient to allow the assessment of the application and a credit decision outcome.
  • An application submitted to Credit prior to a policy change effective date with a credit decision run on or after the policy change effective date.
  • Credit has the authority to determine whether the deal meets pipeline criteria including definition of what constitutes supporting documents.
  • The credit decision prior to the policy change effective date is not more than 90 days old. For clarity, applications that have been formally approved and accepted by customers within 90 days of formal approval are still permitted to settle as per standard process.  Refer Loan Approval policy > section 27. Assessment period under Responsible Lending, noting longer settlement periods apply for Building loans (contract and owner builder) and unregistered land purchases.
  • If there are any special pipeline conditions relating to a specific policy change, these will be included within the related lending update communication.  
 

3. Reassessment

Re-assessment of an application will be required and new credit policy will apply if any of the following changes are made:
Change Example
Applicants – deletion or addition
  • Additional borrower is added to application.
  • Applicant becomes a guarantor or guarantor becomes a borrower.
Assets decrease
  • Title search indicates an asset is not owned by the borrower and is removed from assets.
Credit bureau - a new decline or refer decision is received related to bureau default or bankruptcy
  • An application show no defaults at the original credit decision date
  • Eg When the credit decision is run after the policy change, applicant 1 now has a Telstra default for $120. This application can-not be honoured under pipeline as there is a new decline due for applicant 1.
Employment mode
  • Applicant 1 is working full time at the original credit decision date.
  Applicant 1 changes from full time employment to casual employment.
  This application can-not be honoured under pipeline, even if the income remains the same.
Income reductions
  • Applicant 2 has allowable social security income removed due to age of dependants, and social  security income does not meet income requirements. 
Increase in expenses, outgoings or existing loan repayments
  • Applicant 1 and 2 have increased rental outgo, this is an increase in expenses
  • Applicant 2 has an existing St.G Bank Group loan and the loan repayments are increasing ie not the loan being applied for as part of this application.
Increase in loan amount by >10% of the original loan amount for Non-Mortgage Insured applications
  • Original credit decision is an AIP for a loan amount of $400k with property estimated value of  $500k. Applicants have now found a property valued at $550k and require a loan of $440k. Non  low doc loan.
    This can be honoured under pipeline as the loan increase is <=10% of the original loan requested.
    Note: the increased loan amount must have passed serviceability prior to the policy change  effective date.
Increase in loan amount for Mortgage Insured applications
Note: Acceptable under pipeline where there is a decrease in base loan amount for a mortgage insured application, provided there are no other changes which trigger re-assessment
  • Any increase in a Mortgage Insured application cannot be progressed under pipeline.
Increase in liabilities (St.George Bank group or other financier)
  • Applicant has taken out 3 new visa cards since their original credit decision (AIP).
  • These liabilities were not previously captured on the customers statement of position
    This is a change to the applicant’s financial position and the application can-not be honoured  under pipeline.
Increase in LVR
  • Application is lodged for a refinance of an existing debt. 
    The customer estimates that the property is valued at $500k and based on the estimate our LVR is 80%.
  • When the valuation is completed the customer estimate was high and the actual value of the  property came in at $470k.  Our LVR is now 85%.  As there has been an increase in LVR we are not able to honour under pipeline. 
Securities – deletion or addition
Note - the deletion or addition of security does not apply to AIP applications where the security was not previously identified. Where security was not previously identified the new security must meet all policy requirements to be acceptable as pipeline. Additionally the AIP to full approval must not contain any other unacceptable changes noted in section 1.
  • Customers had an approval in principal based on a loan amount of $400k for a property purchase price of $500k.  The address was not specified.
    Applicants have now located a property to purchase and requested a loan amount of $440k with  a purchase price of $550k.  The new property details are added to the application. The property meets  standard acceptable security requirements.
    • If the original AIP was not subject to mortgage insurance and the loan increase is <=10% of the original loan requested AND serviceability of the debt was able to be demonstrated under prior credit policy, then the transaction could be honoured under pipeline.
    • If the original AIP was subject to mortgage insurance the loan amount is unable to be increased when the purchase property is identified.  Applications will be required to meet new credit policy.
 

 

4. Referral to Credit

 Applications that do not meet pipeline criteria may be referred to Credit for consideration where the request for override can be justified and there is no trigger for reassessment as per section 3. Reassessment.

 

 4. Assessment period under Responsible Lending

For further information regarding where a loan may require reassessment and pipeline no longer applies refer to Loan Approval policy.
