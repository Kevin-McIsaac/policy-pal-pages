1. About this policy


1.1 Why this policy?
 This policy identifies the acceptable sources of income and methods used to verify these incomes for residential lending purposes including new loan origination, loan increases, any servicing or variation activity requiring a serviceability assessment.
 
If the income type is not listed, then the income type is not acceptable. Income types not listed may only be considered by Credit where:
  • appropriate mitigants have been provided and;
  • evidence of the income can be obtained to complete verificationAll income and employment verification is to be completed prior to approval.
     
    The following applies when verifying income and employment documentation provided by applicant:
  • Ensure that all applicants/guarantors have signed the Privacy Statement (refer to  Home Loan application form policy)
  • Ensure documents furnished by applicant/s conform to policy requirements. Ascertain whether verbal verification is required.

You may also need to refer to other lending policy rules, including but not limited to:
  • Document Standards
  • Serviceability assessment
  • Self-employed applicants
Generally, income verification criteria for non-mortgage (Non MI) and mortgage insured loans (MI) are identical unless specifically stipulated in this policy.
 
If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, refer to Lenders mortgage insurance (LMI) where required.

 
For all Loans
  • Self-employed income (including trust distributions and company income) is required to be verified in conjunction with Self-employed applicants policy.  
  • Where the applicant earns PAYG income (or other supplementary income) but also has a directorship and / or shareholding or ABN for a business, and the applicant is not being assessed under Fast Track, Brokers are required to undertake further investigation to identify how the applicant is related to the business and if the applicant is a director and/or shareholder.  Where the applicant is a director and/or shareholder, further investigation of the business is to be completed (even where income from the business is not being used for serviceability assessment) to establish the below:
    • when the applicant became a director;
    • the percentage of shareholding;
    • if the business is trading / non-trading; and
    • if the business profitable or able to able to service it’s own commitments
  1Where the applicant is either an Independent director or an Executive director they are not considered to be the business owner and do not need to be assessed as a self-employed applicant.
  • Independent director: A director that does not have a vested interest I.E. they are not a shareholder and the directorship is not held in a business that is owned (shareholding > 0%) or controlled (directorship) by a spouse or family member (e.g. child or parent)  or a subsidiary  entity of the director or director’s spouse / family member.
  • Executive director: A director that does have a vested interest (a minor % shareholding of <=5%) however limited to also being an independent PAYG employee. E.g. the CEO of Westpac is a director but also an independent PAYG employee (PAYG employee of a business that is not their own business).
For Non Mortgaged insured loans
Applicants are not required to provide evidence of income (e.g. rental, investment or from other sources), where it is not used to service the loan, unless:
  • required for self-employed applicants as detailed above in ‘For all loans’, or;
  • specifically requested by Credit on a case by case basis  
 For Mortgaged Insured loans
All applicants are to provide satisfactory documentation to verify employment/ income.
 
Important:
Brokers need to include in ApplyOnline notes that other income might be available but is not required for serviceability.

Salary sacrifice / Fringe benefit deduction / Pre-tax deductions on a payslip are not an income but  deductions from gross income. An employee may choose to have their taxable wage / salary reduced (sacrificed) via a deduction to their gross salary / wage.
 
Some examples include: repayment of car leases or other loan repayment, purchases of computers, payment of childcare fees, purchase leave or additional superannuation contributions above the Australian Superannuation Guarantee (SG).
 
Salary sacrifice / Fringe benefit deduction / Pre-tax deductions amounts may be included as part of the total gross income when a signed and dated 'Salary Sacrifice Declaration' form is held and the requirements below have been met.
 
Note: If the salary sacrifice deduction is not required to be included in gross income for serviceability calculations, it is acceptable to proceed with the lower income figure and he salary sacrifice declaration form is not required.  In these instances you must still include any commitment or living expense which is related to the deduction amount.
 
 
For additional superannuation contributions above SG:
The declaration must confirm:
  • The deduction is not required to repay Self-Managed Super Fund borrowings; and
  • The deduction is voluntary, can be cancelled at any time and received as taxable income; and
  • The customer will cancel the deduction if required to meet their existing commitments and the repayments for the loan they have requested
  
*Important: When considering the use of salary sacrifice amounts listed as additional superannuation contributions above SG, the assessors must confirm that the contributions are not:

Salary / Wage – after tax or post tax deductions After Tax or Post Tax deductions are not an income type but are deductions from the applicant’s net salary, i.e. after tax has been applied and deducted.
The applicant(s) may be using the after tax or post-tax deduction to pay an expense, a commitment (loan) or for superannuation contributions.
If the payslip does not contain sufficient detail to identify what the post-tax deduction amount is being used for, further enquiries are to be made with the applicant(s).
Post-tax deductions must not be entered in the income verification tool. The post-tax deduction is to be assessed and entered in the assessment tool as per:
  • Living expense policy, refer specific expense category > expense category table; or
  • Commitments policy, as per the individual commitment type
Where the post-tax deduction is for a superannuation contribution, treat as a living expense. Refer to Living expenses > Expense category table > Other regular or recurring expenses.
Where the applicant tells us they will reduce or cancel any voluntary after tax or post tax deduction to meet their existing commitments and the repayments for the loan they have requested, refer to and follow requirements in:
  • Living expenses > Reducing living expense; or
  • Serviceability assessment > Excluding a commitment

4. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

 

4.1 Foreign income FAQs

 
Question Answer
My applicant is an Australian Citizen living and working in the UK, but they don’t have dual citizenship, do I need to ask them for their work visa? Yes, you need to confirm they are legally able to work in the UK before proceeding with the application.
My applicant has recently become an Australian Citizen, previously they had a Chinese passport, but for this application they have provided me with a copy of their Australian passport. Because they were previously a Chinese citizen, do I still need to get a work permit if they are now living and working in China? As China does not permit dual citizenship with Australia, yes, the applicant will need to provide evidence of their work permit before proceeding with the application.
My applicant is not a resident of Australia, they live and work in China and have a Chinese passport, do I need a work permit? No, as the applicant is living and working within the same country that they have citizenship with, they will not require a work permit. However, this applicant would meet the non-resident definition and is not an acceptable applicant type.
My applicant has provided me with a copy of their Indian passport, they have also given me a copy of their Australian 190 visa, but they are currently living and working in Dubai. What do I need to do? This applicant’s income does not comply with the visa matrix requirements, 190 visa type must earn AUD. If the 190 visa is ignored, then this applicant is a non-resident of Australia. In both scenarios the applicant is unacceptable to the bank and no lending permitted.
 

4.2 Salary Sacrifice FAQs

 
Question Answer

The narrative on the payslip shows ‘voluntary superannuation contribution’ or non-Superannuation Guarantee (SG) contribution, why do I still need to get the customer to complete the salary sacrifice declaration form?
The salary sacrifice declaration form helps us have a conversation with our customer and record the outcome of those discussions.
In some cases the applicant may be unwilling or unable to cancel the deductions, therefore it is important that we do not make an assumption that the amount of the deduction is going to be available to meet this new loan commitment.
For example:
  1. The voluntary salary sacrifice deduction on the payslip is being used by the applicant towards a Self- Managed Superannuation Fund Home Loan.
Because of the shortfall between rent received on the investment property and the SMSF Home loan repayment, the applicant is unable to cancel the deduction.
  1. The applicant’s employer pays an additional superannuation contribution above SGC where the employee makes a voluntary contribution.
If the employee cancels the voluntary contribution, then they will lose the benefit of the above SGC employer contribution. The applicant is unwilling to cancel the deduction as they will suffer a long term financial impact.
  1. The applicant has previously had a marital financial settlement.  They are making voluntary contributions so they can rebuild their superannuation.  As they are planning for their retirement, they are unwilling to cancel the voluntary contribution amount.

When don’t I need to get the salary sacrifice declaration form?
  • If the amount of the salary sacrifice deduction is not required for serviceability assessment, then you do not need a salary sacrifice declaration form. Your loan serviceability assessment will be based on the lower income figure.
  • The deduction is an after tax deduction.  While you may need to include the deduction as a commitment or living expense, it is not reducing the customer’s taxable income and no salary sacrifice declaration form is required.
Why would my customer not be able to cancel a deduction for purchased leave?
The applicant may need to contact their employer to check under what basis they are able to cancel the deduction.
For instance if the applicant has already taken the leave, repayment amount may be spread over the remaining year and might be unable to cancel the deduction.

Why can’t my customer cancel their novated lease deduction?
Early cancellation of the novated lease may result in negative tax implications for the customer.
They need to investigate with both their employer and seek independent taxation advice to make a fully informed decision about any financial implications of cancelling the novated lease.

When the applicant has a novated lease, what repayment do I use for serviceability assessment?
Refer to Verifying Income > Process to follow > Assessing Supplementary or Additional Income > Salary Sacrifice > For purchased leave and other purposes


 

If I don’t use the salary sacrifice deduction amount for serviceability assessment, do I still need to load the commitment or living expense?		 Yes you do.
Even if the income is not used for serviceability assessment you must include the commitment or living expense.

Why can’t I load Salary Sacrifice deduction amounts as un-taxed income?
If the deduction is cancelled, then the income would be taxable.

Question Answer

When using an account statement for verification, do I need every page issued for that statement?
No
You only need the pages which:
  • Display the information you are verifying, and
  • Enable you to comply with the Minimum Document Standards  for an account statement
 

4.4 Emergency Services (FAQs)

 
Question Answer

Do I need to verify the employment type just from what the customer tells me, or do I need to validate it from the payslip or some other source?
Employment type must be customer declared and we don't require any verification or other validation.  This is because the information is not always available on the payslips or other documents. 
If there is an occupation or employment status evident on the payslip which contradicts what the customer states, further clarification is required in line with Validation of information provided by the customer policy.

The eligibility criteria states Fire Officer, Police officer etc, but is there a more precise definition?  For example, what if the applicant is regularly paid overtime but they are an inspector for the fire service, or a call centre operator for the policy department?
The policy is specific to active duty or frontline Fire Officers, Policy Officers, Paramedics.  It is not available to all employees who work for an emergency services provider.  For example a Fire Officer would need to be based at a Fire Station and not in a call centre or administrative only role.

What if an emergency services employee is employed casually or on a contract?
The emergency services policy only applies to permanent employees.
  • Casual employees are to be assess under casual income verification rules.
  • For a dependant contractor refer to verifying salary and wage income (base or base and non-base income)
  • For an independent contractor refer to Self-employed applicants policy.

As we are obtaining a Group 2 document from the previous financial year in addition to the payslips, does this mean the applicant must be continually employed by the same employer for over 12 months?
Yes.  We need to have both the payslip and the Group 2 documents showing the same employer, as this contributes to demonstrating a history of receiving the non-base income. 
If the applicant has not been with the same employer across both income verification sources, you may refer the application to Credit for consideration where the applicant has worked in the same job role but with different employers.

What if a hospital based nurse works across multiple hospitals?
A nurse's employment may require them to work across multiple hospitals, while still being employed with the same organisation.  They may have different payslips which show different locations.  As long as they are ultimately employed by the same employer this is acceptable.

Applicants would also qualify if they are permanently employed by more than one employer.  For example, if a nurse works permanent part time 2 days a week at a public hospital and 3 days a week at a private hospital.  You would need payslips and group 2 documents for each employer to be able to use this policy.

Do we treat nurses employed at a private hospital different to those employed at a public hospital?
No, the policy is the same for both.

Note: Nurses employed outside of a hospital are ineligible.

Are car, uniform and other allowances acceptable non-base income?
Yes, all industry specific or employer allowances can be assessed at 100%.

What if an applicant works part time as a frontline emergency services employee or nurse and also has additional PAYG employment?
The non-emergency services or nursing income is to be assessed as per Verifying salary and wages income (base or base plus non-base). 

Existing Fringe Benefits income policy identifies I need to refer an application to Credit for consideration when using this income source for serviceability calculations.  
If I don't include the 'Reportable Fringe Benefits' amount when calculating total income for the Group 2 document, will I still need to refer the application to Credit? 
No. If you don't need to include the Fringe Benefit income amount to pass serviceability, there is no need to refer the application to Credit
