1. About this policy
 

1.1 Definition - Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI)

Lender’s mortgage insurance protects the Bank against loss in the event the borrower defaults which results in the sale of the security property, and there is a shortfall in the sale proceeds to cover the debt owing to the Bank.
 

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Cover - LMI

Who is covered?

Lender’s mortgage insurance protects the Bank only. The LMI policy does not provide any protection for the borrower. The policy is a contract between the Bank and the Mortgage Insurer, with the insurance premium payable by the borrower.
LMI is not to be confused with life insurance, mortgage protection insurance or property insurance.
 

Type of cover

The total loan (100%) is to be mortgage insured. Top 20% cover is not available.
 

2.2 Premium and duration of cover

A once-only premium is payable, and the amount of premium payable is based on the level of risk as evidenced by the lending value ratio (LVR). The lower the risk, the lower the premium.
Refer to LMI premiums :
  • New Loan Premiums
  • Standard Cover – Owner Occupier and Investment premium rates
  • Stamp duty rates – LMI premiums
  • Capitalised LMI

For all loans, LMI cover is valid for the duration of the loan contract (i.e. Portfolio loan), or the expiration of the loan term (i.e. residential principal and interest loans, or residential Combination Loans) unless a variation to the contract’s terms and conditions are agreed to, which may necessitate an additional premium being payable.

For Re-location loans the period of cover applies for the Re-location period and will continue on the end debt situation if LMI cover is still required.
For Construction loans the period of cover will commence from the date of the first draw down (i.e. settlement for land) or the date of the initial loan establishment and/or drawdown and where payment of the LMI Premium is received. 
 

Changes to the loan contract - LMI premium

A further premium will apply where the loan is increased or the risk represented by the loan to the mortgage insurer changes.

This could occur where joint borrowers, who are jointly and severally liable under the loan contract, legally separate. A single borrower, albeit the primary borrower under the loan contract, may not represent the same risk to the mortgage insurer as did the joint borrowers under the original loan contract.

In these circumstances, a new premium (or an additional premium) may be required. 

Refer to LMI Premiums > Transfer of a mortgage insurance premium to a new loan
 

LMI premium refunds

Where a loan (that is subject to mortgage insurance) has been fully repaid and the mortgage discharged, a refund of LMI may be possible in some limited circumstances. The decision to refund a premium is at the discretion of the Insurer and is subject to meeting the refund policy as detailed below. The refund process will commence automatically, once the Bank notifies the Mortgage Insurer that the loan has been paid in full and the mortgage discharged. The Bank makes no decision about whether or not a refund is due.
The refund policy for Mortgage Insurance is as follows:
  • The loan is repaid in full and mortgage discharged
  • Where the policy has been in force for 12 months or less a refund of 40% of the premium may apply (net of stamp duty)
  • Where the policy has been in place for >12 months to <=24 months a refund of 20% of the premium may apply (net of stamp duty)
  • The minimum refund amount payable by WLMI is $400. NB Stamp duty paid is not eligible to be refunded
  • The minimum refund amount payable by Helia (formerly Genworth) is $500. NB Stamp duty paid is not eligible to be refunded
  • The minimum refund amount payable by WLMI-A is $400. NB Stamp duty paid is not eligible to be refunded
Note:
Situations whereby a LMI refund is not applicable
  • No Deposit loans - whether LEF or LMI was paid
  • There are additional loans still outstanding covered under the original LMI policy
  • Accounts that are in Collections or subject to a claim.

2.4 Duty of disclosure

A duty of disclosure is owed to the insurer under the Insurance Contracts Act.
It requires disclosure to the mortgage insurer of all matters known, or that could reasonably be expected to have been known, that are relevant to the Insurer’s decision as to whether to accept the risk of the Insurance, and if so, on what terms.
This duty also extends to any subsequent renewal, variance or reinstatement of the LMI contract.
Failure to comply with the duty of disclosure, may entitle the insurer to reduce its liability under the LMI contract in respect of a claim and or to cancel the contract.
If the non-disclosure is dishonest (made knowingly), the insurer may also have the option of avoiding the contract.
Also see Validation of information provided by the customer > Credit Bureau commitment mismatch (scenario table) > scenario 3 ‘The credit bureau report shows a facility but the borrower forgot they had the facility and did not declare it.’
 

2.5 When LMI is required

The need for LMI is determined by the loan to value ratio (LVR) applicable to the risk associated to the property offered as security, and or the borrower risk.
The lender is to determine whether LMI cover is required under the Bank’s credit policy before approval of the loan.
LVR policy (refer to Assessing Loan to Value Ratio (LVR) policy), identifies when LMI is required. The credit policy does not attempt to address the mortgage insurers’ requirements on the acceptability of the risk.
 
Cash out limit
The maximum cash out in a mortgage insured loan is $100,000.
Where the cash out request is greater than $100,000, applications may be referred to Credit on a case by case basis for support.  If supported by Credit, the application will be sent to Mortgage Insurers for consideration of approval.
For acceptable ‘cash out’ purposes refer to Eligibility policy >  Loan Purpose

LMI is not available
LMI is not available for loans secured by non-residential security property, e.g. commercial real estate. 
 

2.6 LMI approval - new loans

LMI may be approved either:
  • within open policy, or
  • outside open policy by authorised LMI DUA Holders, or
  • by MI underwriters, for loan applications outside open policy parameters
Note: Mortgage insurers have their own set of acceptance criteria based on the level of risk they are prepared to undertake, and it may vary from the Bank’s credit policy for acceptable risk.


Postcodes requiring referral to Mortgage Insurer for consideration

Refer to Assessing Loan to Value Ratio (LVR) policy > Assessing LVR by Security > Mortgage Insured Postcode referrals for details of impacted postcode locations.
 

2.7 LMI premiums

2.7.1 New loans premiums

Payment of LMI premiums

LMI premiums will be deducted from the loan amount at settlement or initial advance (Building Loans).
 

Calculation of LMI premium

The below formula for calculating LMI Premiums is provided for information purposes only:
Loan Value x Effective Rate
+ Stamp Duty
+ (GST- Reduced Input Tax Credit) [zero where the provider is internal to the Group, e.g. WLMI]
= Total payable by customer 

The minimum premium is $600 plus Stamp Duty.

Maximum LVR availability is as per Assessing Loan to Value Ratio (LVR) and LVR calculation and Exceeding maximum LVR
  
Note:
Where the maximum LVR allowable is exceeded to cover the cost of the LMI premium, use the applicable resultant LVR to assess the appropriate premium. e.g If LVR 92% for a P&I loan use the >89 to 90% LVR premium relevant for the loan type for new customers; if LVR is 97% for a P&I loan use the >94% to 95% LVR premium relevant for the loan type for existing customers.

2.7.4 Add-ons / loan increases premium calculation

Loan increases or further advances using existing security (and where LMI has been previously paid) will need referral to WLMI for manual calculation of the premium.

If the term of the loan is extended, a new contract will be issued for the additional amount. The premium is calculated on the total exposure less the amount already previously paid. 

Example LMI Premium Calculation:  Customer’s outstanding loan amount is $500,000.00 and the new loan amount is $600,000.00. The LMI is calculated on the total exposure amount ($600,000.00) by the LMI Rate based on the LVR.  The total premium is calculated to be $5,160.00.  As the customer has previously paid an LMI premium of $3,100.00, this is credited, resulting in a total LMI premium payable of $2,060.00. 

Note:
The minimum premium is $600 plus Stamp Duty.
Credit should be contacted if any queries.

 

2.7.5 Transfer of a mortgage insurance premium to new loan

A mortgage insurance premium may be transferred to a new loan when the existing loan is being paid out and the current LVR and loan balance is not increasing.
This may occur when:
  • there is a transfer from joint to single name or vice versa
  • the new loan is used to payout existing St.George Bank Group facilities
All loans involving transfers, where LMI was paid on existing loan and LMI is required on new loan, are to be referred to WLMI for calculation of premium. 

Note:
The minimum premium is $600 plus Stamp Duty.
 

2.7.6 Capitalised LMI

What is Capitalised LMI?

Capitalised LMI allows the LMI premium amount (including stamp duty) to be added to the gross loan amount*, and thereby extend the LVR above the "normal" maximum LVR for the applicable product or policy.
 
The LMI premium amount is calculated on the loan amount without LMI, and is then added to the loan amount.
 
Primarily, capitalised LMI is used when a customer with a loan on or near the maximum LVR would like to include the LMI premium in the borrowed funds. Policy limits are still applied to the loan amount that does not include LMI, but the Capitalisation process allows that LVR maximum to be exceeded by the LMI premium (although there is a ceiling on the maximum LVR, including Capitalised LMI, that can be accepted - refer below).  This is referred to as the “Post-Cap LVR”.
 

What loans can be capitalised

Capitalised LMI can be offered to customers with the following products:
  • Standard or Negotiated variable rate loan
  • Standard or Negotiated fixed rate loan
  • Basic home loan 
*Notes:
Capitalisation is allowed for these products however the total LVR, including the capitalised LMI, cannot exceed the normal product maximum
All other loan products (including loans with Family Pledge) are not eligible for Capitalised LMI.
 

Capitalisation exceptions

Please be aware that capitalisation of LMI is not available in all circumstances.
  • For any proposals >$1.5m capitalisation is not available.
  • Capitalisation will be considered on a case by case basis where proposals are outside Open Policy to a maximum loan amount of $1.5m.
  • Where capitalisation is required for the loan to be able to proceed (i.e. insufficient funds are available to complete without LMI capitalisation) the matter should be referred to the WLMI underwriters prior to a conditional / approval in principal being provided to the Broker or customer to ascertain whether capitalisation is available.
