1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy?

 This policy will help you determine the maximum loan term when assessing a residential loan application.
Generally, the maximum loan term is determined on the applicant's ability to repay without their incurring substantial hardship.
Refer to individual Product Specifications for any additional loan term restrictions or requirements that may apply to specific products.
If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, refer to Lenders mortgage insurance (LMI) where required.
 
  

2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Assessing maximum loan term

 The standard maximum term for an amortising loan (i.e. principal and interest (P&I) repayment) is 30 years, however this will depend on the applicant’s age.  For details regarding maximum Interest Only terms refer to the table Assessing maximum loan terms for interest only (IO) products .
When assessing loan applications and structuring the loan term specific consideration should be given to the applicant’s remaining time in the workforce. The applicant’s ability to retire or service the new debt post retirement age must be considered, without their incurring substantial hardship
The ability to meet repayment obligations and clear the debt may be from:
  • Income (including retirement income), or
  • Asset equity (including the sale of an asset)
 

2.2 Exit Strategy – Responsible lending

During the Requirement and Objective (R&O) conversation, where any borrower is 45 years or older at the time of application or will reach 75 years during the term of the credit contract, the mortgage broker must:
  • Make further enquiries to find out:
    • When the applicant intends to retire (retirement age); and
    • Where retirement is likely to be prior to the completion of the requested loan term (i.e. their intended retirement age falls during the loan term or they reach 75 of age during the loan term), what is their strategy for servicing and / or repayment of the debt following retirement.
Note: The borrower may use a combination of more than one strategies.
  • The maximum loan term cannot be extended past the borrower’s retirement age unless an appropriate exit strategy is established.
  • Refer to Credit Processes for Record Keeping Requirement under Responsible Lending for further details.
  • Complete verification activities required under Verifying sources of exit strategy
If customer is already retired at the time they take the loan out and serviceability is calculated on their retirement income, we do not need to capture retirement strategy. Comments and the answer will be N/A.

Refer to Guidelines for Examples of Acceptable and Non-compliant Responses to Retirement Strategy questions for further details.
 

2.2.1 Exit strategy exclusions

  • Relocation loans with nil end debt do not require an exit strategy as serviceability is not required to be demonstrated.
  • Guarantors do not require an exit strategy as the Responsible Lending obligation is to capture the borrower’s exit strategy, not the guarantors.
 

2.3 Verifying sources of exit strategy

  • Verification of the exit strategy is to be completed when the proposed strategy is through:
    • Non-real estate asset, and/or
    • Real estate asset
  • When the borrower is relying only on the above strategies (individually or in combination), the associated asset values must be greater than or equal to the amortised loan amount for the loan being requested at the intended retirement age.
  • Where the borrower is proposing to replace a real estate asset with one of a lower security value (i.e. downsizing of property), the equity position will need to consider both the repayment of the debt and purchase of new real estate.
Where a combination of strategies will be used, the details of each strategy must be documented.
 

2.3.1 Document standards

The documents used for verification must:
  • comply with specific rules set out in this policy
  • comply with Document Standards
  • comply with the Minimum Document Standards Matrix

2.4 Assessing maximum loan term for interest only (IO) products

 
Category Policy rule
Interest only (IO) repayment term
  • The maximum interest only repayment period is determined by the purpose of the loan as follows:
  1. Owner occupied – the interest only repayment period may be for a minimum of 1 year up to a maximum of 5 years.
  2. Investment (excluding SMSF home loan) – the interest only repayment period may be for a minimum of 1 year up to a maximum of 10 years.
Note: SMSF Home Loans – interest only repayments are no longer permitted for new loans. Any IO term commitments pre 31 July 2018 will be honoured.
  • The contract loan term will be the total of the interest only period plus the P&I period – the maximum total term is 30 years.
    Note: This does not apply for Building loans as this should exclude the 24 month construction period (i.e. the interest only period allowable to complete construction) when calculating maximum loan term in relation to serviceability assessment. 
These loans are to have a minimum loan term of the requested interest only repayment period and at least a 1-year principle and interest term.
For example: if a customer has requested a 5 year interest only loan, then the minimum total loan term must be for at least 6 years (5 year interest only then 1 year principle and interest repayment). The overall term (total loan term) must be greater than the interest only term.
  • An interest only repayment term can be a combination of loan terms i.e. 2 years IO then another 3 years IO up to the product maximum.  Prior to the second IO repayment period being approved, a new serviceability assessment is required to demonstrate the customer’s ability to repay the debt over the reduced remaining P&I repayment period. 
  • After the maximum interest only term expires, i.e. 5 years for Owner Occupied and 10 years for Investment loan, the loan must be converted to P&I repayments.
  • For an existing owner occupied or investment loan, any requests for extension beyond the maximum interest only repayment period will require the loan to be re-originated. This ensures that our current policy and product requirements, and maximum risk appetite for interest only repayment periods, are complied with.
Relocation loans The relocation period is 6 months for non-TBE loans and 12 months for TBE loans

4. Guidelines

 

4.1 Examples of acceptable and non-compliant responses to retirement strategy questions

Category Guidelines
Examples of appropriate comments (this is not an exhaustive list)
  1. ‘The customer plans to pay off the loan before their retirement age and currently has enough surplus income to do this.’
  2. ‘The customer has or will have income from other investments that can be used to service the debt following retirement. Details of the other investments are […].’
  3. ‘The customer has and will continue to have income from superannuation that can be used to service the debt following retirement. Their current superannuation balance is $[...].’
    As long as the total in superannuation supports the loan and leaves enough for the customer to live off, this is acceptable.
  4. ‘The customer currently has recurring rental income of $[…] per week that I have verified; this can be used to service the debt following retirement.’
  5. ‘The customer has other assets that they can sell to pay the remainder of the loan amount, without the risk of any financial hardship. Details of the other assets are […].’
  6. ‘The customer is an SMSF individual trustee who purchased the property as a superannuation investment. They advised that the property will be sold when contributions can no longer be made.’
  7. ‘The customer is proposing to replace a real estate asset with one of a lower security value (downsizing of property). The owner occupied property is [….]’
Examples of responses which are not compliant (this is not an exhaustive list)
  1. ‘The customer plans to retire during the loan term but that in all likelihood they will probably get an inheritance before they retire, so they should be able to repay the loan.’
  2. ‘Customer plans to win the lottery!’
  3. ‘Customer says: “If something bad happens to me financially, my insurance should cover everything.’
  4. ‘Exit strategy is evident based on customer’s financial position and accounts held with us.’ 

4.2 Examples of acceptable and non-acceptable use of Superannuation lump sum as exit strategy

  #     Scenario When customer declares that they intend to use Superannuation as an Exit Strategy during R&O.
Does the borrower need to be retiring at age 67 or more (Government Age Pension age)?
Please note: all scenarios will require a retirement age that meets super preservation age.
  1  Customer indicates they will be retiring at 65yrs.
The loan is secured against an investment security only.
The customer also owns (unencumbered or mortgaged) an owner occupied residence not offered as security		 No. The 67yrs requirement doesn’t apply as customer will have 2 real estate properties upon loan settlement (i.e.  new investment property and existing OO).
 
  2 Customer indicates they will be retiring at 62yrs.
The loan is secured against an investment security only.
The customer does not hold / own any owner occupied residence (i.e. they are renting)		 Yes. The 67yrs requirement is applicable here as customer only has 1 real estate property upon loan settlement. 
Given customer indicates they plan to retire at 62yrs the use of superannuation as an exit strategy is not allowed as per policy. Lender would be required to have further discussion with customers to explore other sources of exit strategy.
  3             Customer indicates they will be retiring at 66yrs.
The loan is secured against an owner occupied primary security and investment security.		 No. The 67yrs requirement doesn’t apply as customer will have 2 real estate properties upon loan settlement (i.e. investment property and OO).
 
  4 Customer indicates they will be retiring at 65yrs.
The loan is secured against an owner occupied security only. The customer has other non –real estate assets (savings, shares) but no other real estate assets.  		Yes. The 67yrs requirement is applicable here as customer only has 1 real estate property upon loan settlement.
Given customer indicates they plan to retire at 65yrs the use of superannuation as an exit strategy is not allowed as per policy.
Lender would be required to have further discussion with customers to explore other sources of exit strategy.
  5 Customer indicates they will be retiring at 62yrs.
The loan is secured against an owner occupied primary security and owner occupied secondary security
The customer has no assets other than super and the above properties		 No. The 67yrs requirement doesn’t apply as customer will have 2 real estate properties upon loan settlement (i.e. primary and secondary OO properties).
 
 
  6 Customer indicates they will be retiring at 62yrs.
The loan is secured against an owner occupied secondary security only (holiday home).
The customer also holds another owner occupied residence (i.e. their primary place of residence)		 No. The 67yrs requirement doesn’t apply as customer will have 2 real estate properties upon loan settlement (i.e. primary and secondary OO properties).
