18.1 LVR calculation – Loan to value ratio (LVR)

 
The Loan to Value Ratio (LVR) is calculated and expressed as a percentage by dividing the loan amount by the lower of either the purchase price or the Security Value (refer Assessing Residential Security Value > About this policy > What are acceptable real estate valuation methods except where detailed below.
 
Exceptions
In the following circumstances the Security Value may be used to calculate the LVR:

 1. Purchase price is either not available or not applicable, eg:
  • second mortgages (behind other financial institutions)
  • add-on loans/increases/additional loans
  • dual/multiple securities (existing plus new security)
  • refinances
  • favourable purchase (preferential purchase price) for sales between family members
  • favourable purchase (preferential purchase price) other than between family members - subject to prior referral to and final acceptance of such proposals by Credit
  • land value for Building Loans where the residence is being erected on land owned by the applicant, ie. the purchase price of the land may not be applicable (refer full details below).
  • The original date of the contract of sale is more than 3 months old and the loan amount does not exceed the purchase price stated on the contract of sale.
 
2. Building loans
  • Land already owned by the applicant
For Building loans where the applicant owns the land, the purchase price is not applicable. In these instances the valuation amount for LVR calculation is to be based on the valuation amount of the land plus the lower of either the cost (tender price) or the 'on completion' value of the Building loan improvements.

 
  • Land purchase and house construction
Where the land is being purchased as part of the Building loan application, normal policy to apply i.e. use the lower of the 'on completion' valuation or purchase price (sum of land purchase price plus cost to construct).

 
3. Multiple residences
Where more than one residence is to be erected eg duplex, villas, town houses, the LVR will be based on the lower of either the cost or the 'in line' valuation amount.
   Note:
The 'in-line' valuation will reflect the 'on completion' valuations less a discount a willing buyer would expect to receive for purchasing the whole of the property development.
Any 'on completion' valuations for each residence are not to be used to calculate the LVR.
 

18.5 Exceeding maximum LVR

 
For Owner Occupied loans:
The maximum LVRs inclusive of any capped mortgage insurance premium is not to exceed 95%.  Noting that in some cases (as identified in Assessing Loan to Value Ratio (LVR) > Rules ) the maximum base LVR is set at 90%,  when this occurs, the cost of Lender's Mortgage Insurance (LMI) premium may be capitalised to a maximum of 2% over and above the 90% base LVR . This means that, when the maximum base LVR is 90% as per policy and where LMI is being capitalised, the total maximum LVR will be 92%.
 
For Investment loans:
When a new loan or increase is originated and all security is Non-owner occupied the maximum LVR available is 90% inclusive of any capped Mortgage Insurance premium.

Note:
The loan amount minus the LMI premium is to meet LVR policy. This is defined as the net loan amount.
 
Serviceability / LVR / LMI premium
Normal serviceability and LVR policy will apply i.e. serviceability and LVR will be assessed on the loan amount including the LMI premium.
The LMI premium band is based on loan amount (excluding capitalised LMI premium) LVR. This is defined as the gross loan amount.
