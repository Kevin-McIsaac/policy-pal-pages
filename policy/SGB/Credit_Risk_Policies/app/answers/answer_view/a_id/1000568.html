1. About this policy

1.1 Why this policy

 This policy will give you an overview of all acceptable guarantee scenarios, including the Family Pledge Home Loan Option, and the requirements for each scenario.

1.2 What is a guarantee?

A guarantee is a contractual promise given by a third party, i.e. the guarantor, who agrees to have a liability to pay for the loan if the customer(s) is/are unable or unwilling to repay the loan.

A Security guarantor is when the borrower will not or does not own 100% of the security property offered to support the loan. The guarantor is providing security to support the loan only and not providing income to boost loan serviceability.  
 

1.3 Background - Guarantees

The National Credit Code (NCC) and the Banking Code of Practice (BCoP) provide protections to individuals who enter into a guarantee as security for a loan.

The following is summary of the broad principles regarding personal guarantees:
For details of BCoP requirements relating to Guarantees, refer to Banking Code of  Practice (BCoP).
  • In all instances, regardless of product, whenever a guarantee is to be taken, we require guarantors to obtain independent legal advice before entering into a guarantee. The completed Guarantor’s Solicitor’s Certificate is to be provided to us prior to settlement (i.e. release of loan funds) to evidence that independent legal advice has been received. Waiving of independent legal advice may only be approved by Consumer Bank Credit / Group Credit on exception basis.
  • A guarantor's income cannot be used to assist in the assessment of the borrower’s ability to service the new loan. The borrower must be able to service the loan on their own, or by virtue of a company or trust completely controlled by the borrower alone.
  • In all instances, prospective guarantors are to be informed that they need to make any appropriate enquiries about the credit worthiness, financial position and integrity of the borrowers. This should be pointed out at application and again at approval. (The prospective guarantors are reminded of this prior to signing the Guarantee and Indemnity.)
 If the loan application requires mortgage insurance, you may also need to refer to  Lenders mortgage insurance (LMI) for further information.
 

2. Rules you must apply

 2.1 Acceptable guarantors and guarantee scenarios

For Consumer Lending policy, the Bank requires a guarantor to be an individual, an entity cannot be a guarantor.
It is contrary to Bank’s policy to add a borrower and or guarantor for the purpose of increasing serviceability.
 
In some scenarios the guarantor of a loan may share common debts with the borrower. In the first instance test if the borrower can service 100% of the common debt without the guarantor’s income. If the borrower cannot service 100% of the common debt, and the Security Guarantor will also need to act as a Common Debt Reducer, refer to Serviceability Assessment > Common Debt Reducer (CDR) for additional requirements.   
 
Where the Security Guarantor is not a Common Debt Reducer, income verification is not required, and the Security Guarantor must not be included in the serviceability assessment.
 
Note: A Credit Bureau check is not to be completed on a security guarantor.

2.2 Guarantee amount

Apart from the Family Pledge Home Loan option, which can be limited to an agreed amount lesser than the full loan amount, all other security guarantees will require a guarantee for the full loan amount.
 
Note: Under NCC a guarantee can secure an amount that does not exceed the sum of the borrower’s liabilities, plus reasonable expenses of enforcing the guarantee, or any lesser amount agreed between us and the guarantor. BCoP also requires a guarantee to limit the guarantor’s liability.

 2.3 Unacceptable Guarantors

 The following are unacceptable guarantors:
  • Non-residents (refer to Non-resident Policy)
  • Temporary Australian residency visa holder (refer to Migrant Lending Policy )
  • Entities
  • Unsupported guarantees (apart from a director’s guarantee when the company is borrowing)
  • Director’s guarantee from an unrelated company
  • Any guarantee structure not identified as acceptable in this policy
  • Also refer to Personal Guarantee Scenarios > Unacceptable scenarios.

2.4 Guarantor interview

 The guarantor must be interviewed separately in the absence of the borrower.

2.5 Independent Legal Advice 

We require a guarantor to obtain independent legal advice to understand their obligations and liability under the guarantee.

2.6 Accepting a guarantee - 3 day requirement

The guarantor must be provided with the following:
  • The terms and conditions of the guarantee
  • Any notice of demand we have made on the borrower in the past two years
  • A copy of the borrower’s loan contract
  • A copy of the borrower’s credit report
  • A copy of the borrower’s financial accounts or statements held in the last two years for the guaranteed loan.
  • A copy of any other information the guarantor reasonably requests about the guaranteed loan.
We must not accept the returned, signed guarantee document before 3 days has passed. The intention is to allow the guarantor time to consider the guarantee they are about to provide.

Exceptions to the 3 day rule are as follows:  
  • If the guarantor has obtained independent legal advice and provided evidence
  • If the guarantor is acting as trustee for a trust on the loan application
  • If the borrower is a company and the guarantee provided is from the company director. 
     

    2.7 Security Guarantor

     
    A Security guarantor is required when the borrower will, or does not, own 100% of the security property offered to support the loan.  For SBG, the most common Security Guarantee type is the Family Pledge Home Loan option, however a security guarantee from the borrower’s spouse is also permitted.
     
    A Security Guarantee from a co-owner of an existing real estate property, that is not spousal, is outside a lender’s authority to approve. However, where suitable mitigating reasons can be presented to support consideration, this may be referred to Credit for exception consideration on a case by case basis. 
     
    Unless the Security Guarantor is also a Common Debt Reducer, income verification is not required, and the Security Guarantor must not be included in the serviceability assessment. When the Security Guarantor is acting as a Common Debt Reducer then refer to Serviceability Assessment>Common Debt Reducer (CDR).
     
    Note: Apart from Family Pledge guarantees, all other Security Guarantees will require a guarantee for the entire loan amount. A Family Pledge Guarantee is limited to a specified amount.
    For further information see Security – Family Pledge Home Loan option.
 

3. Family Pledge Home Loan Option

3.1 Overview - Family Pledge Home Loan Option

The Security Guarantee, known as a Family Pledge, allows other family members with equity available in their own property, to help customers cover the shortfall between the security value extended against the borrower’s property and the proposed loan amount.
It will be a single loan or a Flexible Choice (multi-sequence) loan, secured by the borrower’s property as the primary (1st) security in all cases, with a limited, Security Guarantee (“Family Pledge”) from acceptable family members, supported by a registered first mortgage over the family member’s property or a registered second mortgage.
Note: When the Family Pledge (limited supported guarantee amount) is insufficient to bridge the gap between the loan amount sought by the borrower(s) and the value extended against the borrower's security property, the application may be considered for mortgage insurance. Refer to Lender’s Mortgage Insurance - Family Pledge Home Loan Option .
 

3.2 Acceptable Family Pledge Guarantors - Family Pledge Home Loan Option

Acceptable Guarantors for the Family Pledge Home Loan option are:
  • Parents and step-parents or legal guardians,
  • Siblings and step-siblings or
  • Sons and daughters and step-children.

3.3 Un-acceptable Family Pledge Guarantors - Family Pledge Home Loan Option

 All other family members and non-family members are not eligible to provide a Family Pledge guarantee, including but not limited to: 
  • Uncles and Aunts
  • Grandparents
  • Non-resident family member (refer to Non-resident Policy )
  • Temporary residency visa holder family members (refer to Migrant Lending Policy )
  • Non-family members
  • Entity (company), including the entity of an acceptable guarantor or trust.
Uncles, Aunts and Non-family members may only be considered on an exception basis by Credit. Strong mitigating reasons would need to be provided for Credit to consider an exception request. Prior to submitting an application to credit, you should first contact Credit and discuss your customer’s particular circumstances to see if the application will be able to proceed.
 

3.4 Eligibility - Family Pledge Home Loan Option

The following eligibility criteria must be met for Parental Guarantee / Family Pledge:
  • Family member is an acceptable Guarantor 
  • Purchase or refinance of:
    • An owner occupied property, where the borrowers do not have ownership of more than one other property at time of application and the already owned property does not have sufficient equity to provide security for the new purchase or refinance (without incurring LMI).
    • An Investment property, where the borrowers do not have ownership of any other property at time of application.
Note: Ownership rules apply at time of application. The percentage of ownership does not impact the above policy rule.
  • The proposed loan amount may only include the purchase price or, refinance amount and the construction costs and/or direct up-front borrowing expenses. If customers A & B jointly purchase a property and subsequently a relationship breakdown occurs resulting in the property being transferred to A or B singularly, we can consider providing finance to either A or B with a new Family Pledge being taken. This is subject to normal loan assessment conditions being met and the existing joint debt being repaid.
  • Construction or home improvements when undertaken by a licensed builder and where controlled under progressive drawdowns.
  • Increases / top ups to an existing home loan with a Family Pledge security guarantee when the increase will be used to renovate the home, and this will be controlled under progressive drawdowns.  The Family Pledge limited guarantee amount is not to be increased. Note: Progressively advanced loans will require a separate sequence  to be added to the original loan.
  • Individual borrowers are restricted to a maximum of one parental guarantee / family pledge borrowing.
  • No single guarantee exceeds more than 50% of the guarantor’s security value.
  • Serviceability is demonstrated by the borrowers only and no income is taken from the guarantor(s).
  • The guarantee is to be supported by a 1st or 2nd mortgage other than:
    • Where the existing 1st mortgage is a reverse mortgage styled facility; or
    • Where security is a 2nd mortgage and LMI is applicable and the 1st mortgage is not held by St.George Bank Group (StG / BSA / BoM)
  • Only one property is offered by the guarantor as security guarantee however that property is used for more than one Family Pledge option (e.g. property offered as guarantee to support other siblings’ loans). Vacant Land is acceptable as the Family Guarantor security subject to meeting acceptable security policy requirements. 
 Scenarios which do not meet the eligibility criteria previously outlined are unacceptable for new or existing Family Pledge Home Loan Option, including but not limited to:
  • Where a Family Pledge is being added to an existing home loan
  • Loans where the purpose involves an owner builder
  • Refinance of any debt other than that listed in above eligibility criteria (e.g. non-mortgage debt consolidation of any personal debts such as credit card or personal loans into the loan amount).  Exceptions to this policy condition are not permitted
  • Cash out 
  • Access to equity in the borrower’s property for the purpose of providing supporting security to another loan (via cross collateralisation or debt allocation/debt apportioning) while a security guarantee is in place.  If equity has been established in the borrower’s property, the borrower(s) are encouraged to release the security guarantee / guarantor’s security property (for more information refer to Releasing a security guarantee).  Once the security guarantor is released, application processing can continue as per standard policy for non-Family pledge home loan option and debt allocation may be considered.


3.5 Refinance existing land to build - Family Pledge Home Loan Option

Where the customer initially purchases vacant land with a Family Pledge Home Loan option, but they do not currently have a build contract in place, a refinance of the land loan may be undertaken at a future date to permit the construction of a dwelling on the land.
Where LMI is applicable, vacant land security will only be acceptable for the Borrowers security where we are also providing a construction loan (either as full approval or AIP held in the system).
 
For an internal refinance of an existing land loan.
A new family pledge will need to be prepared at the time the internal refinance occurs to allow for the increased borrowing amount and the increased Family Pledge amount.

3.8 Lender’s Mortgage Insurance (LMI) - Family Pledge Home Loan Option

The intent of the Family Pledge Home Loan option is to allow customers to maximise the amount they can borrow, without the requirement for LMI.
In the event the limited supported guarantee is insufficient to bridge the gap between the loan amount sought by the borrower(s) and the value extended against their security property, the application may be considered for mortgage insurance.
Loan purpose may be investment or owner occupier.
The following criteria must be met:
  • Borrowers will be required to meet normal LMI approval conditions, which include but are not limited to, verifying income to MI standards.
  • Subject to MI standards being met, applications require referral to Credit.
  • Capitalisation of LMI premium is not available for Family Pledge Home Loan Option.
  • The guarantor must have sufficient equity in their property such that LMI would not be required in order to support the limited guarantee being provided. Ie the Borrowers debt solely is to be insured.
  • The following calculation is used to determine the LVR with a first mortgage guarantee. Borrower’s new loan amount divided by the borrower’s security value plus the limited guarantee.  
  • LMI is not available where the guarantor is providing a 2 nd mortgage in support of the limited guarantee (where the first mortgage is not held by St.George, BankSA or Bank of Melbourne).
    NB. Westpac and RAMS are considered other financial institutions in terms of this policy, assess in terms of 2 nd Mortgage.
  • Family Pledge home loans where LMI is required, borrowers need to satisfy the 5% genuine savings requirement as per normal policy - refer to Verifying Genuine Savings and Customer Contributions .
  • Vacant land as the borrowers security is only available in conjunction with a progressive draw construction loan (construction portion may be submitted for Approval in Principle)
  • Vacant Land is acceptable as the Family Guarantor security subject to meeting acceptable security policy requirements.
     



 

4. Director’s Guarantee

 

A director’s guarantee is a personal guarantee from the director of a company. This is required when the company is entering into a credit contract because a director is not held personally liable for the debts of the company. In the event the company is not able to pay the debt, the director under the director’s guarantee will be personally liable for the debt.

A director’s guarantee can be considered where

  • The company is the borrower, the security property offered is owned by the company and the loan requested is for the benefit of the company (i.e. purchase or refinance of an investment property owned by the company).

  • The borrower is a corporate trustee and is offering security to support the loan. A guarantee from the corporate trustee director(s) is required. (NB: If the corporate trustee is an SMSF or the loan is to purchase commercial property, then the loan is not suitable under consumer policy)

In all other cases that a director’s guarantee may be required, the loan application must be referred to Credit for consideration.




 

5. Trustee’s Guarantee

 
A trust cannot enter into a credit contract or offer security as it is not a legal entity. The trustee can enter into a credit contract or offer security in his or her capacity as trustee of the trust, if the Trust Deed empowers them to do so.
 
If the trustee is an individual, he or she is personally liable for the debts of the trust. A trustee’s guarantee is not necessarily required when the trustee is borrowing in his or her capacity as trustee (because they are personally liable for the debt anyway). . A trustee cannot give a mortgage over property held in trust to secure their personal debt.
 
When the trustee is a company then the directors of the company must provide a personal guarantee  because a director is not otherwise liable for the debts of a company.
 
The following documents are to be obtained by lenders and forwarded to Customer Mortgage Services (CMS) with the loan file together with the covering memorandum:
 
  • A certified copy of the stamped, dated and executed copy of the Trust Deed and any amending deeds
  • A list of current Adult Beneficiaries or Unit Holders;
  • Details of Security; (i.e. whether security provided is Trust Asset or not)
  • Purpose of Loan
  • Covering memorandum

 

6. Releasing a security guarantee

The release of the guarantor’s security property (including a family pledge guarantee) is not an event which is initiated by the bank, or which occurs automatically or at a predefined time during the loan term. Consideration to releasing the guarantor from the guarantee will occur at the borrower or guarantor’s request and will be subject to the bank’s consent at that time.

The Bank will consider requests to release of the guarantee (and related Mortgage) when:

  • The LVR, based on the Security Value of the borrower’s property and Total Security Exposure (TSE) does not require LMI (in terms of the prevailing policy at the time of the request). An updated valuation is required to determine the Security Value. 

  • Repayment history for all borrowers’ home loans to be satisfactory. 

  • Repayments on any of the borrower's unsecured loans (Credit Cards, Personal Loans) are not in arrears.

  • If the original application was not mortgage insured and the resulting LVR requires LMI, the application will require re-origination and will be subject to a full assessment and new documentation. The mortgage insurer will need to consider the borrower’s suitability. For maximum LVR’s with LMI refer to LVR table.

  • If the original application was mortgage insured and if LMI is still required, the borrower does have the option of paying the LMI premium based on the current LVR. 

The guarantee can be released at the borrower or guarantor’s request. The borrower or guarantor may request a revaluation of the borrower’s property at any time, subject to the payment of the requisite valuation fee at the time of the request. A short form valuation of the borrower’s security property will be required to confirm the LVR of the borrower’s loan. Providing the LVR is under the prevailing LMI threshold(s) or the required LMI premium is paid, the guarantee and the mortgage that supports it, can be released, providing the above conditions are met.

Yes (investment and owner occupied)

Regulated by National Credit Code (NCC):
Yes		 Guarantee required?
  • Each loan will require a guarantee from the other respective owners (ownership at the time of settlement)
  •  ‘A’ & ‘B’  will need to offer a guarantee to support borrowings of ‘C’ & ‘D’ supported by mortgage over the property and  ‘C’ & ‘D’ will need to offer a guarantee to support the borrowings of ‘A’ & ‘B’ supported by mortgage over the property.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

The following are common questions regarding Family Pledge Home Loan option.

Q1. Customer’s C & D want to buy an owner occupier property using the Family Pledge home loan option. Customer C & D already have 3 investment property loans. Are we able to proceed?
A1. No, the applicants exceed the maximum number of already owned properties.

Q2. The guarantor has an owner occupier property and an investment property. They only want to offer the investment property as security for the Family Pledge. However, this will result in the pledge against the Investment property exceeding 50% of its value. Can I proceed?
A2. This transaction would be outside of a lenders authority to approved, and you would need to discuss the application with your dedicated Credit contact. Credit may consider the transaction where sufficient mitigants exist.

Q3. Policy says that the applicants can have a property for own occupation if the applicants already have a property. Does that mean they can have one each and still get a Family Pledge?
A3. No. If the combined number of properties the applicants already have (partial or full ownership) is greater than one they are not eligible for a family pledge. The intent of the product is to help people enter the property market, not build an investment property portfolio.

Q4. If my customer is applying for a Family Pledge Home Loan option, can they also have mortgage insurance?
A4. The intent of the product option is to maximise the amount the applicant can borrow without the requirement for mortgage insurance. However in some cases where warranted and where the applicant qualifies mortgage insurance may be an option.

Q5. If the Family Pledge Home Loan option is being approved with Mortgage Insurance what LVR is available against both the guarantors property and the borrowers property?
A5. Mortgage insurance may be available for the borrower’s property, refer to Assessing Loan to Value Ratio policy for the maximum LVR applicable for the type of security property offered.  Mortgage insurance is not available for guarantor’s property.

Q6. The proposed guarantor(s) for the transaction are the applicant’s parents and grandparents. Is this acceptable?
A6. Grandparents are unacceptable guarantors. Refer to Unacceptable Family Pledge Guarantors

Q7. If the guarantor is the applicant step- mother or step- father and they are the sole owner of the guarantee property, is this acceptable. (i.e. the biological parent is not part of the transaction)
A7. Yes, this is an acceptable scenario.

Q8. Policy states that no single guarantee is to represent more than 50% of the guarantor’s security. What happens if the guarantor wants to help multiple children? Is the total sum of all of guarantees issued able to exceed 50% of the security value?
A8. This scenario can be considered. You will need to ensure you do not exceed the maximum LVR permitted against the guarantors security.

Q9. I’ve reviewed the Family Pledge Home Loan option, but my guarantor is unable to meet the criteria. Can I just take an unlimited guarantee from the guarantor to link in their security property?
A9. No, this is not an acceptable alternative. If the guarantor is unable to meet the eligibility criteria then the transaction cannot proceed. The guarantor is protected by the NCC and BCoP, which require a guarantee to be limited to the sum of the borrower's liabilities, plus reasonable expenses reinforcing the guarantee, or any lesser amount agreed between the bank and the guarantor.

Q10. Where the guarantor is offering a pledge supported by a Second Mortgage security, is this restricted to a major bank lender or are 2 nd or 3 rd tier lenders acceptable?
A10. The repayment of the debt is based on the strength of our applicant. The guarantor’s lender does not reflect on the credit worthiness of the applicant. Be aware that 2 nd mortgages are not permitted where the 1 st mortgage is a private mortgage provider or where the first mortgage is not held within the St George,/BOM / BSA (Westpac and RAMS are considered external mortgage).

Q11. My customer banks with another financial institution. They want to refinance to us and get some equity out of their property. Can we offer them a Family Pledge Home Loan option?
A11. No. Refer to Eligibility - Family Pledge Home Loan option 

Q12. My customer (A) is with SBG at present they have a variable rate, joint home loan with customer (B), but the relationship has dissolved, there is no Family Pledge in place today. Customer (B) is to be released from the borrowing and Customer (A) wants to refinance the security property and Home Loan into their own name. Customer (A) is able to service the loan in their own right, but they are asking if they can have a Family Pledge Home Loan option now. Is this possible?
A12. As per Eligibility - Family Pledge Home Loan option , this is an acceptable exception scenario.

Q13. My customers borrowed to purchase vacant land using the Family Pledge Home Loan option. The loan product they took was a fixed rate. They are now ready to borrow for the build. They need to increase the family pledge, but don’t want to refinance the existing debt because it would incur Break Costs. What do I do?
A13. Refer to Eligibility - Family Pledge Home Loan option

Q14. Can my customer increase their loan with an existing Family Pledge in place?
A14. There are limited scenarios where increases may be considered provide dthe existing family pledge is not increased, refer to Eligibility - Family Pledge Home Loan option .
