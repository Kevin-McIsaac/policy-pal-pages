2. Rules you must apply

2.1 Financial Situation Verification (FSV)

2.1.1 Overview – FSV

In order to meet our Responsible Lending obligations we need to determine that the credit contract will not be unsuitable for the borrower. The unsuitability assessment includes verifying the borrower’s financial situation.
For St.George Bank Group the financial situation verification includes:
  • Income document verification  
  • Serviceability assessment
  • Credit bureau enquiry and check
  • Commitment assessment
  • Checking for undisclosed Liabilities
 An approval in principle or formal approval can only be issued after the FSV check has been completed.
The below chapters provide additional information to assist with the completion of the FSV check:
  • Verifying Income
  • Serviceability assessment
  • Living Expenses
  • Commitments
  • Validation of information provided by the customer


2.1.2 FSV expiry

The FSV expiry date is linked to the loan purpose, where the purpose is for:
  • a combination of purchase where we are also taking the property as security and non-purchase* within a single clas application, the expiry period is 120 days; or
  • a purchase where we are taking the property as security, the expiry period is 120 days; or
  • a non-purchase*, the expiry period is 90 days
*A non-purchase includes refinances, limit increases, cashout or any other purpose where we are not financing the purchase of a property that we are also taking as security.
If the FSV is due to expire within 14 days or has already expired, a system FSV red traffic light will prevent the Assessor or Credit from:
  • running LDS and issuing either an AIP or formal approval; or
  • printing Loan Offer documents
 
To progress the application, all FSV assessment requirements must be re-verified, and FSV task re-completed (to reset the FSV expiry clock) before formal approval can be attempted.  Also refer to Overview - FSV and Changes to financial situation – FSV.
Note: There is no ability to over-ride FSV expiry.

 

2.1.3 Age of Documents - FSV

In all cases Age of documents are measured against the most recently completed FSV date.  Also see Document standards > Minimum documents standards matrix for additional information.

New supporting documents will be required where:
  • FSV has expired; or
  • FSV will expire in less than 14 days; or
  • When the AIP was issued the verification document was not available or did not meet policy requirements in terms of minimum information to be supplied; or
  • The document exceeded maximum age of document policy rules (before FSV has been completed and documents exception has not been approved by an appropriate DRA holder); or
  • Credit or the Mortgage Insurers have requested updated information to proceed to formal approval.
Also refer to Overview - FSV
 

2.1.4 Applications submitted via a 3rd party lodgement system – FSV

Where an application is submitted via a 3rd party lodgement system the FSV task will be a pre-completed task. This allows the assessment system to issue an unverified ‘approval in principle’ for eligible applications.
Once the application has been received by the bank, the documents submitted in support of the application are to be verified.
The assessor may manually reset the FSV task to ‘pending’ before re-completing the verification tasks and re-completing the FSV task to reset the FSV expiry clock.  If the task is not reset by the assessor, then the original FSV completion date linked to the date the application was submitted via the 3rd party lodgement system will be applied to the application.  Also see FSV expiry.
 

2.1.5 Changes to financial situation - FSV

Where FSV has expired or is due to expire within 14 days, FSV will need to be recompleted (refer to FSV expiry). 
The FSV task may also need to be recompleted where the applicant’s financial situation has changed. See below table:
Change Examples What do I need to do
Applicants – addition or deletion
  • Additional borrower is added to an application
  • Borrower becomes a guarantor or guarantor becomes a borrower

  • Obtain supporting documents for the new additional borrower (applicant 2) and complete FSV check for additional them
  • If there have been no changes to the original applicant(s) financial situation (e.g. applicant 1), then you do not need to obtain new documents for them.
  • Serviceability must be re-tested and Loan Decision System re-run
  • The original FSV expiry date will apply to the loan application. The FSV task is completed at application level (not per applicant)
Borrower removed

Assets decrease

  • Applicant 1 is working full time at the original credit decision date.
  • Applicant 1 changes from full time employment to casual employment.
  • Obtain new income documents
  • The FSV task must be manually set to ‘pending’
  • FSV can only be marked as ‘complete’ once  all required FSV tasks have been done.
  • Serviceability must be re-tested and Loan Decision System re-run
  • The new FSV completion date will apply to the loan application reset and recomplete the FSV task
Income reductions
  • Applicant 2 has social security income loaded in the assessment system.  During verification it is identified that the age of dependants does not meet verifying income requirements and the income is removed.
  • Rental income estimate is provided.  During the processing of the application the rental income amount is verified at a lower level.
  • If income is removed from serviceability assessment, no new documents required.
  • If income reduces, obtain evidence of the new income
  • Serviceability must be re-tested and Loan Decision System re-run

  • Serviceability must be re-tested and Loan Decision System re-run
  • If there are no other changes to the applicant’s financial situation, you do not need to obtain new supporting documents or reset and recomplete the FSV.
  • The most recently completed (original) FSV expiry date will apply to the loan application.
Increase in loan amount
  • Original credit decision is an AIP for a loan amount of $400k with property estimated value of  $500k. Applicants have now found a property valued at $550k and require a loan of $440k
  • If there are no other changes to the applicant’s financial situation, you do not need to obtain new supporting documents or reset and recomplete the FSV.
  • Serviceability must be re-tested and Loan Decision System re-run
  • The most recently completed (original) FSV expiry date will apply to the loan application.
Increase in liabilities
St.George Bank group or other financial institution (OFI)
  • Applicant has taken out 3 new visa cards since their original credit decision (AIP).

  • Re-run Loan Decision System
  • The new FSV completion date will apply to the loan application

    The customer estimates that the property is valued at $500k and based on the estimate our LVR is 80%.
  • When the valuation is completed the customer estimate was high and the actual value of the  property came in at $470k.  Our LVR is now 85%.
  • If there are no other changes to the applicant’s financial situation, you do not need to obtain new supporting documents or reset and recomplete the FSV
  • re-run Loan Decision System
  • The most recently completed (original) FSV expiry date will apply to the loan application.
Securities – deletion or addition
  • Customers had an approval in principal based on a loan amount of $400k for a property purchase price of $500k.  The address was not specified.
  • Applicants have now located a property to purchase and requested a loan amount of $440k with  a purchase price of $550k.  The new property details are added to ApplyOnline.  The property meets  standard acceptable security requirements.
  • If there are no other changes to the applicant’s financial situation, you do not need to obtain new supporting documents or reset and recomplete the FSV
  • re-run Loan Decision System
  • The most recently completed (original) FSV expiry date will apply to the loan application.
Note: Some of the above scenarios overlap with Pipeline reassessment rules.  If there has been a change to credit policy prior to Formal Approval being issued, you should refer to Pipeline Authority policy. 

 

2.2 Approval in Principle 

 
An Approval in Principle (AIP) letter can only be issued where all required verification tasks have been completed (including the Financial Situation Verification (FSV)).
The term “formal application date” refers to a point in time during the assessment of a loan application where we have completed tasks to determine if the loan is suitable for the customer. These tasks include verifying income and FSV.

In all cases Age of documents are tested against the FSV date, not the formal application date

Applications received via a 3rd party lodgement system – formal application date
  • Formal application date is the date the application was submitted via the 3rd party lodgement system. The submission must be accompanied by standard supporting documents sufficient to allow the assessment of the application and a credit decision outcome.
  • Credit  decision outcome may be AIP or Formal Approval.
Note: Also see AIP to Formal Approval or extending an AIP past the initial 90 day period

Approval in Principle issued
Where an AIP is issued, this becomes the formal application date. Age of documents will be tested against this date, even when the file is ready to be progressed to formal approval.

Where an Approval in Principle (AIP) letter is issued, it is valid for a maximum period of 180 days from when the AIP process was completed.
If the AIP has not progressed to a formal approval within 90 days and the applicants wish to maintain their AIP for a further 90 days (total 180 days) Brokers are:
  • To confirm that the applicants needs and financial situation have not changed since the time of application. A comment must be entered into the application (clas) confirming the borrower(s) and (if applicable) guarantor(s) have confirmed their financial position has not changed.
  • Ensure the financial situation verification check has not expired.
Refer to FSV expiry and AIP to Formal Approval or extending an AIP past the initial 90 day period

   

2.2.1 Imaging of supporting documents

Any documents used to complete assessment of the Approval in Principle (AIP) are to be imaged. 
  

2.2.2 AIP to Formal Approval or extending an AIP past the initial 90 day period

Following the issuance of an AIP and prior to consideration of formal approval revalidation of the borrowers financial position will be requried to ensure there have been no significant changes and reassessment of the applicant(s) will be required if:

  • The FSV task has expired, or
  • FSV is due to expire within 14 days; or
  • Other reassessment criteria have been triggered


AIP was issued by Credit or Mortgage Insurer
Where the original approval in principle was issued by Credit or a Mortgage Insurer the application is to be referred to them for formal approval.


 

2.3 Assessment period under Responsible Lending
 

2.3.1 Overview

Where an Approval in Principle (AIP) has previously been issued and the application is to be considered for formal approval, follow the requirements as per section   1.1 Approval in Principal. 
A formal credit assessment is made when all of the following have been completed satisfactorily:
  • Financial Situation Verification#
  • Verification of supporting documentation
  • Credit Policy applied via running a credit decision
  • Credit bureau enquiry obtained
  • Security assessed.
The successful outcome of a formal credit assessment is Formal Approval and the issuance of the Loan Offer document (credit contract) to the customer(s).
#An application will be unable to be formally approved where the FSV check has not been completed, FSV is due to expire within 14 days or has already expired, refer to FSV expiry.
 

2.3.2 Imaging of supporting documents

Any documents used to complete assessment and issue formal approval are to be imaged.
 

2.3.3 Unsupported loan types

Unsupported loan types include (but are not limited to):
  • Loans in a company name or with security owned by a company
  • Trustee borrowers
  • Building loans
  • Non – real estate security (this security type only permitted for substitution of security – short term - for further information refer to Assessing Acceptable Securities > Process to follow > Use of Term Deposit
  • Super Fund Home Loans  #product withdrawn from sale effective 31 July 2018
  • Capitalising portion of relocation loan
  • Pricing discretions greater than 1.6%
  • Increase to loans with an existing family pledge
 

2.3.4 Acceptance of loan offer document

For a loan offer to be valid, customers are required to accept and return the document to the Bank within 14 days of the date of Loan Offer.

Where the loan offer is signed, dated and noted in system as received by the Bank outside of the 14 days period, but the FSV 90 or 120 days has not expired, the Bank may proceed with the credit contract and notify the borrower of our acceptance.
Where the loan offer is signed, dated and noted as received by the Bank outside of the 14 day period and the 90 or 120 day FSV period has expired we must reverify the customer’s financial situation:
  • The bank can-not enter into the credit contract. If the customer still wishes to proceed, the loan will need to be reset and the loan must be fully re-assessed. If a new formal approval is granted, new Loan Offer Documents will be required. The new loan offer must be printed and provided to the applicant(s).   Credit are unable to authorise the use of an earlier LOD version number. 

    Note:
  • If the loan is Mortgage Insured, the application (including the valuation) may no longer meet requirements. Check before relying on existing records.
  • FSV task is unable to be marked as ‘pending’ and recompleted without changing the loan status from approved. 
Where the Loan offer has been accepted by both the customer and the Bank within the FSV expiry period:
  • The customer has up to 112 days from the formal approval date in which to settle / draw down the loan where the purpose of the loan is for a purchase, refinance, non-structural improvements without progress payments, equity release. After this time applications will expire / withdraw.
  

2.3.5 Loan offer expired

Where > 140 days has passed since Formal approval without the loan settling / drawing down, and where the purpose of the loan is for a purchase, refinance, non-structural improvements without progress payments, equity release, the existing file will withdraw.
If the customer still wishes to proceed, the loan will need to be reset and the loan must be fully re-assessed.
If the loan is Mortgage Insured, the valuation may no longer meet requirements. Check before relying on existing records.
If approval is granted, new Loan Offer Documents will be required.
 

2.3.6 Frequently asked questions - Assessment period under Responsible Lending

Q1. My customer is purchasing a property and settlement has been delayed. Although the customer accepted the LOD within 90 days, the proposed settlement date is >140 days since the original formal approval.  Credit told me I need to re-assess the application, complete verification requirements again and issue a new LOD. Why?
A1. After 140 days both the customer’s formal approval and loan offer documents have expired. Before we make a new offer to lend, we need to ensure the loan is still suitable for our customer.  We do this by getting new financial information and re-assessing the application.

Q2. If the customer is self-employed and the financials are still within age of document requirements, what am I supposed to be checking?
A2. While the age of the financials used for assessing affordability may still be acceptable, we need to confirm that our applicant has not sold the business (Eg the income stream the original assessment was based on is still relevant). 
If your investigations show that the original income stream is no longer available, you would need to see if the customer had other acceptable income which could be used to service the loan.
In the event the customer has no other acceptable income, then the application would be unable to proceed.

Q4. If I have a  formally approved an application, and the customer later changes their mind about which product they want to take, do I have to worry about re-assessing the application and completing the verification requirements again?
A4. Yes, when the change of product is made or you add the advantage package in the loan application, you need to re-complete the verification tasks before a new formal approval and LOD’s are issued to the customer (and including FSV check if it has expired).
For more information about formal application date refer to section 1.1 Approval in Principle .

Q6. If the application file has withdrawn, can I have the file and formal approval re-instated and rest on the original LOD’s?
A6. Where the LOD's have been returned but settlement was delayed and exceptional mitigating circumstances are identified, the request may be referred to Credit for consideration.  In addition, settlement is required to occur within 5 days of the application being re-instated to prevent the file being withdrawn a second time.
Where the LOD’s were not returned refer to Acceptance of loan offer document and FSV expiry
