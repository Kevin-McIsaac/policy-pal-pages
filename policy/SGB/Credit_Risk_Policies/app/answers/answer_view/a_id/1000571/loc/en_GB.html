 1. Purpose - Home Loan Application form

 

Purpose

The Home Loan Application Form has been designed to enable the capture of information relevant to the credit assessment of the loan. All sections are to be completed. All applications are to be signed and dated by the applicants (and guarantors, if any).  Applications are not permitted to be signed by a Power of Attorney acting on behalf of the applicant. Original signatures are to be obtained prior to funding the loan, and are to be retained in the loan file.
The information can be gathered using either the pre-printed form or via ApplyOnline input.
 

Customer Interview

The interview process is to be carried out without the presence of a third party, i.e. must be between the broker and applicant(s) / guarantor(s) only.
A formal face to face interview should be completed with the loan applicant(s) or guarantor(s), prior to acceptance of the loan offer.
 

Application processing

  • Prior to commencing any investigation, verification tasks or performing a Credit Reference enquiry :
For an application you must obtain either:
  1. a signed and dated Privacy Statement or;
  2. a signed and dated manual Home Loan application form, which includes the Privacy Authority,  
Note:
Effective 28/4/18 Finance brokers are unable to submit their own applications ie, where the finance broker submitting the application is also one of the applicants.  Brokers are still able to submit loans for their family (as long as they are not an applicant) and they are also able to have another finance broker within their group submit a loan on their behalf.


Retaining and Imaging of Documentation
Where an applicant has signed a manual Home Loan application form, which includes the Privacy consent, the signed manual application must be retained in the loan file
Note:
All supporting documentation provided by the customer must be reviewed to validate the information provided by the customer. This includes:
  • Confirming that there are no undisclosed liabilities or outgoings
  • There is no conflict with the information disclosed by the customer regarding marital status or dependants
  • There is no other information contained in the documents that conflicts with information disclosed by the customer on the application
 

2. Privacy statement – Home Loan Application form

 
All loan applications are to be accompanied by a signed and dated Privacy Statement, even though a credit reference check may not be required at loan application, it may become necessary to do a credit reference check during the life of the loan. Further, the Privacy Statement contains other authorities and acknowledgements permitting the Bank to contact employers, to provide and obtain information to credit reporting bureaus, other credit providers, LMI providers, brokers etc.
The Home Loan Application Form includes the Privacy Statement, which is incorporated in the Declaration section. A Privacy Statement is to be signed and dated by all applicants/guarantors before performing a Credit Reference check or proceeding any further with assessment.
 

3. Valid uses of a Power of Attorney – Home Loan Application form

 
The Power of Attorney (POA) must act in the best interests of the donor (person giving the Power of Attorney) and not in the best interests of the Attorney (themselves).
The St George Bank Group‘s obligation under The Privacy Act, advises that an Attorney can not sign a Home Loan Application form on behalf of a customer / donor where the applicant has capacity.

Brokers should also consider if the following policy is relevant to their applicant: Eligibility > Customers Experiencing Vulnerability

4. Business Purpose Declaration – Home Loan Application form

 
If the loan purpose established is wholly or predominantly (i.e. more than or equal to 50%) for business purposes then it is not eligible for a consumer product and must the referred to Business banking. Refer to Eligibility .
It is the broker's responsibility to determine whether a loan agreement is to be regulated or not under the National Credit Code. This is determined from loan purpose (not the security/s offered to support the loan).
As a guide the following questions may be used to assist lenders in determining whether the loan is regulated or not under NCC:
 
  1. Is this loan in a Company Name? (If YES, never regulate.)
  2. Will another property be the principal place of residence?
  3. Are the loan funds to be used for any business purposes and / or investment purposes other than investment in residential property?
  4. Are the loan funds to be used wholly or predominantly for business purpose and /or investment purposes other than investing in residential property?
Where the answer to questions 1,3-4 inclusive is YES, the loan may be unregulated.

Note:
If the loan purpose established is wholly or predominantly (i.e. more than or equal to 50%) for business purposes and / or for the purpose of investment other than in residential investment property and the Business Purpose Declaration is not signed and dated, it is assumed that the contract is regulated under the National Credit Code. This may result in misrepresentations being made to borrowers about potentially non-existent protection under the National Credit Code or vice versa.
This section of the Home Loan Application Form requires completion and signing by the proposed borrowers (not guarantors) when the loan applied for is for business purposes and / or for the purpose of investment other than in residential investment property. This is a requirement under the National Credit Code.

If there is some doubt about whether the loan should be regulated or unregulated, always regulate the loan.
