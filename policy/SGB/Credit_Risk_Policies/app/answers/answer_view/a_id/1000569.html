1. About this policy

 1.1 Why this policy?

  The Bank may consider waiver of the Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) requirement for certain eligible borrowers when the application satisfies the requirements as stipulated in Industry Specialisation Policy.
 

2. Rules you must apply

 2.1 Industry Specialisation Policy (ISP)

 

  2.1.1 Overview - ISP

 This policy sets out the parameters for using the Industry Specialisation Policy (ISP). Consumer mortgage proposals (originated via First Party Retail Lenders and Finance Brokers) can be accepted under ISP for certain qualifying professionals within Accounting and Legal industries.
Applications may be assessed for applicants who meet the “Industry, Occupation, Qualification and Income requirements” whereby the following allowable variances to standard policy may be applied.
  • LVR: a maximum LVR of 90% for maximum TAE# up to $4.0M may be approved without the need to obtain mortgage insurance. Refer to LVR policy allowable variance - ISP for further details; and
  • Genuine Savings: confirmation and validation of 5% genuine savings is not required for applications whereby mortgage insurance is not required.
All other prevailing standard non-mortgage insured policies for fully documented loans apply.
This policy is to be used as a guide. Approval is not guaranteed in line with the maximum offerings available under this policy.
This policy is available for application submitted for credit in the individual applicants own name, or related company or trust entities, provided direct ownership and/or directorship by the applicant(s) is evident.
There is no authority to extend this sector policy to other occupation types or products.  No Exceptions permitted.

#Maximum TAE (Total Approved Exposure) of $4.0m secured WBC Group (includes SGB/BOM/BSA/RAMS)exposure from loans with LVR > 80% without LMI.

2.1.3 Income verification - ISP

To be eligible for this policy, the qualifying professionals’ gross taxable income must be a minimum of 120,000 per annum.
Applicants earning foreign income are ineligible for this policy, as it exceeds the maximum LVR available for this income type.
Note:
If the application contains multiple qualifying professionals, the combined gross taxable income of the qualifying professionals must meet the minimum gross taxable income stated above. The income used to meet the income threshold requirement must be from the applicants' qualification. 
For PAYG and self-employed professionals, standard income and employment verification is required to be undertaken as per normal policy requirements. Refer to Verifying Income  or Self-employed applicants policy for further details.

2.1.5 Transfer of company debt to personal names in the consumer portfolio - ISP

"Company" debts are defined as those used for purchase or construction of residential and/or business premises.
Where it can be established that a personal debt was obtained in a company name with the outstanding debt appearing on the company's latest balance sheet, and the property securing that debt is held in personal name/s, requests that the facility/debt be transferred across to personal name/s will be accommodated.
Refinance of working capital, equipment finance and all other business related lending purposes are specifically excluded.
Lender is to verify the authenticity of the original transaction and provide appropriate commentary via the mandatory loan notes in the originated application.
 

2.1.6 Products and discounts available - ISP

Applicants can choose from the standard product range, eg Home Loans - Fixed or variable.
Discounted or negotiated interest rates are only available by the addition of the Advantage Package option to the requested residential lending product. Current advertised discounts apply.
Customers may qualify for Industry Specialisation Policy without the addition of Advantage Package. System constraints may make either the loan or borrower types ineligible for packaging.
 Brokers are not permitted to establish the loan with Principal and Interest repayment types and subsequently switch to Interest Only repayment types.
 

2.1.7 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ’s) - ISP

The following are common questions regarding Industry Specialisation Policy. If lenders are unsure how to proceed after reading the detailed policy and the below FAQ’s please contact your Credit Manager.
Q1: Why do applicants need to meet all 4 criteria i.e. Industry, Occupation, Qualification and Income requirements, to be eligible for Industry Specialisation Policy?
A1: The Policy has been developed to target a very specific group of professions and industries that have a strong risk profile, which provides comfort that the Bank can offer up to 90% LVR with no mortgage insurance requirement. All applicants must meet all eligibility requirement, no exceptions permitted.

Q2: Industry Specialisation Policy refers to Gross taxable income. What does this mean? What happens if the applicant is packaged e.g. Super plus car plus cash component?
A2: The qualifying portion of the income would be net of the super and car. So, if the gross figure is $140k and the gross taxable is only $110k, then they would not qualify.

Q3: If the applicant is a lawyer who is living and working in Abu Dhabi, would they be eligible for Industry Specialisation Policy?
A3: This applicant is not eligible for ISP as the policy only relates to applicants who are practicing in Australian States or Territories. Noting the legal profession and the law itself varies materially across different countries and jurisdictions, hence someone’s legal qualifications from an overseas country are not easily transferrable to Australia. In addition applicants earning foreign income are ineligible for ISP as the maximum available LVR is limited to 70% where foreign income is earned.


Q4: For the Accounting Industry segment within ISP to demonstrate the applicant has met qualification and employment criteria can I accept an email from Chartered Accountants Australia and New Zealand confirming membership, or am I required to obtain a certificate?
A4: Policy requests evidence of current membership, this may be confirmed by way of an email, generally this will be from ‘service@charteredaccountantsanz.com’.

5: Examples of applicants meeting the “Industry” eligibility requirement:
  • An accountant working in an accounting firm
  • An accountant working as an in house accountant for a trucking company
  • A lawyer working in a law firm
  • A lawyer working as an in house lawyer for a wine company.

Q6. As proof an applicant holds the relevant qualification, can I accept a receipt of annual membership subscription to the appropriate membership body?
A6. Yes, for all industry types (Accounting and Legal) you may accept a receipt issued to the applicant by the appropriate membership body indicating subscription payment has been made. The appropriate membership body for each acceptable industry type is as per Eligible Criteria .
Refer to Approval and LMI waiver processes - ISP for further details.

Q7. In calculating gross taxable income, can I include the qualifying professionals’ share of proposed or existing rental income?
A7. No, The income used to meet the income threshold requirement must be income from the applicants qualification.
