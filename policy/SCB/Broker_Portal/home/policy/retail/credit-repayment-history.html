Credit Repayment History

Why

Verification of credit repayment history is an important step to assess a customer’s ability to manage their finances and willingness to meet their commitments.

What

You must peruse all available information to assess a customer’s credit repayment history. Existing loans and accounts, with Suncorp Bank and elsewhere, are to be satisfactorily conducted, and credit bureau reports are to be satisfactory.

Definitions of “satisfactory” are provided via the links below.

When the customer provides account statements, check for missing pages which could indicate undisclosed transactions or poor/unsatisfactory conduct.

Any evidence of undisclosed debt or unsatisfactory credit repayment history should be investigated and assessed with mitigating comments included in your loan submission notes.

Loans will not be approved for undischarged bankrupts, as they are not permitted to borrow (see Borrowers & Guarantors).

Where you are aware that the customer is a discharged bankrupt, you must enquire and record:

  • Reason for bankruptcy,
  • Length of bankruptcy,
  • How much creditors were paid (fully or partially paid out and, if the latter, how much per dollar owed),
  • Reasons the customer is an acceptable credit risk if that is your conclusion.

Bankruptcy is usually an indication of higher risk and should put you on notice to make appropriate enquiries. However, it is not always a sign of higher risk, depending on individual circumstances, e.g. due to a divorce. Enquiries should be undertaken that will assist the assessment of the customer’s character and future credit worthiness.

Satisfactory Conduct

Satisfactory conduct means that the borrower’s existing repayment obligations have been comfortably met in the past 12 months (3 months for non-CCR credit cards), including demonstrating consistent repayment practices.

Conduct validation requirements as follows:

  • CCR reported facilities and non-CCR home/term loans/lines of credit: Ensure that in the prior 12 months (evidenced either by CCR report or documents), the repayment history confirms no more than one late repayment up to 29 days on any facility, including all facilities being refinanced. Reasonable enquiry regarding the cause of the late repayment is to be documented on the Brokers notes;
  • Non-CCR credit cards: Ensure that in the prior 3 months (evidenced by documents), the repayment history confirms no more than one late repayment up to 29 days on each facility, including any facilities being refinanced. Reasonable enquiry regarding the cause of the late repayment is to be documented on the Brokers notes;

Note: Validation of satisfactory conduct on non-CCR facilities is only required if the facility is being refinanced.

Late Repayments

Repayments on facilities are late when:

  • Term loans (including I/O) - the contractual repayment has not been met in full by the due date,
  • Other facilities (e.g. overdrafts and lines of credit) - the facility is outside of approved arrangements, including any approved temporary excesses.

Loan and Account Statement Requirements

Validation of credit repayment history can be established by reviewing either account statements, or where available, Comprehensive Credit Reporting.

Note: Documents for facilities being refinanced do not require confirmation of term remaining or interest rate, however, knowledge of the term of facilities being refinanced, if available, can be useful, e.g. a loan held for only a short time before being refinanced could be a warning sign. It is important to note that a payout confirmation letter is mandatory where the loan purpose is to refinance.

In the case of Novated Leases - a recent payslip confirming a deduction for the payment may be accepted. If possible, the remaining term of the lease should be obtained from the customer and noted on the loan application.

In the case of Buy Now Pay Later facilities either a document or Comprehensive Credit Reporting which confirms the limit/debt amount is required. In the absence of a facility limit, the debt amount should be used.

The documents you will need to validate satisfactory loan and account conduct is set out in the Submission Checklist and Definition Guide (PDF).

Satisfactory Report

Credit bureau reports will be obtained and reviewed for all customers and guarantors prior to making a decision on a loan application.

A satisfactory credit bureau report is to:

  • Be initiated by Suncorp Bank, and
  • Positively identify the customer/guarantor, and
  • Show the only adverse listings are to utility services (e.g. Optus, Telstra), and
    • there are no more than two defaults with a combined total of $500 or less, and
    • the credit bureau report confirms the default has been paid in full, or a formal letter from the creditor or credit bureau is held confirming all outstanding monies are paid in full.

Credit bureau reports not meeting the above criteria will be considered on a case-by-case basis, having regard to the customer’s overall risk profile.

Validation of Credit Bureau Reports

The assessing officer will investigate and comment on:

  • all enquiries posted within the last three months,
  • credit facilities with unsatisfactory conduct,
  • liabilities not included in the application,
  • liability limits exceeded the limits disclosed in the application,
  • all current directorships/ guarantors,
  • possible matches,
  • any judgements, insolvencies or bankruptcies and,
  • any new reports or listings generated for the customer where it is obvious a credit file should exist.
  • Any Financial Hardship indicators (FHI)

Where a consumer report makes reference to a commercial file these additional reports are to be reviewed.

If the customer has proactively declared adverse credit history, or you have independently obtained a credit bureau report that shows adverse credit history, you should include details of the circumstances surrounding the event in the notes supporting the loan application.
