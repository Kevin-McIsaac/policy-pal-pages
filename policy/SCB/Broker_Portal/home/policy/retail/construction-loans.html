Construction Loans

Why

Construction loans enable the funding of a dwelling that is yet to be constructed.

What

Construction loans pose a greater credit risk, which increases the documentary requirements and processes involved in obtaining a loan approval, so ensure you allow enough time to meet contractual obligations.

The documents you will need to support a construction loan application is set out in the Submission Checklist and Definition Guide.

This guidance applies to home loan funding for construction of a residential property, for non-speculative development of:

  • One single residence or duplex on a normal residential block for the location, or
  • Splitting of a normal residential block into two lots and the end position does not exceed two residences or a single duplex.

All other proposals are to be referred to Business Banking.

Retail construction finance is not available where:

  • The Loan to Valuation Ratio (LVR) is based solely on the current unimproved value of the vacant land,
  • Funding relates to minor works and there is no reliance placed on satisfactory completion of the proposed works to increase security value,
  • Construction is subject to Split Contracts.

Construction finance will only be provided where an independent and appropriately licensed builder is contracted on a genuine arms-length basis to complete all aspects of the construction/renovation works.

Construction build period

Restrictions apply to construction lending where the construction period is greater than 12 months or 300 working days. 

If the LVR is:

  • ≤80% the loan application must be referred to Credit DCA holder
  • >80% and LMI applies the loan application is not to proceed

Common Questions

Maximum LVR

Refer to Maximum LVR by Loan Purpose & Loan Type.

FHOG proceeds are not generally available until after construction has commenced. They will not be available to assist with either purchase of vacant land or the building contract deposit.

Progress Payments will only be made:

  • After the customer has fully utilised their cash equity contribution,
  • After the corresponding work has been completed, and
  • At the written request of the customer, and
  • On production of the builder’s invoice, and
  • After required valuations have been performed.
Term
Definition
Arms-Length
The builder and their customer have no prior relationship except as parties to this construction contract.
Minor Works
Bathroom and kitchen renovations, landscaping, construction of swimming pools, carport and garages, etc.

The additional risks associated with owner-builder and partial works construction projects will vary but some of the more common problems relate directly to an inexperienced owner- builder's inability to:

  • Prepare realistic expense budgets,
  • Obtain trade discounts and/or favourable payment terms from suppliers,
  • Manage project cash flows over extended periods,
  • Successfully identify and engage suitable trade contractors on reasonable terms,
  • Successfully manage/coordinate multiple suppliers and contractors,
  • Avoid the temptation to constantly vary or materially change the construction plans,
  • Achieve a consistent quality finish that is comparable with professional standards, and
  • Fully complete the project within budget and within a reasonable timeframe.

Often, owner-builders have insufficient cash or other resources to manage or mitigate risks.

Loan applications of this kind need to be supported by strong risk mitigants that must be clearly recorded in your loan submission notes.

Requirements

Customers are to:

  • Demonstrate capacity to repay without any reliance on rental income derived from the property,
  • Provide a significant cash/equity contribution (maximum Loan to Valuation Ratio of 70.0%),
  • Have a proven repayment history on all prior borrowings (refer to Credit Repayment History),
  • Have a satisfactory credit bureau report (refer to Credit Repayment History),
  • Be a registered builder (in the State of the proposed construction),
  • Have successfully completed at least one owner-builder dwelling, and
  • Provide detailed quotes, building specifications and Council approved building plans prior to formal approval.

You need to be satisfied that the customer has the necessary knowledge, experience and skills to manage the project and the related cash flows. This will include:

  • The time the customer needs to commit to the construction project and the impact this will have on their traditional income streams, and
  • How the customer will fund cash flow requirements between progress draws.

Mandatory Conditions

Funds will only be released following satisfactory progress inspection/s by a valuer selected by Suncorp Bank and then on a 'cost to complete' basis. Payment on production of contractor/supplier invoice is not available. Funds will only be provided for completed works and then subject to the Bank retaining sufficient funds to complete the project.

To mitigate risk, funds are to be released on a staged (progress payment) basis to ensure:

  • Drawn funds match the stage reached in construction, and
  • Loan balance is supported by the increasing security value, and
  • Sufficient undrawn loan funds are held to enable construction to be finished.

Draw Down Schedule

The draw down schedule for construction loans will vary according to the style of property, construction methods, State requirements and negotiations between the owner and builder. There are no standard requirements.

Generally, a building contract will incorporate a progress claim schedule (similar to the one below) setting out the payments required for the construction. You should review the payment schedule, investigate any large variances and make appropriate comments in your loan submission notes.
