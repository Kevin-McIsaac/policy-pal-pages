PAYG Work Related Allowances

Work related allowances must be a permanent and ongoing part of the customer's income. Examples include motor vehicle, living away from home and shift allowances or penalty rates.

You may still choose to verify Work Related Allowances over 3 months and use 80% of the income for servicing.

Please refer to Foreign currency income - Overtime for the relevant verification requirements.

 

Restrictions Verifications % Used for Servicing
Full time, Permanent Part-Time and Contracted Employees

Verify income through the following:

Employment Contract or Letter from Employer and all of the following:

  • Three months salary credits from a Commonwealth Bank or Other Financial Institution (OFI) account; and
  • One acceptable payslip showing base salary/wage and=>three months YTD income; and
  • Most recent PAYG Payment Summary/’Tax ready’ Income Statement or Tax Return (or equivalent).


Note:

  • The Letter from Employer or payslip must include the current base salary/wage and the YTD income information.
  • In addition to the above, where the customer resides in a different country to the account where their salary is being paid into, also obtain the most recent three months bank statement/s from the country of residence. Review the statement/s to confirm the source of funds used to cover the customer’s living expenses is from their foreign currency income. Ensure the account conduct is satisfactory and there are no undisclosed commitments during the review period.
  • If the bank statements does not show transfers from the customer’s overseas account or the main source of funds use to cover their living expenses is not from their foreign currency income, leave comments with an explanation for Credit Decisioning to consider.


Bank statements must confirm all of the following:

  • Amount
  • Account name
  • Account number
  • Description of the credit, e.g. salary, wage, pay or employer's name
  • Date range
  • Financial Institution details


Note:

  • Bank statements must be from the financial institution where the salary is credited and not a third party source.
  • An old bank statement can be provided to verify ownership of account.

80% used for Gold and Silver currencies


70% used for all other currencies

 

 

 

Calculations and Examples

Step Action Example

1

Review the bank account statements to confirm regular salary credits from their employer

 

 

2

Annualise the YTD amount stated on the letter or payslip

Note: Where the YTD amount on the letter or payslip contains bonus / commission payments, these amounts are to be deducted from the YTD amount prior to annualising the income.

YTD income stated on letter annualises to USD $55,000 p.a.

This amount includes a base salary/wage of $40,000 p.a. which is stated on the letter.

3

Compare the Annual Gross Salary obtained in step 2 to the annual income on the PAYG payment summary/’Tax ready’ Income Statement or tax return to identify the lower amount.

YTD annualised amount USD $55,000

Tax Return USD $53,250 p.a.
4

Deduct the applicable percentage from the lower income amount as calculated in Step 3.

USD $53,250 p.a. less 20% = USD $42,600

5

Convert the remaining foreign currency income to Australian dollars using the XE Currency Converter

Note the XE Currency Converter must be opened in Internet Explorer

Lower amount (tax return)USD $42,600 p.a. @ .7500 = AUD $56,800pa
6 Print a copy of the foreign currency calculation showing the foreign currency amount, exchange rate and date. This must be submitted with your supporting documents
  

7

Enter converted amount from step 5 as:

  • Base salary/wage

 

 

 

Other/Special Notes:

  • The currency stated on the employment contract will determine the currency that is used for converting to Australian dollars. Where the currency on the employment contract is different from the payslip and/or salary credits ensure the converted amount from the payslip is in line with the amount stated on the employment contract.
