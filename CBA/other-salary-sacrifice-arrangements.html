Salary Sacrifice Arrangements (General):

Salary sacrifice is an arrangement between an employer and an employee, whereby the employee agrees to forgo part of their  salary or wages in return for the employer providing them with benefits of a similar value.

A salary sacrifice arrangement is also commonly referred to as salary packaging or total remuneration packaging.

 

Novated Lease
For novated leases, refer to the Salary Sacrifice (Novated Leases) page. 

FBT ‘Tax Free’ Salary Sacrifice:
For customers employed by a FBT exempt employer who want their pre-tax salary sacrifice amount to be treated as ‘tax-free’, refer to the Salary Sacrifice (FBT Exempt Employers) page.

Salary sacrifice arrangements must be evidenced using one of the following options:

  • a payslip or
  • a letter/statement from the employer or salary sacrfice provider outlining the details of the salary sacrifice arrangement.

Where there are multiple salary sacrifice deductions, and they are not itemised on the payslip, additional documents (e.g. a letter/statement from the salary sacrifice provider outlining the details of the salary sacrifice arrangement) must be provided

Calculations
  1. Determine what type of salary sacrifice items are being paid from the customer’s salary;
    • Liabilities (e.g. home loan repayment, other loan repayment)
    • Living expenses (e.g. gym membership, childcare fees, transport)
    • Other (eg voluntary or compulsory superannuation, purchase leave, charitable donation)
  2. Is the customer able to stop the deduction as part of their salary sacrifice arrangement (e.g., alternate payment arrangements can be made instead of being deducted as a salary sacrifice)?
    • Yes; The customer is able to stop the deduction as a salary sacrifice:
      • Use the gross income before the salary sacrifice deduction and
      • If the customer is making alternate arrangements to pay the deduction, it must still be accounted for in the living expenses or liability. 
      • If the customer is able to cease making the deduction completely with no alternate payment arrangement, do not include as a commitment or living expenses or as a liability. E.g.: a donation to a charitable organisation that the customer advises they will no longer be making.
      • End process.
      • Note: There is no requirement to obtain evidence the customer is able to, or has, ceased the deduction.
         
    • No; The customer is unable to stop the deduction as a salary sacrifice: Proceed to Step 3.
       
  3. Based on the deduction type:
    • it’s a liability: 
    • Use the gross income before the salary sacrifice deduction, and 
    • Add the liability with the repayment amount equalling the deduction amount (both pre and post-tax deductions, where applicable)
    • End of Process.
    • it’s a living expense:
      • Use the gross income before the salary sacrifice deduction, and 
      • Add the figure as a living expense in the relevant living expenses category.
      • End of Process
    • it is ‘other’: 
      • Use the gross income before the salary sacrifice deduction, and 
      • Add to financial position as “other” or as a living expense in the relevant living expenses category.
      • End of Process
 
 
Note: If the Superannuation Guarantee Contribution is listed under pre-tax deductions on the payslip, this does not need to be included as a commitment
 

Other/Special Notes:

  • Comments explaining the customer's salary sacrifice arrangements should be included in supporting commentary.
  • Ensure you obtain the necessary verification documents of liabilities (where required) in addition to confirming the payment amount via the payslip or letter from the employer.
