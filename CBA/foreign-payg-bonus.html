Foreign PAYG Bonus

Restrictions Verification % Used for Servicing
Bonus payments must be a permanent part of income and been paid consistently for at least 2 financial years.

Use the following options.

Bank statement showing bonus payment/s credited into an account and:

  1. Letter/s OR bonus statement/s from employer detailing the bonus amount/s received over the two financial years or
  2. Payslips showing the bonus amount/s received over the past two financial years


Note:

  • The letter from employer or payslips used to verify bonus payments may be dated more than 6 weeks
  • In addition to the above, where the customer resides in a different country to the account where their foreign income salary is being paid into, also obtain the most recent three months bank statement/s from the country of residence. Review the statement/s to confirm the source of funds used to cover the customer’s living expenses is from their foreign currency income. Ensure the account conduct is satisfactory and there are no undisclosed commitments during the review period.
  • If the bank statements does not show transfers from the customer’s overseas account or the main source of funds use to cover their living expenses is not from their foreign currency income, leave comments with an explanation for Credit Decisioning to consider.


Bank statements must confirm all of the following:

  • Amount
  • Account name
  • Account number
  • Description of the credit, e.g. salary, wage, pay or employer's name
  • Date range
  • Financial institution details.


Note:

  • Bank statements must be from the financial institution where the salary is credited and not a third party source.
  • An old bank statement can be provided to verify ownership of account.
 80% used for Gold and Silver currencies
70% used for all other currencies

 

Calculations

Step Action
1 Deduct 20% or the applicable percentage from the average of the two year’s bonus amounts for servicing.
2

Convert the remaining foreign currency income to Australian dollars using the XE Currency Converter.

Note the XE Currency Converter must be opened in Internet Explorer
3 Print a copy of the foreign currency calculation showing the foreign currency amount, exchange rate and date. This must be submitted with your supporting documents
4 Input the calculated amount into the ‘Regular Bonus’ field of the application

 

Other.Special Notes:

  • The currency stated on the employment contract will determine the currency that is used for converting to Australian dollars. Where the currency on the employment contract is different from the payslip and/or salary credits ensure the converted amount from the payslip is in line with the amount stated on the employment contract.
